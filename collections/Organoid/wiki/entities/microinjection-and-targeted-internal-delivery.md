# Microinjection and targeted internal delivery

## Current role in this corpus

Microinjection appears in this collection as the internal-delivery branch used when a 3D organoid should remain closed and structured, but a defined cargo must still reach an internal lumen or compartment.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, microinjection is not mainly about opening access; it is about preserving 3D architecture while placing a microbial, viral, tracer, or reporter payload in a specific internal space.
- Its main advantage is spatial precision. Its main cost is throughput, operator dependence, and injection-site bias.
- It therefore occupies a different design slot from polarity inversion or organ-on-chip: use it when the structure should stay closed, but delivery must become targeted and internal rather than surface-wide.

## Main tension

- precise internal targeting versus low throughput and high operator burden
- preserved 3D architecture versus limited scalability for larger screens
