# Stand-alone vascular organoids

## Current role in this corpus

Stand-alone vascular organoids appear here as the cleanest vessel-first baseline: the vascular system itself is the model rather than a support layer added to another host organoid.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, stand-alone vascular organoids are the route to choose when the vascular branch itself must be defined, benchmarked, or reused as a modular baseline.
- Wimmer 2019 anchors the self-organization-first version of this route, while L 2026 adds a faster, more programmable orthogonal-activation version.
- This entity therefore sits before organ-specific vascular rescue or host-integration claims: first define what the vessel module can do on its own, then decide whether it should be inserted into brain, kidney, tumor, or repair workflows.

## Main tension

- clear vessel-only definition versus limited organ-specific context
- reusable modular baseline versus incomplete tissue coupling

- Which claims truly require a stand-alone vascular baseline before integration into another organoid system?
