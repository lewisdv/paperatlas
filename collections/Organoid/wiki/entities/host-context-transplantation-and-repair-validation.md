# Host-context transplantation and repair validation

## Current role in this corpus

Host-context transplantation and repair validation appears here as the branch for questions that stop being fully answerable inside the dish because the endpoint itself depends on host injury, circulation, circuit participation, or metastatic context.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, host-context transplantation is not a generic maturity upgrade. It is the move to use when the biological claim itself depends on repair, engraftment, host circuitry, circulation, or host-restored disease behavior.
- Watanabe 2022 anchors the repair branch, Kelley 2024 anchors host neural participation, and the cancer PDO-X papers show that some tumor phenotypes only become interpretable once stromal or metastatic host context re-enters the system.
- This entity therefore acts as the cross-domain umbrella above more specific branches like host circuit engagement or PDO-X: first decide whether the endpoint is host-defined at all, then choose the relevant transplantation subtype.

## Main tension

- stronger host-defined validation versus loss of clean organoid-intrinsic attribution
- richer in vivo context versus lower throughput and higher operational burden

- Which endpoints truly require host-context transplantation instead of better coculture or support-compartment engineering?
- How should host-driven rescue or phenotype restoration be separated from organoid-intrinsic biology?
- When is host-context validation a decisive last step, and when does it become a premature escape from unresolved in vitro assay design?
