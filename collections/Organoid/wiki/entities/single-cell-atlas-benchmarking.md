# Single-cell atlas benchmarking

## Current role in this corpus

Single-cell atlas benchmarking appears here as the main transcriptomic comparison layer for deciding whether a brain-organoid protocol is mature, regionally appropriate, reproducible, or drifting into stress-biased states.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, atlas benchmarking is not just a descriptive add-on. It is the main way brain protocols are compared on developmental timing, regional coverage, fidelity to primary tissue, and stress-linked divergence.
- Kanton 2019 anchors the temporal and cross-species trajectory view, He 2024 expands that into a cross-protocol comparison framework, and Bhaduri 2020 shows why reproducibility alone is not enough when organoids diverge from primary cortex.
- This entity therefore acts as a bridge between baseline brain protocol choice and stronger functional validation: atlas scores can sharpen protocol selection, but they do not replace physiology or transplantation.

## Main tension

- large-scale transcriptomic comparability versus incomplete capture of function
- protocol ranking power versus dependence on chosen reference datasets and comparison axes
