# Patient-derived organoid xenografts (PDO-X)

## Current role in this corpus

PDO-X is the branch where a donor-preserving organoid platform is carried into host context rather than being treated as a dish-only endpoint.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, PDO-X is not a default baseline. It is an escalation move used after a stable donor-derived organoid has already shown enough signal to justify host work.
- The main payoff is host-only readout space: metastasis, stromal remodeling, histological fidelity, kinome restoration, or other phenotypes that do not resolve from organoid growth alone.
- PDO-X therefore sits between `patient-derived platform` logic and `host validation` logic. It preserves donor biology better than generic xenograft branches while still reintroducing in vivo tissue context.

## Main tension

- donor-preserving translational relevance versus cost, throughput loss, and host-derived interpretive noise
- in vitro triage efficiency versus the extra realism of metastatic or stromal endpoints
