# CRISPR-Cas9 and next-generation CRISPR editing

## Current role in this corpus

CRISPR in this collection is not a single workflow. It spans classic CRISPR-Cas9 manipulation, pooled screening, and DSB-free editing branches such as base editing and prime editing.

## Working synthesis

- The adult-platform branch uses CRISPR to turn expandable organoids into isogenic comparator systems rather than only donor-derived cultures.
- The brain branch uses pooled CRISPR to turn organoids or assembloids into screening systems, which means baseline reproducibility becomes a prerequisite rather than a luxury.
- The newer editing papers also split editor choice into a design decision: CRISPR-Cas9 for knockout-style engineering, and base or prime editing when the project is really about precise mutation modeling.

## Main tension

- causal leverage versus technical overhead in delivery, selection, and validation
- broad perturbation scale versus the need for a highly stable baseline organoid system
- DSB-free precision versus cloning and targeting constraints
