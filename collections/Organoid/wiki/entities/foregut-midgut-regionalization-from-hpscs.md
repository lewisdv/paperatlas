# Foregut-midgut regionalization from hPSCs

## Current role in this corpus

Foregut-midgut regionalization from hPSCs appears here as the developmental route for endodermal organoids where the main design question is not donor capture, but how to push pluripotent cells into distinct gut, gastric, or boundary-connected identities.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, this route is the main developmental branch for hPSC-derived endoderm when users need regional identity rather than a generic epithelial baseline.
- McCracken 2011 anchors the mid-hindgut intestinal baseline, Broda 2018 sharpens that logic into antral versus fundic gastric divergence, and Koike 2021 pushes the same regionalization logic into a boundary-connected hepato-biliary-pancreatic system.
- This entity therefore sits upstream of later access-control or donor-grounded decisions: first decide whether the unresolved problem is regional developmental identity across foregut or midgut branches.

## Main tension

- developmental regional specification versus simpler but coarser epithelial baselines
- clearer gut-axis identity versus longer, more brittle patterning workflows
