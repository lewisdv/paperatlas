# Distal lung organoids

## Current role in this corpus

Distal lung organoids appear here as the respiratory baseline branch for developmental, interstitial-disease, and viral-infection questions where lung-specific epithelial complexity must be named before downstream assay design.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, lung organoid work is organized less by access or perfusion than by whether the baseline lung identity is rich enough for the question.
- Miller 2019 anchors the foundational respiratory derivation route, while Matkovic Leko 2023 makes the branch more explicit by framing distal lung organoids as shared baselines for development, ILD-like biology, and infection-facing questions.
- This entity therefore marks the collection's main lung rule: choose the right distal-lung baseline first, then decide which disease or readout layer deserves to be added.

## Main tension

- baseline respiratory complexity versus throughput and simplicity
- developmental breadth versus question-specific assay readiness
