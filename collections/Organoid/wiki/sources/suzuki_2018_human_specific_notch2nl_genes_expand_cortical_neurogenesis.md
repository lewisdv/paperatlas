## Abstract

The cerebral cortex underwent rapid expansion and increased complexity during recent hominid evolution. Gene duplications constitute a major evolutionary force, but their impact on human brain development remains unclear. Using tailored RNA sequencing (RNA-seq), we profiled the spatial and temporal expression of hominid-specific duplicated (HS) genes in the human fetal cortex and identified a repertoire of 35 HS genes displaying robust and dynamic patterns during cortical neurogenesis. Among them NOTCH2NL, human-specific paralogs of the NOTCH2 receptor, stood out for their ability to promote cortical progenitor maintenance. NOTCH2NL promote the clonal expansion of human cortical progenitors, ultimately leading to higher neuronal output. At the molecular level, NOTCH2NL function by activating the Notch pathway through inhibition of cis Delta/Notch interactions. Our study uncovers a large repertoire of recently evolved genes active during human corticogenesis and reveals how human-specific NOTCH paralogs may have contributed to the expansion of the human cortex.

## Key findings

- **A repertoire of ~35 hominid-specific (HS) protein-coding genes expressed during human corticogenesis.** Tailored RNA-seq (350–700 bp fragment selection, 2×151 bp paired-end, custom pipeline correcting paralog mis-mapping) of human fetal cortex from **GW 7–21**, with microdissection of cortical plate (CP) vs non-CP (oSVZ/VZ germinal zones) at later stages. Selection threshold = **5 FPKM** (matched to 60 marker genes). Result: **68 genes in 24 families** with robust dynamic expression; **51 HS genes have a conserved-but-distinct ORF (18 ancestors + 35 hominid/human-unique)**; 17 lack a reliable ORF (likely pseudogenes/ncRNA). ISH validated tissue/cellular specificity for 19/21 families tested. The screen recovered SRGAP2 but not ARHGAP11B (below threshold) or TBC1D3 (unreliable annotation at the time).
- **NOTCH2NL = NOTCH2 paralogs, human-specific, in 1q21.1.** Four NOTCH2 paralogs in the human genome on two chromosome-1 loci: **NOTCH2NLA, B, C** (in 1q21.1-2, near a microcephaly/macrocephaly CNV locus) and **NOTCH2NLR**. Each is a segmental duplication of NOTCH2 exons/introns 1–4 plus a 5th exon from a NOTCH2 intron; each encodes the **first six EGF repeats of NOTCH2**, a unique C-terminus, and (for two paralogs) a predicted signal peptide. **3 of 4 paralogs detected only in human; 1 also in chimpanzee** → very recent hominid emergence. Expression (RNA-seq): NOTCH2 high throughout (peak GW9); **NOTCH2NLB > NOTCH2NLA >> C ≈ R**, with B and A rising at later stages including GW21 non-CP (oSVZ). ISH: NOTCH2 mostly apical VZ; NOTCH2NL in a "salt-and-pepper" pattern through the VZ (GW9/GW12) and throughout the oSVZ (GW21, oRG-containing).
- **An in vivo mouse screen singled out NOTCH2NLB.** In utero electroporation of HS paralogs (CROCC, GOLGA6/8, LRRC37A/B, NBPF, NOTCH2NL, NPIP, PDE4DIP, PMS2, WASH families) into embryonic mouse cortex; **NOTCH2NLB stood out for retaining cells in the VZ in the progenitor (PAX6+) state** (E13.5 and E15.5, analyzed 3 days later).
- **NOTCH2NLB clonally expands human cortical progenitors and raises neuronal output.** Lentiviral clonal analysis in human ESC-derived cortical cells (Espuny-Camacho/Vanderhaeghen system that recapitulates human-specific neurogenic tempo); low-titer NOTCH2NLB-IRES-EGFP vs mCherry control, infected at day 30. Over 20 days: **control clones ~doubled** in size while SOX2+ progenitors/clone declined; **NOTCH2NLB clones almost tripled**, the SOX2+ progenitor proportion kept rising, and **neuronal output per clone was larger** than control. → larger clones, slower progenitor exhaustion, more neurons.
- **Mechanism at the cell level: increased cell-cycle re-entry, not faster cycling.** 7-day overexpression increased progenitor fate (PAX6) at the expense of neuronal fate (βIII-tubulin). Short-term (1 hr) S-G2 labeling and pHH3 (M phase) showed **no change in cell-cycle progression/rate**; cumulative (24 hr) labeling + Ki67 showed **decreased cell-cycle exit / increased cell-cycle re-entry**. → NOTCH2NLB drives self-renewal / symmetric proliferative divisions, not faster division. The **EGF repeats are required; the C-terminal domain is dispensable** (deletion-mutant series). In vivo the effect specifically targeted **PAX6+ apical RG in the VZ**, not basal/oRG-like PAX6+ cells or TBR2+ basal progenitors (i.e., distinct from ARHGAP11B/TBC1D3, which expand basal progenitors).
- **NOTCH2NLB activates Notch signaling.** It phenocopies Notch1-ICD (NICD): more progenitors, fewer neurons; both upregulate the direct Notch effector **Hes1**, EGF-repeat-dependent. In vivo, a Notch transcriptional reporter (CBFRE-EGFP) confirmed NOTCH2NLB increases Notch activity to an amplitude **comparable to NICD**, abolished by deleting the EGF repeats.
- **Molecular mechanism: cell-autonomous cis-inhibition of Delta/Notch.** NOTCH2NLB co-IPs with the Notch ligand **DLL1**. In a CHO assay measuring functional DLL1 at the plasma membrane (soluble NOTCH1-Fc binding), NOTCH2NLB **reduced surface-available DLL1** (lower NOTCH1-Fc/DLL1 ratio), whereas the ΔEGF mutant and NICD did not. In vivo, DLL1 overexpression (which normally depletes VZ progenitors and adds CP neurons) was **completely blocked by co-expressed NOTCH2NLB-FL**, but not by ΔEGF. → NOTCH2NLB cell-autonomously suppresses DLL1 function (cis-inhibition), raising Notch tone and expanding progenitors.
- **Explicit convergence with the Fiddes companion paper.** The Discussion notes NOTCH2NLA/B/C sit in the 1q21.1 brain-size CNV interval and cites Fiddes et al. (2018) for precise breakpoint mapping (deletion→microcephaly, duplication→macrocephaly) and for evidence that NOTCH2NL can **also activate Notch receptors in trans** — so non-autonomous (receptor activation) and autonomous (ligand cis-inhibition) effects converge to increase Notch signaling.

## Methods

- **Tailored fetal-cortex RNA-seq:** deep sequencing of human fetal cortex GW7–21 with regional microdissection (frontal→occipital; parietal split into CP vs non-CP); fragment-size selection + 2×151 bp paired-end + manual paralog gene-structure curation + custom mapping-error correction to resolve highly similar HS paralogs; FPKM≥5 threshold.
- **ISH** in human fetal cortex (GW9/12/21) with paralog- and NOTCH2-specific probes.
- **In vivo gain-of-function:** in utero electroporation in embryonic mouse cortex (E13.5/E15.5, analysis +2–3 days) for the HS-paralog screen, NOTCH2NLB fate/PAX6 quantification, Notch reporter (CBFRE-EGFP), and DLL1 epistasis; deletion-mutant series (full-length vs ΔEGF vs ΔC-term) and NICD positive control.
- **Human clonal analysis:** hESC differentiated to cortical cells 30 days, then low-titer lentivirus (NOTCH2NLB-IRES-EGFP vs mCherry); clone size, SOX2+ progenitor and βIII-tubulin+ neuron counts over time.
- **Cell-cycle:** short-term (1 hr) and cumulative (24 hr) DNA labeling + Ki67/pHH3 to separate cell-cycle exit/re-entry from cycling rate.
- **Molecular interaction:** co-IP of NOTCH2NLB + DLL1 (HEK293T); CHO plasma-membrane DLL1 assay (NOTCH1-Fc binding) to quantify functional surface ligand; Hes1 readout.
- **Stats:** Student's t test; one-way ANOVA + Bonferroni. Note: functional work uses human ESC 2D/clonal cortical cultures and in utero electroporation — this paper does not use 3D cerebral organoids (contrast with the Fiddes companion, which uses cortical organoids).

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Core support for the review's human-specific-gene / cortical-expansion theme.** Provides the functional counterpart to Fiddes: NOTCH2NL (a recently evolved, human-specific NOTCH2 paralog enriched in RG/oRG) **expands human cortical progenitors and increases neuronal output**, the developmental logic proposed for human neocortical expansion. Gain-of-function in *human* cortical progenitors (clonal tripling, slower progenitor exhaustion) is the human-cell evidence the manuscript leans on.
- **Mechanistic specificity for the review.** Defines *how* a human-specific gene tunes neurogenesis: cell-autonomous **cis-inhibition of the Delta/Notch ligand DLL1** (plus trans receptor activation per Fiddes), boosting Notch tone and apical-RG self-renewal via Hes1 — increasing cell-cycle re-entry rather than cycling speed. Contrasts NOTCH2NL (self-renewal of apical RG) with ARHGAP11B/TBC1D3 (basal-progenitor expansion).
- **Ties to ASD genetics via 1q21.1.** NOTCH2NLA/B/C lie in the 1q21.1 CNV interval; the paper's progenitor-expansion phenotype provides the dosage-sensitive mechanism predicting duplication→macrocephaly (autism-associated) and deletion→microcephaly, converging with Fiddes' patient breakpoint mapping — directly relevant to the manuscript's proactive-autism-genetics framing.
- **oRG link (suggestive, not directly tested).** NOTCH2NLA/B are highly expressed in oRG and Notch is required for oRG self-renewal, so the authors argue NOTCH2NL could support oRG expansion — connecting to the review's oRG theme — but they explicitly did not assay oRG and saw no oRG/basal-progenitor increase on mouse overexpression.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Human functional work is in 2D/clonal hESC cortical cultures + mouse in utero electroporation, not 3D human organoids.** The strongest organoid evidence (and the explicit deletion-organoid loss-of-function) is in the Fiddes companion; oRG-specific effects are inferred, not measured here.
- **Only NOTCH2NLB was studied functionally in depth** (highest expression); paralog- and allele-specific (paratype) differences emphasized by Fiddes are not dissected.
- **Cis- vs trans- mechanism not fully resolved.** The authors show cell-autonomous DLL1 cis-inhibition but cannot say whether NOTCH2NLB acts on DLL1 trafficking vs at the membrane, nor fully separate cis-inhibition-blockade from trans receptor activation; they defer to convergence with Fiddes (trans activation).
- **Most HS genes in the screen are uncharacterized** — a candidate resource (35 genes) whose individual roles, functionality, or pseudogenization status remain open.
- Causal contribution of NOTCH2NL dosage to 1q21.1 brain-size phenotypes is argued by convergence, not proven in patient-derived cells in this paper.
