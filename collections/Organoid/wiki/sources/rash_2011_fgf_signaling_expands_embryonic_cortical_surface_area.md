# FGF signaling expands embryonic cortical surface area by regulating Notch-dependent neurogenesis.

## Source

- Authors: Rash BG, Lim HD, Breunig JJ, Vaccarino FM
- Journal: The Journal of neuroscience : the official journal of the Society for Neuroscience (2011)
- DOI: [10.1523/jneurosci.4439-11.2011](https://doi.org/10.1523/jneurosci.4439-11.2011)
- PMID: [22031906](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22031906/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

The processes regulating cortical surface area expansion during development and evolution are unknown. We show that loss of function of all fibroblast growth factor receptors (FgfRs) expressed at the earliest stages of cortical development causes severe deficits in surface area growth by embryonic day 12.5 (E12.5) in the mouse. In FgfR mutants, accelerated production of neurons led to severe loss of radial progenitors and premature termination of neurogenesis. Nevertheless, these mutants showed remarkably little change in cortical layer structure. Birth-dating experiments indicated that a greater proportion of layer fates was generated during early neurogenic stages, revealing that FgfR activity normally slows the temporal progression of cortical layer fates. Electroporation of a dominant-negative FgfR at E11.5 increased cortical neurogenesis in normal mice--an effect that was blocked by simultaneous activation of the Notch pathway. Together with changes in the expression of Notch pathway genes in FgfR mutant embryos, these findings indicate that Notch lies downstream of FgfR signaling in the same pathway regulating cortical neurogenesis and begin to establish a mechanism for regulating cortical surface expansion.

## Key findings

- **Core claim: FGF receptor signaling expands embryonic cortical surface area by tempering the rate of early neurogenesis, acting through Notch.** Conditional triple knockout of all CNS-expressed FgfRs in the dorsal telencephalon (mouse) drives radial-glia progenitors to differentiate prematurely instead of self-renewing, collapsing surface-area growth — and Notch is placed genetically downstream of FGF in the same pathway. This is the mechanistic "FGF/Notch neurogenic-window" citation for the review.
- **Model: `Emx1-Cre; FgfR1^f/f; FgfR2^f/f; FgfR3^-/-` triple knockout (Emx1;TKO).** Emx1Cre recombines in dorsal telencephalon by ~E10.0; FgfR4 is not expressed in brain, so this is a near-complete loss of FgfR signaling in the cortical (Emx1) lineage. 16 Emx1;TKO embryos recovered at ~Mendelian 1/16 ratio (no embryonic lethality). FGF pathway loss confirmed functionally: near-complete loss of the Ets target *Pea3* at E11.5 and strong upregulation of FGF-repressed *CoupTF1*.
- **Severe, early-onset surface-area deficit.** Cortices looked normal at E11.5 but by **E12.5 showed a 50.2% reduction in surface area** (one day later); by **E18.5 a 52% reduction in dorsal-telencephalon volume and 46% reduction in surface area**. Notably, growth *rates* after E12.5 were similar to controls (cortical volume rose 13.7-fold E12.5→E18.5 in WT vs 11.2-fold in TKO), pinpointing the **E11.5–E12.5 window** as the FGF-dependent expansion phase. Ventral telencephalon was essentially unaffected; deficits were equally severe rostrally and caudally.
- **Mechanism is precocious neurogenesis, not altered proliferation or death.** Mitotic index (PH3+) and BrdU incorporation were only modestly different; activated-Caspase-3+ cells were rare (~952 per hemisphere in TKO vs ~624 in WT) — too few to explain the volume loss. Instead, radial glia switched from self-renewal to neuron production.
- **Early excess then late depletion of neurons and progenitors.** TKO showed increased density of Tbr1+ neurons at E11.5–E13.5 (with a 4.4-fold rise in ectopic Tbr1+ cells inside the VZ) and increased Tbr2+ intermediate progenitors at E12.5–E13.5 (~2-fold rise in VZ). This early overproduction reversed into a deficit: **Tbr1+ neurons −51.7% and Tbr2+ INPs −84.2% by E18.5**; Pax6+ radial progenitors fell ≥50% from E12.5 and ~79% by E18.5; VZ surface area dropped −50.6% (E12.5) to −65.3% (E18.5). *Ngn2* rose early then was severely curtailed by E18.5 — a premature end to the neurogenic period.
- **Decreased progenitor self-renewal shown by cell-cycle re-entry.** Fraction of BrdU+ cells re-entering the cycle (BrdU+/Ki67+) fell from 81.9%±5.6 (control) to 56.4%±2.3 (TKO) at E11.5, and from 49.5%±5.37 to 33.6%±2.5 at E12.5–E13.5; Tbr1+/BrdU+ birth-dated neuron production rose up to ~50% at E13.5.
- **Layer structure is preserved despite the size collapse — but layer *timing* shifts earlier.** The Cux1(layer 2/3)-to-ZFPM2(layer 6/SP) ratio was unchanged (p = 0.92) and inside-out order was retained; only a modest Ctip2 (layer 5) increase appeared. Dual birth-dating (CldU E12.5 / IdU E16.5, examined E18.5) showed a **~5-fold increase in upper-layer Cux1+ neurons born at E12.5** and near-disappearance of E16.5-born (IdU+) neurons → FGF normally *slows the temporal progression of layer fates*, so loss compresses the schedule. (Authors note cortical surface varies ~1000-fold across mammals while thickness/layering is far more conserved.)
- **Cell-autonomous role confirmed by clonal analysis.** Radial glia electroporated at E11.5 with dominant-negative FgfR (SW2, BLBP promoter) and cultured 3 d in vitro produced more βIII-tubulin+ neurons, more neuron-only clones, fewer Nestin-only (radial-glia) clones (p = 0.003 / 0.005), and smaller clones — i.e. FGF loss cell-autonomously drives cycle exit and neuronal fate.
- **Notch is genetically downstream of FGF (epistasis).** TKO cortex showed decreased active Notch (NICD), decreased *Notch1* mRNA, decreased *Hes1*, increased *Ngn2/Dll1/Mash1*, and complete loss of BLBP (a direct Notch target) in radial glia — BLBP rescued by restoring one copy of FgfR3. In vivo electroporation: dominant-negative FgfR (SW2) cell-autonomously lowered NICD and Hes1 and increased Tbr1+ neurogenesis; constitutively active FgfR (FgfR3E) or active Notch (NICD) suppressed neurogenesis. Co-electroporating SW2 + NICD gave a phenotype indistinguishable from NICD alone — **Notch activation overrode FGF loss**, placing Notch downstream of FGF.
- **High dosage sensitivity / allelic series.** Restoring a single copy of either FgfR2 or FgfR3 substantially rescued the TKO surface-area phenotype, both rostrally and caudally (equivalently) — implying small changes in FGF tone can incrementally tune areal expansion (a proposed substrate for evolutionary cortical-size diversity).

## Methods

- **Genetics:** floxed FgfR1 and FgfR2 alleles + null FgfR3 + Emx1Cre (dorsal-telencephalon driver, active by E10.0). Littermates lacking Cre served as controls; a single Cre allele alone gave no phenotype. Allelic series compared FgfR1/2 double mutants, FgfR3 singles, full TKO, and one-copy-restored genotypes.
- **In utero electroporation (E11.5, CD1 mice):** BLBP-promoter constructs — pBLBP-eGFP (control), pBLBP-SW2 (truncated/dominant-negative FgfR1), pUbiC-NICD-Myc (active Notch), constitutively active FgfR3E/FgfR3M; 0.5 µl at 1 µg/µl, 27 V (BTX ECM 830). Densitometry of NICD/Hes1 in eGFP+ vs neighboring cells.
- **Clonal culture:** eGFP+ (±SW2) cortical cells dissociated, grown 3 d in DMEM/B27 + 5 ng/ml FGF2, scored blind for Nestin vs βIII-tubulin (n = 523 control / 731 SW2 cells; 133 control / 302 SW2 clones).
- **Birth-dating / cell-cycle:** BrdU pulses at E10.5/E11.5/E12.5 (24 h chase) with Tbr1 or Ki67 co-stain; dual CldU (E12.5) + IdU (E16.5) labeling examined at E18.5 for layer birth-dates.
- **Quantification:** unbiased stereology (optical fractionator, planimetry, Neurolucida contour tracing of cortical/VZ surface area) across E11.5–E18.5; in situ hybridization for Notch-pathway genes (*Notch1, Hes1, Ngn2, Dll1, Mash1, Pea3, CoupTF1*); factorial ANOVA / two-tailed t-test, p < 0.05.
- **System caveat:** this is an *in vivo mouse genetics* paper, not an organoid study — relevant to the review as the developmental mechanism that organoid FGF protocols and ASD CNV phenotypes must be interpreted against.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **The "FGF control of the cortical neurogenic window" citation.** This paper is the mechanistic anchor for the review's claim that FGF signaling sets *how long* progenitors self-renew before neurogenesis depletes them — i.e. FGF tunes the size of the founder progenitor pool and therefore cortical surface area, through Notch-dependent lateral inhibition. The actionable window is narrow and early (mouse E11.5–E12.5).
- **Mechanistic glue for the "neurogenic-window vulnerability" thesis.** It shows that *shifting the balance toward premature neurogenesis* depletes radial glia and prematurely ends neurogenesis while leaving lamination structurally intact but temporally compressed — a phenotype template that recurs in ASD organoid models where progenitor/neuron balance is perturbed (excess neurons + progenitor depletion in 16p11.2 deletions, [Urresti 2021](urresti_2021_cortical_organoids_model_early_brain_development_disrupted.md); transient progenitor-over-neuron enrichment in MeCP2 loss, [Tenreiro 2025](tenreiro_2025_mecp2_regulates_telencephalic_development_human_cerebral_organoids.md)).
- **Direct relevance to FGF/Notch axes in organoid protocols.** FGF2 and EGF are standard expansion factors in guided cortical-organoid/spheroid protocols (e.g. [Pasca 2015](paca_2015_functional_cortical_neurons_astrocytes_human_pluripotent_stem.md); FGF2 used in the [Urresti 2021](urresti_2021_cortical_organoids_model_early_brain_development_disrupted.md) protocol). This paper supplies the in vivo logic for why FGF dose and timing should determine progenitor-pool expansion vs premature differentiation in those cultures.
- **Notch-window context.** Connects to Notch-centered citations in the corpus: oscillatory Notch maintaining progenitors ([Shimojo 2008](shimojo_2008_oscillations_notch_signaling_regulate_maintenance_neural_progenitors.md)) and the human-specific NOTCH2NL paralogs that expand cortical neurogenesis by delaying differentiation ([Suzuki 2018](suzuki_2018_human_specific_notch2nl_genes_expand_cortical_neurogenesis.md), [Fiddes 2018](fiddes_2018_human_specific_notch2nl_genes_affect_notch_signaling.md)) — together framing Notch as the convergent node FGF acts through to set human cortical surface area.
- **Surface-area vs thickness framing for evolution/disease.** The dissociation of surface-area growth from layer thickness/identity (and the dosage-sensitive allelic rescue) gives the review a clean conceptual handle on areal expansion as a tunable, FGF/Notch-controlled parameter — relevant to macrocephaly/microcephaly phenotypes modeled in organoids.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Mouse, not human.** Conclusions are from mouse cortical genetics; human-specific oRG/OSVZ amplification (not prominent in mouse) and human FGF/Notch dosage effects must be tested separately ([Hansen 2010](hansen_2010_neurogenic_radial_glia_outer_subventricular_zone_human.md), [Benito-Kwiecinski 2021](benitokwiecinski_2021_early_cell_shape_transition_drives_evolutionary_expansion.md)).
- **Level of Notch regulation is not pinned down.** Authors cannot exclude that FGF acts above Notch transcription versus further downstream (Hes genes, CBF1/Rbpj) or via a CBF1-independent role in intermediate progenitors; the SW2 epistasis shows hierarchy, not the exact node.
- **Other pathways are not accounted for.** VZ surface area still grew somewhat E12.5–E13.5, so FGF loss does not fully halt self-renewal; Wnt ligands from the cortical hem and individual FGF ligands (Fgf2/8/10/15/17/18) with opposing/compensatory roles are invoked but not dissected here.
- **Layer "preservation" is at the population level.** Whether single-cell laminar identity, connectivity, and later function are truly normal in the smaller cortex is not assayed; only marker ratios and birth-dates are quantified.
- **Apoptosis modestly elevated.** Caspase-3+ counts trended up; ruled out as the *major* driver of volume loss but not zero.
