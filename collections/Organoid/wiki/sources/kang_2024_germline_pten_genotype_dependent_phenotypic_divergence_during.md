# Germline PTEN genotype-dependent phenotypic divergence during the early neural developmental process of forebrain organoids.

## Source

- Authors: Kang SC, Sarn NB, Venegas J, Tan Z, Hitomi M, Eng C
- Journal: Molecular psychiatry (2024)
- DOI: [10.1038/s41380-023-02325-3](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41380-023-02325-3)
- PMID: [38030818](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38030818/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

PTEN germline mutations account for ~0.2-1% of all autism spectrum disorder (ASD) cases, as well as ~17% of ASD patients with macrocephaly, making it one of the top ASD-associated risk genes. Individuals with germline PTEN mutations receive the molecular diagnosis of PTEN Hamartoma Tumor Syndrome (PHTS), an inherited cancer predisposition syndrome, about 20-23% of whom are diagnosed with ASD. We generated forebrain organoid cultures from gene-edited isogenic human induced pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs) harboring a PTENG132D (ASD) or PTENM134R (cancer) mutant allele to model how these mutations interrupt neurodevelopmental processes. Here, we show that the PTENG132D allele disrupts early neuroectoderm formation during the first several days of organoid generation, and results in deficient electrophysiology. While organoids generated from PTENM134R hiPSCs remained morphologically similar to wild-type organoids during this early stage in development, we observed disrupted neuronal differentiation, radial glia positioning, and cortical layering in both PTEN-mutant organoids at the later stage of 72+ days of development. Perifosine, an AKT inhibitor, reduced over-activated AKT and partially corrected the abnormalities in cellular organization observed in PTENG132D organoids. Single cell RNAseq analyses on early-stage organoids revealed that genes related to neural cell fate were decreased in PTENG132D mutant organoids, and AKT inhibition was capable of upregulating gene signatures related to neuronal cell fate and CNS maturation pathways. These findings demonstrate that different PTEN missense mutations can have a profound impact on neurodevelopment at diverse stages which in turn may predispose PHTS individuals to ASD. Further study will shed light on ways to mitigate pathological impact of PTEN mutants on neurodevelopment by stage-specific manipulation of downstream PTEN signaling components.

## Key findings

Central thesis: **two different germline PTEN missense alleles, in an otherwise isogenic background, produce divergent forebrain-organoid phenotypes that track their clinical association** — the ASD-linked allele is far more damaging than the cancer-linked allele, and the damage hits at distinct developmental stages. This is a *gene × allele/background* organoid disease model (the PTEN entry for the review's Table 1).

- **Two isogenic alleles, one parental line.** CRISPR/Cas9 knock-in of `PTEN` g.132 G>D (`PTEN^G132D`, from a PHTS-ASD patient) and g.134 M>R (`PTEN^M134R`, from a PHTS-cancer-without-ASD patient) into the male hiPSC line BJFF.6, both heterozygous (`/WT`). Genotype is the *only* variable. Forebrain organoids generated by a Qian et al. SpinΩ bioreactor protocol; assessed days 3–72+.
- **Early (days 0–7): `G132D` disrupts neuroectoderm; `M134R` looks WT.** `PTEN^G132D/WT` organoids formed narrow/elliptical, disfigured lumens (vs symmetrical circular lumens in WT and `M134R`); circularity ~0.6 ± 0.19 vs ~0.8 for the other two. Day-14 corticogenesis-unit SOX2+ perimeter: WT 476 ± 142 µm, `G132D` 185 ± 69 µm, `M134R` 452 ± 205 µm. NPC fractions in `G132D` were drastically reduced: SOX2+ down 58.6% and PAX6+ down 85.9% vs WT (`G132D` 38.5% SOX2+ / 12.9% PAX6+ vs WT ~91.7% / ~91.3%; `M134R` 77.5% / 67.1%). `M134R` early morphology was indistinguishable from WT until ~day 28, then overgrew/folded.
- **Failed E→N cadherin switch / blocked EMT in `G132D`.** WT and `M134R` sharply down-modulated E-cadherin and up-regulated N-cadherin by day 5; `G132D` retained high E-CDH and showed only minimal, delayed N-CDH (N-CDH not detected by IF at day 7) — i.e. cells stay in a pluripotent/stem state and fail the epithelial-to-neuroepithelial (EMT) transition. scRNA-seq pseudotime (Monocle3) confirmed `G132D` cells fail to mature and retain a pluripotent transcriptomic pattern (high KI67, low PAX6/SOX2).
- **Over-activated AKT is allele-graded.** `G132D` had significantly lower total PTEN protein (day 7: 0.10 vs WT 0.66, `M134R` 0.44) and the highest pAKT(S473) (day 5: `G132D` 1.53 vs WT 0.50, `M134R` 0.05). pAKT magnitude ordered `G132D` > `M134R` > WT during days 0–7, mirroring phenotype severity. No remarkable early differences in pAKT(T308), pS6, or 4E-BP1 between genotypes — i.e. the early defect tracks AKT(S473)/mTORC2-axis output more than canonical mTORC1 readouts.
- **Late (days 28–72+): both mutants disrupt layering, but `G132D` worse.** Peripheral TUJ1+ thickness grew 2.5-fold (d28→d72) in WT but increased less in `G132D` and not at all in `M134R`. TBR1+ neurons per corticogenesis unit rose 4.4-fold (d28→d72) in WT (to ~88) but stayed low in both mutants (`G132D` ~11, `M134R` ~9 at d72) and were disorganized relative to SOX2+ cells. pVIM+ radial-glia alignment around the VZ-like lumen was lost by d72 in `M134R` and was disorganized throughout in `G132D`.
- **Electrophysiology: `G132D` neurons fail to mature.** WT and `M134R` NPCs showed depolarization-activated Na+ currents (`I_Na`) and, by day 21, action-potential firing; `M134R` `I_Na` was smaller at day 7 (p = 0.042) but caught up by day 14 ("delayed but catching up"). `G132D` NPCs showed almost no `I_Na` at any of days 7/14/21 and lacked NaV1.1 immunostaining.
- **Pharmacological rescue (partial).** 1 µM **Perifosine** (AKT inhibitor) during days 0–7 suppressed pAKT in all genotypes and partially rescued `G132D`: lumen circularity rose from 0.575 → 0.765, VZ thickness roughly doubled (d5→d7) and exceeded WT by d7, and radial glia became tightly packed around the lumen. scRNA-seq of Perifosine-treated `G132D` showed upregulation of neural pathways ("Outgrowth of neurons/neurites," "Microtubule dynamics," "Differentiation of neural precursor cells"; involving ID1/ID3 repressors). **But** Perifosine did **not** restore the E→N cadherin switch / EMT, and SGK-1 (mTORC2 substrate) phosphorylation was unchanged → the cadherin-shift defect is AKT/mTORC2-independent.

## Methods

- **Cell lines / editing:** isogenic heterozygous `PTEN^G132D/WT` and `PTEN^M134R/WT` knock-ins (CRISPR/Cas9) in parental hiPSC line BJFF.6 (male skin-fibroblast-derived); heterozygosity confirmed by NGS. WT parental as control.
- **Organoids:** forebrain organoids via a modified Qian et al. SpinΩ miniaturized-spinning-bioreactor protocol — collagenase-IV EBs (day 0), dual-SMAD-type forebrain induction, Geltrex embedding (day 7), bioreactor from day 14, Matrigel-supplemented medium from day 35, cultured to days 72+. ≥3 organoid batches/condition, ≥3 independent repeats.
- **Readouts:** IF/confocal (SOX2, PAX6, NESTIN, pVIM, KI67, TUJ1, TBR1, CTIP2, SATB2, β-CATENIN, ZO-1, E-/N-cadherin, NaV1.1) with circularity, perimeter, thickness and marker-fraction quantification (ImageJ/FIJI); Western blot (PTEN, pAKT-S473/T308, AKT, pS6, 4E-BP1, GSK3β, E-/N-CDH, SGK-1); whole-cell patch-clamp of NPC cultures (`I_Na`, APs, days 7/14/21).
- **scRNA-seq:** 10x Genomics 3' v3.1 on day-7 organoids ± AKT inhibitor; 38,030 high-quality cells; Seurat 3.2.1 clustering, Monocle3 pseudotime trajectory, Ingenuity Pathway Analysis for DEGs (|log2FC|>1, p<0.05).
- **Drug:** 1 µM Perifosine (AKT inhibitor) added days 0–7 during forebrain induction.
- **Stats:** one-way (and two-way, for ± Perifosine) ANOVA with post-hoc Tukey-Kramer/Sidak; mean ± SD/SEM.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

This is the **PTEN entry in the review's Table 1 of gene × background organoid disease models** and a direct demonstration of *allele-resolved, genotype-dependent phenotypic divergence* — the kind of programmable, isogenic comparison the review's "proactive autism genetics" thesis is built on.

- **Gene × background / allele specificity:** by holding the genome constant and varying only the PTEN missense allele, it shows the ASD-associated `G132D` and the cancer-associated `M134R` diverge in both severity and developmental timing — an organoid demonstration that within a single top ASD risk gene, the specific variant dictates the neurodevelopmental phenotype.
- **Progenitor-stage vulnerability:** the most severe `G132D` defect is at the earliest neuroectoderm/NPC stage (failed EMT, depleted SOX2+/PAX6+ progenitors, disrupted radial-glia positioning), reinforcing the review's progenitor-proliferation/early-fate vulnerability theme. Convergence with other ASD genes is explicit: the authors tie the asynchronous-differentiation phenotype to [Paulsen 2022](paulsen_2022_autism_genes_converge_asynchronous_development_shared_neuron.md) and note PTEN-KO organoids show delayed differentiation + expanded proliferative pool.
- **mTOR/PI3K-AKT axis link:** situates PTEN-ASD on the same PI3K–AKT–mTOR signaling network as the tuberous-sclerosis work, providing a counterpart to [Blair 2018](blair_2018_genetically_engineered_human_cortical_spheroid_models_tuberous.md) (TSC1/2 → mTORC1) — both are mTOR-pathway organoid disease models with pharmacological proof-of-concept (Perifosine here; rapamycin in Blair).
- **Proactive/therapeutic angle:** early-window AKT inhibition partially correcting structural defects supports the review's "proactive" framing (stage-specific manipulation of downstream PTEN signaling, potentially in utero) while honestly bounding it (EMT/cadherin defect not rescued).

## Open questions / limitations

- **Partial rescue only.** Perifosine did not restore the E→N cadherin switch/EMT or full cellular organization; the cadherin-shift defect is mTORC2/SGK-1-independent and mechanistically unresolved (authors invoke PDK1- and non-canonical/nuclear PTEN functions).
- **Short culture window.** Organoids were followed only to ~70–72 days; later stages (up to ~6 months), gliogenesis, mature electrophysiology, and whether transient vs sustained AKT inhibition rescues long-term deficits are unexamined.
- **Two alleles, one parental line, one sex.** Only 2 of many PHTS PTEN variants tested, in a single male hiPSC background — generalizability across variants, donors and sex is open.
- **No microglia / non-cell-autonomous biology.** The germline model cannot separate cell-autonomous from microglia-mediated pathology; authors propose adding healthy microglia.
- **Mechanism of `M134R` late overgrowth.** Why the "cancer" allele looks WT early but overgrows/folds and loses radial-glia alignment by day 72 is described but not mechanistically dissected.
- **Small n for some late/ephys measures** (e.g. TBR1 counts and `I_Na` from a handful of organoids/cells per genotype).
