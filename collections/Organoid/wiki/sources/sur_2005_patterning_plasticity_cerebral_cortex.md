# Patterning and plasticity of the cerebral cortex.

## Abstract

The cerebral cortex of the human brain is a sheet of about 10 billion neurons divided into discrete subdivisions or areas that process particular aspects of sensation, movement, and cognition. Recent evidence has begun to transform our understanding of how cortical areas form, make specific connections with other brain regions, develop unique processing networks, and adapt to changes in inputs.

## Key findings

Short review (Science vol. 310, 4 Nov 2005, pp. 805-810) synthesizing how cortical areas are specified and then refined. Core thesis: the historical **Protomap (Rakic) vs. Protocortex (O'Leary)** debate is resolved — arealization is an interwoven cascade of *both* intrinsic (progenitor-zone) and extrinsic (thalamic-input/activity) mechanisms, not one or the other.

**Early patterning of the pallium**
- The cortex (pallium) develops from a "morphologically uniform" ventricular zone (VZ) in the dorsocaudal telencephalic vesicles. Pallium subdivides into medial (MP → hippocampal formation/limbic lobe), dorsal (DP → neocortex), lateral (LP → olfactory/piriform cortex), and ventral (VP → claustrum + parts of amygdala) domains.
- Regional identity is directed by secreted morphogens from **signaling/patterning centers** at neural-plate edges and telencephalic midline:
  - **Shh** — ventral telencephalon/hypothalamus; regionalizes subpallium, also patterns pallium.
  - **Bmp + Wnt** — dorsal midline; pattern medial/dorsal pallium (hippocampus, neocortex, choroid plexus). Inactivation of **Wnt3a** or the Wnt factor **Lef-1** severely disrupts hippocampus formation.
  - **Fgf8** — rostral telencephalon; promotes telencephalic outgrowth and rostral regionalization. Dosage-dependent: regulates size/nature of frontal cortex, midline structures, basal ganglia. Fgf8 level controls Bmp4 expression *nonmonotonically* (Bmp4/apoptosis fall in Fgf8 hypomorphs but rise as Fgf8 approaches null), partly via the Fgf-induced RTK-signaling repressor **Sprouty**.
- Patterning centers act by generating **graded transcription-factor (TF) expression** across mediolateral (dorsoventral) and rostrocaudal axes. Key graded TFs: **Foxg1 (BF1), COUPTF1, Emx2, Lef1, Lhx2, Pax6**. Fgf8 increases Foxg1 and *reduces* Emx2 and COUPTF1. Loss-of-function of COUPTF1, Emx2, Foxg1, Gli3, Lhx2, Pax6, Tlx all disrupt regionalization; Foxg1/Lhx2 mutants show expanded dorsal-most (Bmp/Wnt) features; Foxg1 inhibits differentiation and promotes proliferation; Gli3 represses ventral fates; Pax6/Tlx set ventral-pallium boundaries; Pax6 also specifies rostral identity, COUPTF1 caudoventral identity.

**Arealization → area identity and size**
- In **Emx2-null** neonates, occipital (presumptive visual) cortex adopts molecular fate of parietal (presumptive somatosensory) cortex, with a corresponding shift in thalamic projections. Subtle increases/decreases in **Emx2** dosage expand/contract relative sizes of neocortical subdivisions — i.e., gradient TF dosage tunes area proportions.
- Concept: altering relative strengths of patterning centers rescales cortical regions. A weak Fgf center predicts a small prefrontal cortex (a "hypofrontal" profile). **Ectopic Fgf8 can duplicate somatosensory cortex** — evidence that a morphogen can specify areal fate and a possible mechanism for evolutionary addition of new areas.
- Regionalization is linked to **neurogenesis** programs (laminar identity, projection patterns). Prevailing model: glutamatergic projection neurons of subplate + layers 6→2 produced sequentially by cortical (pallial) progenitors; **GABAergic local-circuit neurons produced by subpallial progenitors** (then migrate in); Cajal-Retzius cells of layer 1 from dorsal-most pallium. Emx2/Pax6/COUPTF1 drive region- and layer-specific expression of TFs (Id2, RZRbeta, Tbr1), adhesion molecules (Cadherin 6, 8), and axon-guidance molecules (EphrinA5).
- Open mechanistic question flagged: how *continuous* TF gradients in progenitors are translated into *discontinuous* molecular features within layers and area borders.

**Thalamocortical / corticothalamic connectivity (axon guidance)**
- Thalamic-axon entry point into cortex is regulated by cues in the **diencephalon and subcortical telencephalon**, not only by the cortex itself. Mutants in basal ganglia (**Ebf1**) or basal ganglia + prethalamus (**Dlx1/2**) misroute thalamic projections (e.g., somatosensory thalamus axons aim at immature visual cortex). **EphrinA5 (basal ganglia) / EphA4 (thalamus)** gradients organize this topography.
- Fgf8 hypomorphs disrupt neonatal *intraneocortical* projection patterns but spare topographic thalamocortical projections — supporting that thalamocortical topography is set largely by cues sensed en route. Consistent with the **"handshake hypothesis"**: Tbr1 mutants (paucity of corticothalamic axons) show defective thalamic-axon growth to cortex.
- Thalamic axons "wait" in the **subplate** for layer-4 neurons to settle before entering the cortical plate. Subplate ablation prevents formation of ocular dominance columns and orientation columns/cells.

**Activity-dependent network formation & plasticity** (visual cortex as model)
- A molecular/adhesion scaffold (cadherin splice variants, neurexins) lays down area-specific connectivity; **activity** is then superimposed. Activity can be *permissive* or *instructive*; the activity-independent vs. -dependent boundary blurs at later stages.
- **Ocular dominance (OD) columns**: initial eye-specific targeting is molecularly biased, but activity consolidates it. Monocular lid suture in a **critical period** shrinks the deprived eye's columns and shifts cortical responses to the open eye (competition for territory). OD plasticity depends on local GABAergic inhibition — basket cells with alpha1-GABA-receptor synapses; enhancing inhibition widens OD columns; a critical inhibition level gates opening/closing of the critical period. Cortex maintains excitation/inhibition balance and homeostatically scales synaptic drive.
- Structural substrate = **dendritic spines**: LTP correlates with spine enlargement/addition, LTD with spine shrinkage (actin-polymerization dependent). Brief monocular deprivation increases spine motility and reduces spine size in V1 (outside layer 4) as a prelude to losing deprived-eye connections. Signaling cascade implicated: CaMKII, PKA, ERK; extracellular-matrix proteolysis by **tissue plasminogen activator (tPA)** is a key mechanism for enhanced spine dynamics/loss after unbalanced activity.
- **Orientation selectivity / orientation maps**: present near eye-opening (and at birth in monkey V1), sharpened by experience; built on an early intrinsic scaffold then shaped by activity. Long-range horizontal connections link iso-orientation domains; their refinement is experience-dependent.
- **Cross-modal "rewiring" (Sur lab signature experiments)**: routing retinal input to the auditory thalamus (MGN) in neonatal ferrets makes **auditory cortex (A1) develop visual orientation-selective cells, pinwheel orientation maps, and V1-like patchy horizontal connections** — though the rewired map stays somewhat poorer than normal V1. Rewired animals can perceive "visual" via A1 and learn visually-cued fear conditioning rapidly via the auditory-thalamus→amygdala route. Demonstrates input modality can *instruct* cortical-area function; genetically determined pathways still depend on inputs for physiological/behavioral instruction.

## Methods

This is a **review article**, not a primary study — no new experiments, datasets, or organoid work. It synthesizes prior mouse/ferret/cat/primate genetics, electrophysiology, optical imaging, and anatomical tracing literature into a framework spanning early patterning → arealization → axon guidance → activity-dependent plasticity. Figures are schematic models (steps in generating cortical areas; patterning-center map and Fgf8→Emx2/Foxg1 cascade; activity shaping visual-cortex connections; cross-modal rewiring). Numeric specifics in the text are descriptive (e.g., "~10 billion" cortical neurons) rather than measured here. (Note: the source PDF concatenates a second, separate Science review — Feldman & Brecht, "Map Plasticity in Somatosensory Cortex" — which is *not* part of this paper and is excluded.)

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

Provides the **developmental-neurobiology background for cortical patterning and arealization** that grounds the manuscript's framing of what a brain organoid is trying to recapitulate.
- Establishes the canonical **morphogen → graded TF → proto-area** logic (Shh/Bmp/Wnt/Fgf8 acting through Emx2/Pax6/Foxg1/COUPTF1/Lhx2 gradients). This is the in-vivo template against which guided/patterned cortical organoid protocols are benchmarked, and the dorsal-pallium (DP → neocortex) emphasis matches the dorsal-forebrain identity targeted by most cortical organoid methods.
- The **dosage-tunes-area-size** principle (Emx2/Fgf8) and "ectopic Fgf8 duplicates an area" supply the conceptual basis for why patterning-factor perturbations reshape regional output — relevant when an ASD review discusses how genetic perturbation of patterning regulators (e.g., Foxg1, Pax6) could shift regional identity in organoid models.
- Reinforces that **GABAergic interneurons arise subpallially and migrate in**, motivating fusion/assembloid strategies in dorsal+ventral organoid work (a major theme of the broader review's interneuron-migration sections).
- The activity-dependent half (OD columns, E/I balance, critical-period inhibition, spine dynamics) is background for why organoids — lacking thalamic input and patterned sensory activity — capture early intrinsic patterning far better than late activity-dependent circuit refinement; an explicit limitation context for organoid disease modeling.
- Cited as **cortical-patterning/plasticity background** (per ingest brief), not for a specific quantitative claim.

## Open questions / limitations

- As a 2005 review, predates the modern human-specific progenitor literature (outer radial glia, NOTCH2NL) now central to organoid biology; its progenitor account is largely rodent-cell-biology framed.
- Explicit open question raised in-text: how graded TF expression in progenitors is converted into discontinuous laminar/areal molecular borders and region-specific connectivity maps.
- Mechanistic balance of intrinsic vs. extrinsic contributions to higher-order (associative) areas and to **lateralization of cortical function** flagged as unresolved.
- Largely non-quantitative for our purposes; numbers in text (e.g., neuron counts) are illustrative, not measurements from this work.
- Human relevance is inferred from rodent/ferret models; primate-specific features (expanded SVZ/oRG, higher-order areas) are noted as a frontier but not addressed experimentally here.
