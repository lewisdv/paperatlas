# Tbr2 directs conversion of radial glia into basal precursors and guides neuronal amplification by indirect neurogenesis in the developing neocortex.

## Source

- Authors: Sessa A, Mao CA, Hadjantonakis AK, Klein WH, Broccoli V
- Journal: Neuron (2008), vol. 60, 56-69 (9 October 2008)
- DOI: [10.1016/j.neuron.2008.09.028](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2008.09.028)
- PMID: [18940588](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18940588/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

T-brain gene-2 (Tbr2) is specifically expressed in the intermediate (basal) progenitor cells (IPCs) of the developing cerebral cortex; however, its function in this biological context has so far been overlooked due to the early lethality of Tbr2 mutant embryos. Conditional ablation of Tbr2 in the developing forebrain resulted in the loss of IPCs and their differentiated progeny in mutant cortex. Intriguingly, early loss of IPCs led to a decrease in cortical surface expansion and thickness with a neuronal reduction observed in all cortical layers. These findings suggest that IPC progeny contribute to the correct morphogenesis of each cortical layer. Our observations were confirmed by tracing Tbr2+ IPC cell fate using Tbr2::GFP transgenic mice. Finally, we demonstrated that misexpression of Tbr2 is sufficient to induce IPC identity in ventricular radial glial cells (RGCs). Together, these findings identify Tbr2 as a critical factor for the specification of IPCs during corticogenesis.

## Key findings

Primary mouse-genetics study establishing **Tbr2 (Eomes) as both necessary and sufficient for intermediate/basal progenitor cell (IPC) identity** in the developing neocortex. Loss-of-function (conditional knockout) deletes the IPC compartment and shrinks all cortical layers; gain-of-function (in utero electroporation) converts ventricular radial glia (RGCs) into IPCs. This is the cellular machinery of **indirect neurogenesis** — RGC → TBR2+ IPC (transit-amplifying) → neurons — as opposed to direct neurogenesis (RGC → neuron).

**Background cell biology (as framed by the paper)**
- Two germinal layers: apical **RGCs** lining the ventricle form the VZ (express Pax6, Emx2, Sox2; defined apico-basal polarity, apical cell junctions); basally located **IPCs** form the SVZ (express Tbr2, Cux1/2, Lmo4, Svet1; lack apical/basal contact, multipolar). IPCs arise from asymmetric RGC divisions, then typically undergo **one symmetric division (a few undergo a second)**, each terminal division yielding a pair (or two pairs) of neurons — i.e. IPCs are **neurogenic transit-amplifying progenitors**.
- Tbr2 transcript/protein is confined to IPCs but **not** their progeny, making it a clean IPC marker (used here as such, and exploited as a lineage reporter via protein perdurance).

**Loss of function — Tbr2^Δflox/Δflox; Foxg1-Cre (forebrain conditional KO)**
- Foxg1-Cre drives recombination in forebrain from **E9.5**; exon-3 excision removes most of the T-box domain → frameshift → null allele. Tbr2 transcript/protein undetectable in mutant forebrain by E12.5/E14.5. Mutants are **lethal at birth**, so analyzed at E18.5 (vs. control Tbr2^+/Δflox; Foxg1-Cre).
- Gross: reduced cortex size, severely **hypoblastic olfactory bulbs**; cortical-surface reduction noticeable as early as **E14.5**.
- Cortical thickness at E18.5: cortical plate (CP) **40.7% ± 8% thinner** overall; deep layers reduced **28.2% ± 5%**, upper layers **50.2% ± 6%** (n = 3/genotype). **~2-fold** thickness reduction by Math2 (Nex1/NeuroD6) panneuronal marker.
- CP cell **density unchanged** (WT 8700 ± 700 vs. mutant 9200 ± 500 cells/mm^2; n = 3 each) → deficit is true cell loss, not spreading.
- **IPC/SVZ proliferation collapses, RGC/VZ spared**: 1 hr BrdU pulse at E15.5 → **~22% fewer BrdU+ nuclei** (22.2% ± 3%, p < 0.01), concentrated in SVZ; basal (subventricular) PH3+ mitoses reduced by **74.5% ± 2-3%** (loss of IPC mitoses) while apical (ventricular, RGC) mitoses unchanged. Basal PH3+ deficit already present at **E11.5**.
- IPC marker genes strongly downregulated at E14.5-E15.5 (qRT-PCR, mutant vs. WT=1): **Svet1 −92% ± 2%, Lmo4 −78% ± 3%, NeuroD1 −56% ± 5%, Cux2 −65%** (reported as ~3-fold / "35% ± 6%" of control).
- **Biphasic neurogenesis defect**: at E11.5, *excess* premature neuronal differentiation (enlarged β-III-tubulin+/TuJ1+ domain; ectopic TuJ1+ neurons in the VZ) — RGCs shift from indirect to **direct** neurogenesis. Later, neuron production *falls*: NeuroD1+ neurons **~50% reduced** (E14.5); Tbr1+ mature early-born neurons **~60% reduced** (E14.5; reported ~59.8% ± 4%).
- **All layers affected** (argues IPC progeny populate every layer, not just upper): reduced deep-layer markers FoxP2 (VI/subplate), Tle4 (V/VI), ER81/Etv1 (V) and upper-layer markers Rorb (IV), Lhx2/Satb2/Lmo3 (II-III). Quantified in somatosensory cortex: **Tbr1+ deep neurons −66.4% ± 4.5%** and **−33.6% ± 5%** (two cell-count panels), **Satb2+ upper neurons −40.5% ± 2.4%** and **−59.5% ± 3%**, both p < 0.05. (Magnitudes differ slightly between Figure 4 panels; both directions consistent.)
- **No increased apoptosis** (TUNEL, activated caspase-3 at E11.5/E13.5/E14.5) → phenotype is failed IPC *specification/generation*, not failed IPC *maintenance/survival*.
- RGC pool intact: Pax6, Nestin, Glast, ZO-1 normal in number, organization, polarity.
- Severe **axon-guidance defect**: complete agenesis of the corpus callosum, Probst bundles (L1+ callosal fibers stalling at midline), and defective/absent anterior + hippocampal commissures — midline-crossing failure.

**Gain of function — Tbr2 misexpression (in utero electroporation, pCAG-Tbr2-IRES-EGFP into RGCs)**
- Misexpression (E13.5, analyzed +48 h at E15.5): expands the VZ/SVZ at the expense of the IZ; targeted cells adopt **multipolar (IPC-like) morphology** instead of bipolar.
- Drives ectopic proliferation in basal position: **~2-fold** more BrdU+ cells; **46% of Tbr2+ cells in S phase** (vs. 27% control); **8-fold increase in basal PH3+ mitoses** (cell-autonomous — supernumerary basal mitoses are GFP+), with a reciprocal **~4-fold drop in apical PH3+** mitoses.
- **Inhibits cell-cycle exit**: BrdU+/Ki67− (exited) fraction falls from **14.65% → 10.07%** (p < 0.05) over 24 h.
- **Sufficient to impose IPC molecular identity**: ectopically expands SVZ markers Svet1, Vglut2 (Slc17a6), NeuroD1, Lmo4 into apical/basal regions.
- **Represses RGC identity** cell-autonomously: focal loss of Pax6, Emx2, Sox2; disrupts VZ architecture and apical junctions (Nestin disarray; loss of Pals1, β-catenin). Drives ectopic VZ neuronal differentiation (TuJ1+, bHLHb5+, Satb2+). Some neighboring non-transfected RGCs also detach and differentiate (β-III-tubulin+/GFP−) — a **non-cell-autonomous** secondary effect attributed to lost VZ adhesion. No significant VZ apoptosis.
- **Long-term fate / laminar shift** (electroporate E12.5, analyze E18.5): control GFP+ cells settle deep and express Tbr1 (layer VI); Tbr2+ cells instead occupy **superficial** positions and express Satb2 (upper layers) — Tbr1 colocalization down ~5-fold, Satb2 colocalization up ~2.5-fold. Tbr2+ cells stay in cycle longer (BrdU+ when labeled at E15.5 but not E13.5), explaining the later birthdate / upper-layer positioning.

**Lineage tracing (Eomes::GFP / Tbr2::GFP BAC transgenic)**
- GFP overlaps endogenous Tbr2 at E11.5/E14.5; because GFP perdures, it traces IPC progeny. GFP+ cells populate **all cortical layers** (Tbr1+ deep and Satb2+ upper), with prevalence in upper layers — confirming Tbr2+ IPCs contribute neurons to every layer.

## Methods

- **System**: mouse embryonic forebrain, E10.5-E18.5/P0; in vivo only (no in vitro/organoid work).
- **Conditional knockout**: Tbr2^flox/flox (exon-3 floxed; Mao et al. 2008) × Foxg1-Cre (forebrain Cre from E9.5; Hébert & McConnell 2000) → Tbr2^Δflox/Δflox; Foxg1-Cre null vs. Tbr2^+/Δflox; Foxg1-Cre control.
- **Gain of function**: in utero electroporation at E12.5 or E13.5 of pCAG-Tbr2-IRES-EGFP (or pCAG-EGFP control) into ventricular RGCs (4 × 42 mV pulses, 400 ms intervals); harvest +24 h to +6 days.
- **Lineage tracer**: Eomes::GFP / Tbr2::GFP BAC transgenic (Kwon & Hadjantonakis 2007).
- **Readouts**: IHC (Tbr2, Tbr1, Pax6, Nestin, NeuroD1, β-III-tubulin/TuJ1, PH3, BrdU, Ki67, Satb2, neurofilament-H, L1, Pals1, β-catenin), in situ hybridization (Svet1, Vglut2, Lmo4, Math2, NeuroD1, FoxP2, Tle4, ER81, Rorb, Lhx2, Lmo3, Emx2, bHLHb5), qRT-PCR (β-actin normalized), Cresyl violet / Nissl / H&E, TUNEL + activated caspase-3.
- **Proliferation/cell-cycle**: BrdU pulse (1 h for S phase; 24 h to score cell-cycle exit as BrdU+/Ki67−); PH3 to distinguish apical (RGC) vs. basal (IPC) mitoses.
- **Statistics**: ≥3 sections from ≥3 animals/genotype; mean ± SD; one-tailed paired Student's t test.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

Supplies the **molecular control point for indirect neurogenesis (TBR2+ IPC amplification)** — the review's point that IPCs/oSVZ expansion underlies cortical neuronal output and human cortical expansion.
- Establishes the **RGC → TBR2+ IPC → neuron** transit-amplifying axis and shows Tbr2 is the switch that *makes* an IPC: removing it deletes the SVZ and shrinks every cortical layer; forcing it converts RGCs into proliferating IPCs. This is the canonical (mouse) basis for why organoid protocols and disease models track TBR2/EOMES as the readout of intermediate-progenitor amplification and indirect neurogenesis.
- The **biphasic phenotype** (transient excess of direct neurogenesis when IPCs are lost, then net neuronal deficit) gives a mechanistic template for interpreting how ASD/neurodevelopmental perturbations that bias the RGC division mode (direct vs. indirect) can simultaneously alter neuron *timing* and total neuron *number* in organoids.
- Connects directly to the review's progenitor-expansion theme: enlargement of the SVZ/IPC compartment is invoked as a driver of mammalian/human cortical surface expansion, the same expansion (via outer radial glia and a thick oSVZ) that human cortical organoids attempt to recapitulate.
- **Disease tie-in present in the paper**: homozygous silencing of human EOMES/TBR2 causes **microcephaly with polymicrogyria and corpus callosum agenesis** (Baala 2007) — the mutant mouse phenocopies this (thin cortex + callosal agenesis), reinforcing TBR2/IPC biology as relevant to human cortical malformation that organoid models study.
- Cited specifically for **TBR2+ IPC-driven indirect neurogenesis / progenitor amplification** (per ingest brief).

## Open questions / limitations

- All findings are **mouse in vivo**; the paper does no in vitro/organoid work, and human-specific IPC/oRG biology (expanded oSVZ, outer radial glia) is only inferred, not tested here.
- The downstream **molecular network through which Tbr2 imposes IPC identity is unknown** (authors call it "still unknown molecular mechanisms"); they speculate Tbr2 may repress adherens-junction signaling (E-cadherin, by analogy to its EMT role in gastrulation) to drive RGC delamination, but this is a hypothesis.
- Quantitative neuron-loss magnitudes differ between figure panels (e.g. Tbr1+ deep-neuron reduction reported as ~66% in one panel and ~34% in another; Satb2+ as ~40% vs. ~60%) — direction is robust, exact magnitude region/panel-dependent.
- **Whether Tbr2 acts purely cell-autonomously is partly open**: gain-of-function shows both cell-autonomous IPC conversion *and* non-cell-autonomous neighboring-RGC differentiation (via VZ disorganization).
- **Contradiction with prior literature noted in-text**: results argue IPC progeny populate *all* layers, conflicting with earlier reports (Tarabykin 2001; Wu 2005; Cubelos 2007) and with a contemporaneous Sox1-Cre Tbr2 knockout (Arnold 2008b) that found mainly upper-layer loss and only marginal callosal involvement — Cre line / onset timing likely explain the divergence.
- IPCs were reported to maintain RGCs by presenting Notch ligands (Kawaguchi 2008; Yoon 2008); here RGC proliferation/Hes1-5 looked preserved despite IPC loss, attributed (on unpublished data) to other VZ cell types presenting ligand — i.e. the Notch-feedback role of IPCs is not directly tested.
