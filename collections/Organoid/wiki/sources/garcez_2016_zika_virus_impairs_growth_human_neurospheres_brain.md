# Zika virus impairs growth in human neurospheres and brain organoids.

## Source

- Authors: Garcez PP, Loiola EC, Madeiro da Costa R, Higa LM, Trindade P, Delvecchio R, Nascimento JM, Brindeiro R, Tanuri A, Rehen SK
- Journal: Science (New York, N.Y.) (2016) — Science 352(6287):816-818 (published online 14 April 2016)
- DOI: [10.1126/science.aaf6116](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aaf6116)
- PMID: [27064148](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27064148/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest
- Document type: **Short experimental Report** (Rio de Janeiro; D'Or Institute / UFRJ / Unicamp). One of the first demonstrations that ZIKV stunts growth of human 3D neural cultures.

## Abstract

Since the emergence of Zika virus (ZIKV), reports of microcephaly have increased considerably in Brazil; however, causality between the viral epidemic and malformations in fetal brains needs further confirmation. We examined the effects of ZIKV infection in human neural stem cells growing as neurospheres and brain organoids. Using immunocytochemistry and electron microscopy, we showed that ZIKV targets human brain cells, reducing their viability and growth as neurospheres and brain organoids. These results suggest that ZIKV abrogates neurogenesis during human brain development.

## Key findings

In vitro proof, in **human iPSC-derived** neural models, that ZIKV infects neural stem cells and suppresses growth of 3D neural tissue — addressing the then-open question of whether the Brazilian ZIKV–microcephaly correlation was causal.

- **ZIKV productively infects human iPSC-derived NSCs.** At 24 h post-infection, viral envelope protein was detected in **10.10% of cells at MOI 0.025 and 21.7% at MOI 0.25** (ZIKV co-localizing perinuclearly with SOX2+ nuclei). Viral RNA accumulated in NSC supernatant by qRT-PCR (productive replication), even at MOI 0.0025.
- **Neurosphere formation is disrupted.** Mock NSCs formed round neurospheres by 3 DIV; ZIKV-infected NSCs formed morphologically abnormal neurospheres with cell detachment. By 6 DIV, hundreds of mock neurospheres survived but only a few ZIKV-infected ones did (MOI 2.5-0.025).
- **Cell death is the mechanism.** Electron microscopy of 6-DIV infected neurospheres showed viral particles (bound to membranes, in mitochondria/vesicles), apoptotic/pyknotic nuclei, swollen mitochondria, and smooth membrane structures (resembling other flavivirus infections). Apoptotic nuclei were seen in **all** infected neurospheres analyzed.
- **Brain-organoid growth is reduced ~40%.** 35-day-old iPSC brain organoids (Lancaster-style) were exposed to ZIKV or mock and tracked for **11 DIV** (n = 6 each). Average growth area of ZIKV-exposed organoids was reduced ~40%: **0.624 ± 0.064 mm² (ZIKV) vs 1.051 ± 0.108 mm² (mock)**, normalized; measured at 46 DIV (P < 0.05).
- **Specificity vs another flavivirus (key control).** Dengue virus 2 (DENV2), genetically related to ZIKV, infected NSCs at a similar rate at 24 h but, at matched MOI 0.025, did **not** raise caspase-3/7 cell death at 3 DIV, produced normal round neurospheres at 6 DIV, and did **not** reduce organoid growth over 11 days (DENV2 1.023 ± 0.131 mm² vs mock 1.011 ± 0.247 mm²). ⇒ the deleterious effect is not a generic flavivirus property.
- Interpretation by the authors: neurospheres capture very early neurogenesis and organoids recapitulate first-trimester-like cellular/molecular events (gene expression, cortical layering); ZIKV insult in this window plausibly **abrogates neurogenesis** and links the outbreak to congenital brain malformation, paralleling TORCHS-type congenital infections.

## Methods

Experimental, human iPSC-based, three nested 2D→3D models:

- **Cells/models:** human iPSC-derived neural stem cells (NSCs) → neurospheres → cerebral organoids (organoids per Lancaster et al. 2013 / Lancaster & Knoblich 2014). Neurospheres generated à la Reynolds & Weiss.
- **Virus:** ZIKV (Brazilian isolate lineage; Asian lineage per the outbreak); exposures at **MOI 0.25 to 0.0025** depending on assay. **DENV2 used as a flavivirus control**; mock as negative control.
- **Readouts:** confocal immunocytochemistry (ZIKV envelope, SOX2, DAPI); **qRT-PCR** of viral RNA (normalized to 12-h post-infection controls); **transmission electron microscopy** of neurosphere ultrastructure; **caspase-3/7** cell-death assays; neurosphere counts; **longitudinal organoid area measurements** over 11 DIV.
- **Stats:** Student's t test; data as mean ± SEM (n = 3-6 depending on panel). Ethics protocol no. 727.269 (Hospital Copa D'Or). Note: funding was general, not Zika-specific.
- **Scope limits:** small n; one virus strain; supplemental DENV2/viability data in figs. S1-S2.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Core proof-of-concept that brain organoids capture environmental/infectious insults on progenitors.** Together with Li 2016 (in this batch), it is the canonical demonstration that an *environmental* perturbation — not a genetic mutation — measurably impairs human progenitor proliferation/survival and 3D tissue growth in a dish.
- **Underwrites the gene×environment modeling argument** central to the "Programmable Brain Organoids for Proactive Autism Genetics" review: if organoids faithfully register an environmental hit to neural stem cells (here, ~40% growth reduction with a clean DENV2 specificity control), they are a valid platform for modeling gene×environment interactions on the same proliferative compartments.
- **Connects to the developmental reference landscape** (Silbereis 2016, this batch): ZIKV strikes the SOX2+ NSC/progenitor pool whose protracted, expanded proliferation defines human corticogenesis and microcephaly risk.
- Provides a quantitative, organoid-native phenotype (growth area) and a built-in specificity control — a useful methodological template for environmental-insult assays the review may cite.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Correlational at the human level**: shows ZIKV harms human neural cultures but does not by itself prove in vivo causation of microcephaly (the companion in vivo mouse evidence is Li 2016).
- **Phenotype is gross (size/viability)**, not mechanistically resolved here — no cell-cycle, lineage-marker, or transcriptomic dissection of *which* progenitor steps fail (contrast Li 2016's PAX6→TBR2 and cell-cycle analysis).
- **Small samples** (n = 3-6) and a single ZIKV isolate; MOIs are relatively high for some assays.
- Organoids/neurospheres lack vasculature, microglia/immune cells, and a blood-brain barrier, so route-of-infection, innate-immune, and placental contributions to human microcephaly are not modeled.
- Exposure was acute to pre-formed cultures; timing/dose-response across developmental windows is left open by the authors.
