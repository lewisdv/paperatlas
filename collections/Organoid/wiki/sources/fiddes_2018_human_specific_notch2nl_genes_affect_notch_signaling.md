# Human-Specific NOTCH2NL Genes Affect Notch Signaling and Cortical Neurogenesis.

## Abstract

Genetic changes causing brain size expansion in human evolution have remained elusive. Notch signaling is essential for radial glia stem cell proliferation and is a determinant of neuronal number in the mammalian cortex. We find that three paralogs of human-specific NOTCH2NL are highly expressed in radial glia. Functional analysis reveals that different alleles of NOTCH2NL have varying potencies to enhance Notch signaling by interacting directly with NOTCH receptors. Consistent with a role in Notch signaling, NOTCH2NL ectopic expression delays differentiation of neuronal progenitors, while deletion accelerates differentiation into cortical neurons. Furthermore, NOTCH2NL genes provide the breakpoints in 1q21.1 distal deletion/duplication syndrome, where duplications are associated with macrocephaly and autism and deletions with microcephaly and schizophrenia. Thus, the emergence of human-specific NOTCH2NL genes may have contributed to the rapid evolution of the larger human neocortex, accompanied by loss of genomic stability at the 1q21.1 locus and resulting recurrent neurodevelopmental disorders.

## Key findings

- **Three human-specific NOTCH2NL paralogs at 1q21.1.** The corrected GRCh38 assembly resolves **NOTCH2NLA, NOTCH2NLB, NOTCH2NLC** in the 1q21.1 locus plus a fourth divergent paralog **NOTCH2NLR** (near NOTCH2 on 1p). The >100 kb regions spanning each gene are **>99.1% identical to NOTCH2**. NOTCH2NL is a partial duplication of NOTCH2: it keeps the NOTCH2 promoter and the **six N-terminal EGF-like domains** (exons 1–4) but lacks the transmembrane and intracellular domains; a 5th exon from NOTCH2 intron adds **20 unique amino acids**. A **4 bp deletion** in exon 5 of NOTCH2NLA/B/C is **essential for protein expression**; NOTCH2NLR lacks it and is likely a non-functional pseudogene (14% of the Simons Diversity population, n=266, lack NOTCH2NLR).
- **Human-specific, ~3–4 mya origin.** Chimpanzee and gorilla carry only truncated/fusion **NOTCH2NL pseudogenes** (e.g., 8 pseudogenes in chimp) that do **not** encode NOTCH2-related protein; no NOTCH2NL protein is detectable in chimpanzee cortical tissue under conditions where human protein is seen. The functional human gene was "revived" from an ancestral PDE4DIP-NOTCH2NL fusion pseudogene by **ectopic gene conversion from NOTCH2**, estimated at **~3–4 million years ago** — coincident with the onset of hominin neocortical expansion — then duplicated twice more.
- **Extreme allelic/structural complexity ("paratypes").** Ongoing ectopic gene conversion between NOTCH2NLA and NOTCH2NLB is so extensive they behave as a single gene with multiple alleles. The authors identified **8 NOTCH2NL alleles ("paratypes")** across 15 genomes; a typical genotype has **6 paratypes** (not 2 haplotypes). Combined NOTCH2NLA+B copy number is **stably ~4 in all individuals** (suggesting dosage selection), whereas NOTCH2NLC-like (NOTCH2NLSh-2ntdel) copy number varies (0/1/2 copies at 0.4%/6%/92% in Simons data).
- **Radial-glia-enriched expression.** In **3,466 human fetal brain single cells (GW 11–21.5; Nowakowski et al. 2017)**, NOTCH2NL expression mirrors NOTCH2 and is highest in **radial glia including oRG**, plus astrocytes and microglia. In H9 hESC-derived cortical organoids all H9 alleles are expressed; **NOTCH2NLL is the most abundant (43.6% of transcripts** by full-length MinION nanopore cDNA-seq), others 8–40% of NOTCH2NLL.
- **Ectopic NOTCH2NL delays neuronal differentiation (mouse organoids).** Mouse ESC line stably expressing human NOTCH2NLSh,T197I vs empty vector; cortical organoids harvested day 6 (Pax6/Sox2 RG stage). **147 DE genes** (p-adj < 0.05). Upregulated = negative regulators of neuron differentiation (**Foxg1, Id4, Fezf2, Sox3, Six3**; Id4 is a Notch effector); downregulated = neuronal differentiation genes (Cntn2, Nefl, Gap43, Sox10). → ectopic NOTCH2NL delays progenitor differentiation.
- **NOTCH2NL deletion accelerates differentiation (human organoids).** CRISPR/Cas9 H9 clone with homozygous NOTCH2NLA+B deletion + heterozygous NOTCH2NLC deletion (NOTCH2/NOTCH2NLR intact) = **H9-NOTCH2NLΔ**; control H9*. NOTCH2NLΔ organoids were **smaller** throughout. By RNA-seq, **week-4 NOTCH2NLΔ organoids correlate better with week-5 than week-4 control organoids** — i.e., precociously advanced. 212 DE genes correlating better with w5 were enriched for **neuron differentiation** (BCL11B/CTIP2, DLX1, SEMA3A, UNC5D, FGFR2); IF confirmed **increased CTIP2 (layer V) protein** in w4 NOTCH2NLΔ, mimicking the normal w4→w5 transition. → loss of NOTCH2NL drives premature cortical-neuron differentiation.
- **NOTCH2NL physically binds NOTCH receptors and enhances signaling.** NOTCH2NL co-IPs with NOTCH2 (not with EGFR/PDGFRB). In a NOTCH2-GAL4 / pGL3-UAS luciferase reporter (U2OS): NOTCH2NLSh and NOTCH2NLL,T197I increased NOTCH2 activation by **+24% and +63%** at baseline; under JAG2 ligand co-culture (10× higher baseline) still **+23% and +21%**. Secreted NOTCH2NL in conditioned medium also activated the reporter (+24%, +22%). Enhancement is **not restricted to NOTCH2** (also NOTCH1, NOTCH3) or to a specific ligand (also DLL1, DLL4). Single segregating amino-acid changes tune potency: e.g., the **T197I substitution** (disrupts a conserved fucosylation site) made NOTCH2NLSh more potent under JAG2 (+53% vs +25%), with context-dependent effects for the long form. Short forms (no signal peptide) appear to act extracellularly via unconventional secretion; long forms use the canonical secretory pathway.
- **NOTCH2NL genes are the 1q21.1 breakpoints / CNV substrate.** Remapping CNV data to GRCh38 placed NOTCH2NLA/B inside the 1q21.1 distal deletion/duplication interval: **all 9 microcephaly cases consistent with NOTCH2NLA/B deletion; both macrocephaly cases consistent with duplication**. Deep-sequencing of **6 Simons VIP autism patients** (1 duplication, 5 deletions) found breakpoints within/near NOTCH2NLA/B, giving a **~2.4 Mb** deleted/duplicated region; copy number ~1.5 at NOTCH2NLA/B in deletions (one hybrid A/B gene) and ~2.5 in duplications (extra hybrid allele) — i.e., CNVs arise from **non-allelic homologous recombination (NAHR)** between the near-identical NOTCH2NLA/B loci. Clinical directionality: **duplication → macrocephaly + autism; deletion → microcephaly + schizophrenia**. In Simons VIP, 1q21.1 distal **duplication probands: autism 41%, intellectual disability 29%, ADHD 29%**; deletion probands lower but 26% anxiety/mood.

## Methods

- **Genomics/evolution:** de novo assembly of NOTCH2NL loci (H9 hESC; "Gordian" assembler, assembly-by-phasing) in 8 normal + 6 patient genomes; SUN-based copy-number analysis across 2,531 1000-Genomes + 266 Simons Diversity genomes; archaic-human (Neanderthal/Denisovan) comparison to date the gene-conversion event; primate (chimp/gorilla iPSC) NOTCH2NL fusion-gene characterization; 10x Chromium oligo-capture deep sequencing of 1q21.1 patient fibroblasts.
- **Expression:** scRNA-seq of 3,466 fetal brain cells (Nowakowski et al. 2017); Illumina + full-length MinION nanopore cDNA RNA-seq of H9 hESCs and week-5 cortical organoids to quantify paratype-specific expression.
- **Functional cortical models:** mouse ESC cortical organoids (Eiraku/SFEBq) stably expressing human NOTCH2NLSh,T197I vs EV, day-6 RNA-seq; human H9 cortical organoids (Eiraku directed differentiation) with CRISPR NOTCH2NL deletion (H9-NOTCH2NLΔ) vs H9*, weekly RNA-seq + IF (PAX6/BLBP, TBR2, CTIP2).
- **Notch signaling assays:** co-IP/immunoblot of NOTCH2 + NOTCH2NL; NOTCH-GAL4 / pGL3-UAS luciferase reporter in U2OS ± U2OS-JAG2 ligand co-culture and ± conditioned medium; tested NOTCH1/2/3 and JAG2/DLL1/DLL4. Stats: two-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD; reporter n up to 24 across 4 experiments.
- **Clinical CNV:** reanalysis of microarray CNV from 11 microcephaly/macrocephaly patients remapped to GRCh38; primary fibroblasts from 6 Simons VIP autism 1q21.1 patients for breakpoint mapping.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Primary support for the review's "human-specific genes expand/delay cortical neurogenesis" theme.** NOTCH2NL is a recently evolved, human-specific NOTCH paralog enriched in radial glia/oRG that **enhances Notch signaling, delays progenitor differentiation, and thereby prolongs the neurogenic window** — the molecular logic by which the human cortex was expanded. Demonstrated in both gain-of-function (mouse organoids, ectopic NOTCH2NL) and loss-of-function (human CRISPR-deletion organoids, premature differentiation) directions.
- **Directly ties human-specific gene biology to ASD genetics via 1q21.1.** NOTCH2NLA/B provide the NAHR substrate / breakpoints for the **~2.4 Mb 1q21.1 distal CNV syndrome**: duplication → macrocephaly + autism; deletion → microcephaly + schizophrenia. This is the clearest mechanistic bridge in the collection between a human-specific cortical-expansion gene and a recurrent autism-associated CNV — exactly the "proactive autism genetics" angle of the manuscript.
- **oRG connection.** Notch signaling is required for oRG self-renewal; NOTCH2NL's RG/oRG-enriched expression and signal-enhancing activity position it as a candidate factor supporting the sustained oRG proliferation that underlies human cortical expansion — linking this paper to the review's oRG theme alongside the lissencephaly/oRG-mitosis work.
- **Cortical organoids as the functional assay.** Both the gain- and loss-of-function tests were run in cortical organoids, reinforcing the review's case for organoids as the platform to dissect human-specific neurodevelopmental genes.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Gain-of-function was tested in mouse (not human) organoids** with a single allele (NOTCH2NLSh,T197I); human-organoid overexpression of the proliferation/neuronal-output phenotype is supplied by the companion Suzuki et al. (2018), referenced here but not performed in this paper.
- **Selective forces on NOTCH2NL dosage/paratypes remain "mysterious."** Combined A+B copy number is stably ~4 (suggesting selection), yet normal individuals carry non-functional R113* paratypes (31/266 with 1, 4/266 with 2), arguing against tight ongoing selection. The authors leave open whether the optimal paratype balance is fixed.
- **NOTCH2NL is not the only gene in the 1q21.1 interval.** HYDIN2, CHD1L, BCL9, GJA5/8, NBPF/DUF1220 paralogs, etc. also change copy number; the paper argues NOTCH2NL *enables* the CNV (NAHR substrate) and likely contributes to brain-size phenotypes, but multiple genes may contribute and causal attribution is incomplete.
- **Reporter/co-IP assays are in non-neural U2OS cells**; the precise endogenous mechanism (cis vs trans, secretion route) in human RG is inferred. (The companion Suzuki paper proposes cis Delta/Notch inhibition.)
- Older 1q21.1 microarray data lack unique probes for precise breakpoints in this repetitive region; deep-sequencing breakpoint mapping limited to 6 patients.
