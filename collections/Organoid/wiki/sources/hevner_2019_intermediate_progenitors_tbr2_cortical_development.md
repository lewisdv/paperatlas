## Abstract

In developing cerebral cortex, intermediate progenitors (IPs) are transit amplifying cells that specifically express Tbr2 (gene: Eomes), a T-box transcription factor. IPs are derived from radial glia (RG) progenitors, the neural stem cells of developing cortex. In turn, IPs generate glutamatergic projection neurons (PNs) exclusively. IPs are found in ventricular and subventricular zones, where they differentiate as distinct ventricular IP (vIP) and outer IP (oIP) subtypes. Morphologically, IPs have short processes, resembling filopodia or neurites, that transiently contact other cells, most importantly dividing RG cells to mediate Delta-Notch signaling. Also, IPs secrete a chemokine, Cxcl12, which guides interneuron and microglia migrations and promotes thalamocortical axon growth. In mice, IPs produce clones of 1-12 PNs, sometimes spanning multiple layers. After mitosis, IP daughter cells undergo asymmetric cell death in the majority of instances. In mice, Tbr2 is necessary for PN differentiation and subtype specification, and to repress IP-genic transcription factors. Tbr2 directly represses Insm1, an IP-genic transcription factor gene, as well as Pax6, a key activator of Tbr2 transcription. Without Tbr2, abnormal IPs transiently accumulate in elevated numbers. More broadly, Tbr2 regulates the transcriptome by activating or repressing hundreds of direct target genes. Notably, Tbr2 'unlocks' and activates PN-specific genes, such as Tbr1, by recruiting Jmjd3, a histone H3K27me3 demethylase that removes repressive epigenetic marks placed by polycomb repressive complex 2. IPs have played an important role in the evolution and gyrification of mammalian cerebral cortex, and TBR2 is essential for human brain development.

## Key findings

A synthesis of **intermediate progenitors (IPs / IPCs)** as the **Tbr2(EOMES)+ transit-amplifying** step of **indirect neurogenesis** (RG → IP → projection neuron), the genetic/epigenetic logic of Tbr2, and the role of IPs in cortical evolution and gyrification.

### IP identity, subtypes, and the lineage cascade
- IPs are **transit-amplifying cells** of the cortex, derived from radial glia (RG), generating **glutamatergic projection neurons (PNs) exclusively** (never GABAergic interneurons). Marked specifically by **Tbr2/Eomes** (Englund et al. 2005; transient early Tbr2 also in Cajal-Retzius/subplate before ~E12.5 in mouse).
- **Sequential TF cascade Pax6 → Tbr2 → Tbr1** maps onto **RG → IP → postmitotic PN**. Pax6 (RG) directly activates Tbr2; Tbr2 directly activates Tbr1 (PN) and **directly represses Pax6** (negative feedback) and the IP-genic factor **Insm1**.
- **Two mouse IP subtypes** (single-cell, Kawaguchi 2008): **ventricular IPs (vIPs)** - radial "pin-like," in the VZ, less differentiated, Tbr2+; and **outer IPs (oIPs)** - multipolar, in the SVZ, more differentiated (Neurod1+). vIPs arise from RG, then via Robo2-dependent detachment convert to oIPs; some vIPs make neurons directly.
- Non-surface (abventricular) mitoses historically ~**20%** (Smart 1973) up to **~35%** (Takahashi 1995) of cortical divisions - the cellular basis later attributed to IPs.

### Clonal output and asymmetric death
- Mouse IP clones span **1-12 PNs** (most IPs make just 1-2; a minority keep proliferating; older reports up to 8 [Wu 2005] or 32 [Vasistha 2015]).
- IPs from **early** stages make both lower- and upper-layer PNs; **late** IPs make upper layers only - consistent with **progressive restriction** and **laminar-fate multipotency** set by local VZ/SVZ context at neuron birth.
- **~66% of IP-derived clones (Tbr2-CreER MADM) show asymmetric cell death** (one daughter only) - i.e. the majority of IP daughter cells die; functions (output regulation, genome QC, subtype ratios) unknown.

### IP signaling functions (non-cell-autonomous)
- **Delta-Notch maintenance of RG:** IP processes carry **Dll1/Dll3** and contact dividing RG (Notch1-3+), instructing RG to **stay as stem cells**; loss of Notch (or of the E3 ligase **Mib1**) → premature RG→IP conversion. IPs are thus the principal Notch-ligand source balancing RG self-renewal vs differentiation.
- **Cxcl12 secretion** by IPs guides tangential **interneuron** and **microglia** migration (via Cxcr4/Cxcr7) and promotes **thalamocortical axon** growth - coordinating PN genesis with cortical wiring.

### Tbr2 genetics and epigenetics
- **Tbr2/Eomes** is essential beyond cortex (homozygous null → blastocyst-stage arrest; trophoblast/mesoderm). Cortical conditional KO phenotypes are **Cre-driver-dependent**: Foxg1-Cre cKO is an outlier (Foxg1 haploinsufficiency alone cuts IPs ~39% at E16.5; lethal at birth); Sox1-/Nes11-/Gfap-/Emx1-Cre cKOs survive.
- Non-surface mitoses change by model: Foxg1-Cre **-75%** (E15.5); Sox1-Cre **~-25%** (E14); **Nes11-Cre** shows dynamics - surface mitoses **+70%** at E12.5, then non-surface **+40% above normal** at E14.5 with **ectopic mitoses in IZ/CP** (oIPs accumulate from a block in IP→PN differentiation).
- **Tbr2 directly regulates ~882 genes (502 activated, 380 repressed; "chip-ChIP", Elsen 2018)**, including **18 epigenetic-factor genes**. Tbr2 **recruits Jmjd3 (H3K27me3 demethylase)** to "unlock" PN genes (e.g. Tbr1, Satb2) from PRC2 repression; drives **BAF chromatin-remodeler subunit switching** (Smarcd3, Bcl7a, Bcl11b/Ctip2, Dpf3). Upper layers (2-4, Cux1/2, Satb2) are most affected in cKO; Tbr2 also directly represses layer-5 genes (Kat6b) and IP-genic Pax6/Insm1.
- Rostrocaudal **Tbr2 gradient** (high rostral) propagates the RG "protomap" to an IP "intermediate map" to PNs; loss → **caudalized** area patterning + small olfactory bulb.

### Evolution, gyrification, and human disease
- IPs and Tbr2+ SVZ progenitors are **conserved** across fetal human, non-human primate, ferret, and even non-mammals (lizard, turtle, chicken, frog) - so IPs as a **cell type predate** six-layered/gyrencephalic neocortex.
- Gyrencephalic species have an **expanded (inner+outer) SVZ** rich in **oIPs and oRG**; **oRG were first defined in the human outer SVZ** (Hansen 2010). Experimental Tbr2 interference **impairs cortical folding** and, in ferret, reduces both oRG and oIP and causes premature SVZ neurogenesis - implicating localized oRG+oIP proliferation in **gyrification initiation**.
- Evolutionary shifts in the **balance of direct (RG) vs indirect (IP) neurogenesis** track **Robo (direct) vs Dll1 (indirect)** signaling levels (Cardenas 2018).
- **TBR2/EOMES is essential for human brain development:** homozygous silencing causes a severe syndrome - **microcephaly, polymicrogyria, corpus-callosum agenesis, cerebellar hypoplasia, severe motor/cognitive delay, hypotonia** (Baala 2007). (Hevner cautions the "polymicrogyria" call was imaging-only; the malformation is best described as abnormal gyrification on a background of reduced neurogenesis/microcephaly.)

## Scope

- **Format:** single-author narrative review in *Journal of Anatomy*; no new experiments or datasets. Synthesizes ~60 primary references plus the author's own group's work (Mihalas & Hevner clonal MADM; Elsen/Sessa Tbr2 networks).
- **Taxonomic emphasis:** predominantly **mouse** developmental neuroanatomy (Tbr2 cKO phenotypes, clonal lineage tracing, single-cell IP subtyping), with comparative sections on ferret/primate/human and non-mammalian vertebrates.
- **Conceptual organization:** IP discovery and subtypes → Delta-Notch and Cxcl12 functions → Tbr2 mutant phenotypes (mitosis, layers, arealization) → Tbr2 direct-target / epigenetic network (Pax6→Tbr2→Tbr1) → evolution, gyrification, and human EOMES disease. Figures are schematics of the PN lineage, IP interactions, cKO phenotypes, and the Tbr2 TF network.
- **Not covered / explicitly open:** mechanism of IP-daughter apoptosis; factors driving IP proliferation vs immediate differentiation; what represses Tbr2 at cell-cycle exit; when in evolution IPs first became important; whether passing through an IP stage is required for functional cortical neurons (regenerative-medicine question).

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

**Secondary / supporting source** for the review's **indirect-neurogenesis (TBR2+ IPC amplification)** point - background cell-biology rather than an organoid result.

- **Defines the IPC compartment** the organoid-fidelity papers detect and measure. EOMES/TBR2+ IPCs are the RG→neuron amplifying step; their relative under-production in organoids is exactly the deficit quantified by [Camp 2015](camp_2015_human_cerebral_organoids_recapitulate_gene_expression_programs.md) (basal progenitors 6% of organoid NPCs vs 34% fetal) and seen as a TBR2+ SVZ-like compartment in [Pollen 2019](pollen_2019_establishing_cerebral_organoids_as_models_human_specific.md) IHC. This review supplies the **mechanistic identity** (Pax6→Tbr2→Tbr1, Dll1-Notch feedback) behind those marker readouts.
- **Frames indirect neurogenesis as the amplification lever for cortical expansion** - the conceptual core the review borrows: oIP + oRG proliferation in an expanded SVZ underlies gyrification and human cortical size, the same biology the human-specific-evolution organoid strand ([Pollen 2019](pollen_2019_establishing_cerebral_organoids_as_models_human_specific.md), NOTCH2NL/ARHGAP11B) is built on. EOMES is also one of the cortical-progenitor TFs down-regulated across the organoid neurogenic transition in [Amiri 2018](amiri_2018_transcriptome_epigenome_landscape_human_cortical_development_modeled.md).
- **Connects IPCs to ASD-relevant processes** without itself being an ASD paper: IP-secreted Cxcl12 guides **interneuron migration** (E/I-balance relevance, cf. Mariani FOXG1/GABA work), and Tbr2's epigenetic toolkit (Jmjd3/PRC2, BAF switching) overlaps chromatin-regulator ASD genes (CHD8, ARID1B/BAF). Useful as the citation for "why IPC dynamics matter to cortical disorders."
- **Human-genetics anchor:** EOMES loss → microcephaly/malformation syndrome (Baala 2007) shows TBR2 is required in *human* corticogenesis - supporting use of the gene/pathway as an organoid-modeling target.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Mouse-dominated; human/primate evidence is comparative and thinner.** Most quantitative claims (clone sizes, % asymmetric death, cKO mitotic shifts) are murine; their quantitative transfer to human (where oRG/oIP and the OSVZ are far larger) is asserted, not measured.
- **Cre-driver dependence undermines clean Tbr2 phenotypes.** The author repeatedly flags Foxg1-Cre confounds (Foxg1 haploinsufficiency) and discounts that model's layer-5 result - illustrating how model choice drives conflicting reports of IP number/direction.
- **IP-daughter apoptosis is mechanistically unexplained** (the ~66% asymmetric-death figure is from the author's own MADM work; triggers and purpose unknown; partly "data not shown"/"preliminary").
- **Human EOMES-deficiency neuropathology is imaging-based only** (Baala 2007) - histology of the cortical malformation was never characterized, so "polymicrogyria" vs other abnormal gyrification is unresolved.
- **Review, not primary data:** claims inherit the limitations of the underlying primary studies and the author's framing; no systematic methodology or meta-analysis.
