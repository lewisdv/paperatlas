# SHANK3 and IGF1 restore synaptic deficits in neurons from 22q13 deletion syndrome patients.

## Source

- Authors: Shcheglovitov A, Shcheglovitova O, Yazawa M, Portmann T, Shu R, Sebastiano V, Krawisz A, Froehlich W, Bernstein JA, Hallmayer JF, Dolmetsch RE (Stanford Dept. of Neurobiology; senior author Ricardo E. Dolmetsch).
- Journal: Nature 503, 267–271 (14 November 2013).
- DOI: [10.1038/nature12618](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature12618)
- PMID: [24132240](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24132240/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

Phelan-McDermid syndrome (PMDS) is a complex neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by global developmental delay, severely impaired speech, intellectual disability, and an increased risk of autism spectrum disorders (ASDs). PMDS is caused by heterozygous deletions of chromosome 22q13.3. Among the genes in the deleted region is SHANK3, which encodes a protein in the postsynaptic density (PSD). Rare mutations in SHANK3 have been associated with idiopathic ASDs, non-syndromic intellectual disability, and schizophrenia. Although SHANK3 is considered to be the most likely candidate gene for the neurological abnormalities in PMDS patients, the cellular and molecular phenotypes associated with this syndrome in human neurons are unknown. We generated induced pluripotent stem (iPS) cells from individuals with PMDS and autism and used them to produce functional neurons. We show that PMDS neurons have reduced SHANK3 expression and major defects in excitatory, but not inhibitory, synaptic transmission. Excitatory synaptic transmission in PMDS neurons can be corrected by restoring SHANK3 expression or by treating neurons with insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF1). IGF1 treatment promotes formation of mature excitatory synapses that lack SHANK3 but contain PSD95 and N-methyl-D-aspartate (NMDA) receptors with fast deactivation kinetics. Our findings provide direct evidence for a disruption in the ratio of cellular excitation and inhibition in PMDS neurons, and point to a molecular pathway that can be recruited to restore it.

## Key findings

- **Patient-specific iPSC model of Phelan-McDermid syndrome (PMDS / 22q13.3 deletion).** Six iPSC lines from two patients with PMDS and autism, each carrying a heterozygous **~1 Mb deletion of chromosome 22q13.3** (encompassing SHANK3); compared to control iPSC/ESC lines. SHANK3 haploinsufficiency confirmed by MLPA.
- **Enrichment for mature forebrain neurons via CaMKIIα-GFP.** In unselected cultures only ~2–5% of cells expressed SHANK1–SHANK3 and ~42% expressed pan-neuronal markers (~15% cortical-layer markers). A `CaMKIIα-GFP` reporter gave **~12-fold enrichment** for SHANK1–3-expressing cells; GFP+ cells were ~99% MAP2+, ~29% TBR1+, ~66% CTIP2+, ~42% SATB2+, ~7.5% GABA/GAD67+ (predominantly excitatory cortical/striatal identity).
- **Selective excitatory synaptic deficit.** PMDS neurons had increased input resistance and reduced amplitude and frequency of spontaneous EPSCs (***P < 0.001). In co-culture (controls + PMDS on the same astrocyte bed, labeled GFP vs mKate2), spontaneous EPSC amplitude and frequency were significantly reduced, and evoked AMPA- and NMDA-EPSCs were smaller across stimulus intensities — i.e. the defect is **postsynaptic and cell-autonomous**.
- **Inhibition is spared.** Spontaneous and evoked IPSCs did not differ between control and PMDS neurons; GABA-evoked currents were normal — implying a shifted excitation/inhibition (E/I) balance at the cellular level.
- **Mechanism: fewer glutamate receptors and fewer excitatory synapses.** PMDS neurons expressed significantly less GLUA1 and GLUN1 protein and had smaller focal AMPA- and NMDA-evoked currents; SYN1+ and HOMER1+SYN1+ puncta were significantly reduced.
- **SHANK3 mRNA and longest-isoform protein were specifically reduced** (SHANK1, SHANK2, MAP2 unchanged), confirming that the heterozygous deletion downregulates SHANK3.
- **Genetic rescue.** Re-expressing the longest SHANK3 isoform (GFP-tagged lentivirus) in PMDS neurons restored spontaneous EPSC amplitude and frequency; it fully rescued evoked AMPA- and NMDA-EPSCs in **43% of recorded cells** and rescued AMPA-only EPSCs in the remaining ~57% — suggesting SHANK3 acts differently on AMPA vs NMDA receptors.
- **Pharmacological rescue with IGF-1.** Of agents tested (TSA, valproic acid, nifedipine, IGF1, IGF2), **only IGF1 (20 ng/mL, ~2–3 weeks)** restored HOMER1+SYN1+ puncta, spontaneous EPSC amplitude/frequency, evoked AMPA-/NMDA-EPSC amplitudes, and normalized input resistance to control levels; IGF1 had no effect on IPSCs.
- **IGF-1 works by shifting synapse composition, not by restoring SHANK3.** IGF1 actually *decreased* SHANK3 protein and promoted a class of excitatory synapses that **lack SHANK3 but contain PSD95** (in PMDS, PSD95+ puncta increased ~340%; in controls ~45%). PMDS neurons showed faster-decaying NMDA-EPSCs (GLUN2A-like); IGF1 made control NMDA-EPSCs decay faster to match PMDS, whereas SHANK3 re-expression slowed PMDS decay toward control (GLUN2B-like) — consistent with IGF1 bypassing SHANK3 by driving formation of PSD95-containing, rapidly-deactivating mature synapses.

## Methods

- **iPSC generation:** retroviral SOX2/OCT3-4/c-MYC/KLF4 reprogramming of patient skin-punch fibroblasts; lines karyotyped (SKY), teratoma-validated, pluripotency-marker confirmed; SHANK3 haploinsufficiency verified by SALSA MLPA.
- **Neural differentiation:** modified monolayer protocol (dual-SMAD-based neural conversion combined with a cortical-neuron protocol); cells characterized by qRT–PCR and multiplex single-cell qRT–PCR (Fluidigm 96.96 arrays). Mature excitatory neurons enriched by `CaMKIIα-GFP` (or `CaMKIIα-mKate2`) lentivirus and co-cultured on rat cortical astrocytes.
- **Electrophysiology:** whole-cell patch clamp (HEKA EPC10) at room temperature, day 31–45 (no astrocytes) or day 45–52 (astrocyte co-culture); spontaneous and evoked EPSCs/IPSCs at −70 mV / +60 mV with pharmacological isolation (NBQX, APV, bicuculline/picrotoxin); focal AMPA (200 µM), NMDA (100 µM + 10 µM Gly), and GABA application; NMDA-EPSC decay quantified as weighted τdecay.
- **SHANK3 rescue construct:** longest rat SHANK3 cDNA fused to EGFP in a pHAGE-EF1α lentiviral vector; transduced at day 25–30.
- **Drug treatments:** 0.1 µM TSA, 1 mM VPA, 3 µM nifedipine, 20 ng/mL IGF1, 20 ng/mL IGF2; TSA/VPA/Nif assessed at 24–36 h, IGF1/IGF2 at 2–3 weeks.
- **Synapse assays:** immunostaining for SYN1, HOMER1, PSD95, SHANK3; puncta quantified (manual + Volocity) on masked GFP/mKate2 neurons, blinded.
- Note: this is a **2D iPSC-derived monolayer neuron** model, not a 3D organoid/assembloid.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Foundational human-neuron disease-modeling reference** for the review's "synaptic disease modeling in human neurons" theme — one of the earliest demonstrations that a copy-number ASD/NDD disorder (22q13.3 / SHANK3) produces a measurable, reversible synaptic phenotype in patient iPSC-derived neurons.
- **SHANK3 / Phelan-McDermid entry** for the review's gene/disorder table: establishes SHANK3 haploinsufficiency → selective excitatory synaptic loss → E/I imbalance, the prototype "synaptopathy" framing the review can contrast with later 3D/organoid SHANK3 work.
- **Therapeutic angle (IGF-1).** Provides the mechanistic, human-cell rationale for IGF-1 as a candidate treatment in PMDS/ASD — restoring excitatory transmission by promoting PSD95-containing, SHANK3-independent mature synapses. Directly supports any "proactive"/therapeutic section of the review (genetic correction vs pharmacological rescue).
- **Methodological contrast for "perturbation in 3D":** demonstrates the single-gene, dish-based predecessor to pooled assembloid CRISPR screens (cf. [Meng 2023](meng_2023_assembloid_crispr_screens_reveal_impact_disease_genes.md)); useful for arguing why scalable 3D perturbation platforms are the next step.
- Lineage note: same Stanford (Pașca/Dolmetsch-adjacent) iPSC-neuron platform later extended to Timothy syndrome and to organoid/assembloid models; see [Pașca 2015](paca_2015_functional_cortical_neurons_astrocytes_human_pluripotent_stem.md).

## Open questions / limitations

- **2D monolayer neurons**, not organoids; lacks 3D cytoarchitecture, glial/vascular niche, and circuit-level maturation.
- Only **two patients (six lines)**; the ~1 Mb deletions remove 10–100 additional genes, so phenotypes are attributed to SHANK3 mainly by rescue rather than isogenic correction.
- Only partial overlap with mouse Shank3 models; discrepancy possibly due to multiple SHANK3 isoforms (patients lose the whole gene on one allele; mice often lose only some isoforms and are studied as homozygous KO).
- IGF-1 rescue is **mechanistically indirect** (it reduces SHANK3 and shifts synapses to PSD95-containing) and may reflect accelerated maturation rather than correction of the underlying deficit; long-term and in-vivo consequences are untested here.
- IGF-1 fully restored whole-cell NMDA but not AMPA receptor currents, and SHANK3 vs IGF1 act differently on AMPA vs NMDA receptors — the precise receptor-trafficking mechanism is unresolved.
