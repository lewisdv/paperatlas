# Human iPSC-Derived Cerebral Organoids Model Cellular Features of Lissencephaly and Reveal Prolonged Mitosis of Outer Radial Glia.

## Abstract

Classical lissencephaly is a genetic neurological disorder associated with mental retardation and intractable epilepsy, and Miller-Dieker syndrome (MDS) is the most severe form of the disease. In this study, to investigate the effects of MDS on human progenitor subtypes that control neuronal output and influence brain topology, we analyzed cerebral organoids derived from control and MDS-induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) using time-lapse imaging, immunostaining, and single-cell RNA sequencing. We saw a cell migration defect that was rescued when we corrected the MDS causative chromosomal deletion and severe apoptosis of the founder neuroepithelial stem cells, accompanied by increased horizontal cell divisions. We also identified a mitotic defect in outer radial glia, a progenitor subtype that is largely absent from lissencephalic rodents but critical for human neocortical expansion. Our study, therefore, deepens our understanding of MDS cellular pathogenesis and highlights the broad utility of cerebral organoids for modeling human neurodevelopmental disorders.

## Key findings

Disease model: Miller-Dieker syndrome (MDS), the most severe classical lissencephaly, caused by large heterozygous deletions of chromosome band **17p13.3** spanning dozens of genes, including **PAFAH1B1 (LIS1)** and **YWHAE (14-3-3ε)** (the two genes that delineate the minimal MDS critical deletion; they sit several Mb apart on 17p13.3). iPSCs were generated from **3 MDS individuals** (2 independent clones each, normal karyotype, deletion stably maintained) plus controls, via non-integrating episomal reprogramming. 3D cortical organoids made by the Kadoshima/Sasai (2013) re-aggregation method.

- **NESC apoptosis / reduced growth (5 wk).** Initial and day-3 aggregate sizes did not differ, but by ~2.5 weeks (day 18 ± 1) MDS organoids were significantly smaller. **~25% of MDS organoids were <0.5 mm circumference and disintegrated** before analysis; both MDS4 clones grew especially poorly and were dropped from most assays. MDS VZ-like regions showed markedly increased cleaved CASPASE-3+ apoptosis among SOX2+ cells (t test, p = 0.04); the proliferation marker Ki67 was not significantly altered. Contrast: in mouse, *Pafah1b1* heterozygosity gives no strong neuroepithelial phenotype (acute homozygous removal at E8–E8.5 is needed for NESC apoptosis), implying the human model is more sensitive to lissencephaly severity.
- **Horizontal cleavage planes (5 wk).** Mitotic spindle orientation was disrupted in MDS VZ. WT mean cleavage angle ≈ **65°** (mostly vertical/perpendicular to apical surface); MDS mean ≈ **46°**, significantly more horizontal (t test, p = 0.0158). 70–92 dividing cells analyzed from WT (n=2) and MDS (n=2) individuals. Apical determinants (aPKC, N-Cadherin, Pericentrin) generally localized normally, though MDS VZ was sometimes less organized / discontinuous.
- **Neuronal migration defect (5 wk), cell-autonomous and rescuable.** In a Matrigel outgrowth assay 3 days after attachment, **>55% of migrating WT neurons reached 100–800 µm** from the organoid edge, whereas **the majority (~65%) of MDS migrating neurons stayed within 100 µm**. Live imaging: WT neurons migrated saltatory at ~**20 µm/hr** with track straightness ≈ 1; MDS neurons often initiated but failed to maintain migration ("tumbling in place"), reducing average speed to ~**13 µm/hr** (p = 0.0004) and track straightness. MDS migrating nuclei were less elongated (reduced nucleokinesis tension).
- **Genetic rescue.** MDS1 carried the 17p13.3 deletion (~5.7 Mbp) in a ring chromosome; on reprogramming the ring was lost and replaced by a second WT chromosome 17 via compensatory uniparental isodisomy, restoring LIS1 and 14-3-3ε to WT levels (Bershteyn et al. 2014). MDS1-rescue organoid neurons were **indistinguishable from WT** in displacement, speed, and track straightness — confirming the migration defect is caused by the heterozygous deletion.
- **Cortical-explant co-culture (cell-autonomy).** Organoid neurons labeled with AAV1-CAG-tdTomato (~60% CTIP2+, ~25% SOX1+) migrated into GW18.5 human cortical slices: **~50% of WT** organoid-derived neurons reached the pial surface after 4 days vs **~10% of MDS** — supporting a cell-autonomous defect.
- **Lamination + deep-layer neuron overproduction (10 wk).** Organized VZ/SVZ/CP-like regions formed (typically 1–5 per organoid) in both genotypes. Broad laminar distribution was preserved (~60% of PAX6+ in VZ; ~55% of TBR2/EOMES+ IP cells in SVZ; ~70% of CTIP2+ neurons in CP). However MDS showed a slight-but-significant **decrease in PAX6+/TBR2– radial glia with a complementary increase in CTIP2+ deep-layer neurons** (two-way ANOVA + Bonferroni), plus more IP cells in the apical VZ (most pronounced in the more severe MDS3) — consistent with the earlier excess of horizontal divisions driving short-term neuronal overproduction.
- **oRG-like cells form in vitro and carry the oRG molecular signature.** By 10 wk ~30% of PAX6+ cells in organized structures sat in SVZ-like regions; unipolar SOX2+/TBR2– cells with a single basal process (oRG morphology) appeared at the organoid edge after 10–15 wk. scRNA-seq identified **95 cortical radial-glia-like cells**; the oRG signature (Pollen et al. 2015) emerged at week 10 and strengthened at week 15. oRG markers PTPRZ1 and tenascin-C (TNC) co-localized with SOX2, matching human OSVZ (GW17.3) staining.
- **oRG-specific prolonged mitosis (the Table-1 / headline lissencephaly phenotype).** Time-lapse of CMV-GFP–labeled 10-wk progenitors: WT oRG and vRG cells completed division within **~50 min** of rounding up. **MDS oRG cells translocated farther during mitotic somal translocation (MST; p = 0.05) and then remained in mitosis for prolonged periods (up to several hours) before cytokinesis (oRG division time p = 0.0028).** The delay was **specific to oRG** — vRG and IP divisions from the same sessions were unaffected — and phenocopied primary oRG treated with the microtubule depolymerizer nocodazole (Ostrem et al. 2014). Cleavage-angle relative to the basal process did not differ between genotypes (most oRG divisions perpendicular to the basal fiber). 20–50 cells per individual; WT n=3, MDS n=2.

## Methods

- **iPSC derivation:** episomal (non-integrating) reprogramming of fibroblasts from 3 MDS patients + controls; 2 clones/line vetted for pluripotency, normal karyotype, stable 17p13.3 deletion.
- **Cortical organoids:** Kadoshima/Sasai (2013) suspension re-aggregation of equal numbers of dissociated iPSCs in serum-free cortical differentiation medium; analyzed at 5, 10, and 15 weeks.
- **Live imaging:** organoid slice culture infected with CMV-GFP adenovirus (labels vRG, IP, oRG) or AAV1-CAG-tdTomato (neuron-biased); time-lapse to quantify cleavage angle, division duration, MST distance, migration speed/straightness.
- **Migration assays (2 novel):** (1) Matrigel attachment outgrowth with endpoint displacement + live tracking; (2) co-culture of tdTomato organoids against CMV-GFP–labeled GW18.5 human cortical slices to test cell-autonomy.
- **scRNA-seq:** single-cell capture from WT and MDS organoids at 5/10/15 wk (Fluidigm-style capture); outlier removal and enrichment for cortical-lineage cells; clustering to identify 95 radial-glia-like cells; oRG signature scoring vs Pollen et al. 2015 and primary tissue correlation networks (Pearson, thresholds 0.25 / 0.5). Data: dbGaP phs000989.v3.p1.
- **Immunostaining markers:** SOX1/SOX2/PAX6 (RG), TBR2/EOMES (IP), CTIP2 + DCX/TUJ1 (deep-layer neurons), p-Vimentin/p-Histone-H3 (mitosis), cleaved CASP3 (apoptosis), Ki67 (proliferation), PTPRZ1/TNC (oRG).
- **Statistics:** t tests for pairwise live-imaging/IF metrics; two-way ANOVA + Bonferroni for laminar distribution.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **This is the source for the review's Table-1 LIS1 / Miller-Dieker entry.** Concrete deliverable: a patient-iPSC cerebral-organoid model of a Mendelian cortical malformation (MDS lissencephaly, 17p13.3 / *PAFAH1B1*-LIS1 + *YWHAE*) that resolves **cell-type- and stage-specific defects** — NESC apoptosis, horizontal-cleavage spindle misorientation, cell-autonomous migration failure, deep-layer neuron overproduction, and an **oRG-specific prolonged mitosis**.
- **oRG mitosis is the headline theme.** The paper demonstrates that organoids generate bona-fide oRG-like cells (position, unipolar morphology, MST behavior, Pollen-2015 molecular signature) and that these cells carry a disease-specific mitotic delay absent from vRG/IP — directly supporting the review's emphasis on oRG mitosis as a human-specific, organoid-accessible vulnerability for cortical malformation. The migration defect was functionally **rescued** by restoring the WT chromosome, modeling the "programmable / proactive" genetic-correction logic of the manuscript.
- **Human-vs-mouse argument.** Because oRG cells are largely absent in lissencephalic rodents and *Pafah1b1* heterozygous mice are mild, the organoid recapitulates aspects of human disease that mouse models miss — reinforcing the review's case for human organoids over rodent models for ASD/neurodevelopmental genetics.
- Mechanistic hints relevant to convergence of malformation phenotypes: *Magoh* acts upstream of *Pafah1b1* (loss lowers LIS1), linking microcephaly and lissencephaly; the paper also flags high RG expression of the candidate Zika entry receptor AXL.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Which 17p13.3 gene(s) drive the oRG mitotic delay is unresolved.** LIS1/PAFAH1B1 controls mitotic microtubule dynamics and is a strong candidate, but the authors stress LIS1 haploinsufficiency *alone* cannot explain MDS severity (isolated *PAFAH1B1* deletions are milder and not microcephalic); additional deleted genes are likely involved. Not dissected here.
- **Progeny fate of the delayed oRG divisions was not directly observed** (technical limits of long-term organoid-slice imaging); apoptotic oRG-like cells were seen ("data not shown") but the predicted neuron-vs-progenitor / apoptosis fate shift (cf. Pilaz et al. 2016) is inferred, not measured.
- Small genetic n (3 MDS individuals; n=2 for several live-imaging metrics; MDS4 excluded for poor growth); organoid variability and absence of vasculature/microglia. Models only ~first-trimester corticogenesis — no cortical folding (which begins after GW23 in vivo).
- Single genetic rescue (MDS1 via fortuitous ring-chromosome / uniparental isodisomy); the two MDS-critical genes (*PAFAH1B1*, *YWHAE*) are megabases apart and were not individually corrected.
