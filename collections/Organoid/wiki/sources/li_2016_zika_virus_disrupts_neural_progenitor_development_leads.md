## Abstract

The link between Zika virus (ZIKV) infection and microcephaly has raised urgent global alarm. The historical African ZIKV MR766 was recently shown to infect cultured human neural precursor cells (NPCs), but unlike the contemporary ZIKV strains, it is not believed to cause microcephaly. Here we investigated whether the Asian ZIKV strain SZ01 could infect NPCs in vivo and affect brain development. We found that SZ01 replicates efficiently in embryonic mouse brain by directly targeting different neuronal linages. ZIKV infection leads to cell-cycle arrest, apoptosis, and inhibition of NPC differentiation, resulting in cortical thinning and microcephaly. Global gene expression analysis of infected brains reveals upregulation of candidate flavirus entry receptors and dysregulation of genes associated with immune response, apoptosis, and microcephaly. Our model provides evidence for a direct link between Zika virus infection and microcephaly, with potential for further exploration of the underlying mechanisms and management of ZIKV-related pathological effects during brain development.

## Key findings

First in vivo (mouse) evidence that a **contemporary Asian-lineage ZIKV** strain directly infects neural progenitors and causes microcephaly — the causal animal complement to the human-culture and organoid studies (Garcez 2016; Tang 2016; Qian 2016) published the same season.

- **Efficient replication in the embryonic brain.** ZIKV strain **SZ01** (Asian lineage, isolated from a patient infected in Samoa; GenBank KU866423; stock 6.5 × 10⁵ PFU/mL) was injected into one lateral ventricle (LV) of **E13.5 ICR mouse** embryos. A **~300-fold increase in viral RNA copies** was detected **3 days post-infection** (real-time RT-PCR of whole brain). Number of infected cells scaled with viral dose.
- **Tropism for the progenitor zones.** Most infected cells localized to the **ventricular and subventricular zones (VZ/SVZ)** — where NPCs reside — with far fewer in the midbrain or cortical plate (CP) during the early infection window. By **5 days** post-infection, however, **almost all cells including those in the CP were ZIKV-positive**, indicating post-mitotic neurons are also targeted (at lower efficiency than NPCs).
- **Microcephalic phenotype.** Infected brains were grossly **smaller** than mock-infected littermates by **5 days** post-infection, with **enlarged lateral ventricles** and a **thinner CP and VZ/SVZ**. Cortical-layer markers Tbr1 and Foxp2 (stained at E18.5) showed **thinning of cortical layers without apparent disruption of lamination** (Nissl: n = 12 slices across 2 control / 3 infected brains; Tbr1/Foxp2: control n = 5, infected n = 7 slices).
- **Cell death in neurons.** Activated caspase-3⁺ cells were many in the intermediate zone (IZ) and CP **3 days** post-infection (weaker in SVZ), and far more numerous and concentrated in the **CP at 5 days** ⇒ apoptosis occurs **mainly in immature and mature neurons**, not principally in the infected NPCs.
- **Cell-type identity of infected cells (marker panel shared with the corpus).** Infected cells were positive for **Sox2** and **Pax6** (apical progenitors / radial glia) and **Tbr2 / EOMES** (intermediate/basal progenitors, IPCs/BPCs) ⇒ NPCs/IPCs are the **main direct targets**; some infected cells were Tuj1⁺ or Dcx⁺ (immature neurons), likely differentiated from earlier-infected NPCs.
- **Cell-cycle arrest and blocked differentiation.** Infected E16.5 brains had **significantly fewer mitotic (phospho-histone-H3⁺) cells** in VZ/SVZ and abnormal centrosome positioning. BrdU pulse-chase: **more Pax6⁺ cells 1 h after BrdU** but **significantly fewer Tbr2⁺ cells 24 h after BrdU**, and an **increased Ki67⁺BrdU⁺ fraction** at 24 h ⇒ ZIKV **suppresses NPC proliferation, the Pax6⁺→Tbr2⁺ transition, and cell-cycle exit/differentiation**. Consistent with reported S-phase arrest of ZIKV-infected hiPSC-NPCs (Tang 2016).
- **Transcriptome (RNA-seq) of infected whole brain.** Whole E16.5 brains, 3 days post-infection (n = 2 per group; significance at p < 0.05 and |log2 fold-change| > 0.263):
  - **Up:** immune-response and apoptosis GO pathways, especially **cytokine production/response**; antiviral genes (**Tlr3, Ddx58, Ifih1, Oas2, Isg15, Mx1**, plus **Ccl5, Cxcl10, Ifnb1** absent in controls); candidate **flavivirus entry receptors, most notably AXL** (predicted ZIKV receptor; Nowakowski 2016).
  - **Down:** genes for cell proliferation, differentiation, migration, and organ development; and **microcephaly-associated genes** — confirmed downregulation of **ASPM, CASC5, CENPF, MCPH1, RBBP8, STIL, and TBR2** (overlapping the ZIKV-infected hNPC dataset of Tang 2016); CEP152 and WDR62 changes were not significant.
- **Authors' synthesis:** ZIKV directly infects multiple NPC lineages and immature neurons in vivo, attenuating NPC expansion via apoptosis + cell-cycle dysregulation + blocked differentiation — together likely accounting for ZIKV-associated microcephaly; cytokine-mediated indirect effects are also implicated.

## Methods

Experimental, **in vivo mouse model** of congenital ZIKV infection:

- **Virus / animals:** Asian-lineage ZIKV **SZ01** (GenBank KU866423; Deng et al. 2016), 6.5 × 10⁵ PFU/mL stock. **1 µL undiluted virus** (or 10-fold dilution, used only for the viral-load curve in Fig. 1A) injected into one side of the cerebroventricular space / LV of **E13.5 ICR mouse** embryos; mock = culture medium. Per dam, ~⅔ of littermates infected, ~⅓ mock-injected as internal controls. Brains inspected at **E16.5 and E18.5** (3 and 5 days post-infection).
- **Cell-cycle labeling:** BrdU 50 mg/kg i.p. to pregnant dams at **1 h or 24 h** before harvest.
- **Readouts:** real-time RT-PCR viral load (whole brain); immunofluorescence on cryosections (markers: **Sox2, Pax6, Tbr2, β-III-tubulin/Tuj1, Dcx, Tbr1, Foxp2, phospho-histone-H3, Ki67, BrdU, γ-tubulin/centrosome, activated caspase-3**); ZIKV detection using convalescent serum from a ZIKV patient (cross-validated against anti-ZIKV-envelope antibody, Fig. S1); **Nissl/toluidine-blue** for layer thickness; **RNA-seq** (whole E16.5 brain, 2 per group; Annoroad) with GO enrichment (Fisher's exact test).
- **Stats:** Student's unpaired t test; data mean ± SEM (RNA-seq panels mean ± SD). Procedures approved by the IACUC, Beijing Institute of Microbiology and Epidemiology.
- **Hard scope limit (stated by authors):** infected newborn pups were **eaten by their lactating mothers within 2 days** (likely because they were very sick), on two attempts — so **postnatal stages could not be analyzed**; all phenotyping is at embryonic stages.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **The in vivo causal anchor for the environmental/viral-insult arm.** Where Garcez 2016 (this batch) shows ZIKV impairs *human* 3D neural cultures correlationally, Li 2016 supplies the **in vivo proof** that a contemporary ZIKV strain *causes* microcephaly by hitting neural progenitors — closing the causal gap and validating the "environmental insult on the proliferative stage" premise of the "Programmable Brain Organoids for Proactive Autism Genetics" review.
- **Mechanistic resolution organoid assays can mirror.** It pinpoints *which* progenitor steps fail — **Pax6⁺ RG → Tbr2⁺ IPC transition**, mitotic entry (P-H3), and cell-cycle exit — using the **same SOX2/PAX6/TBR2 marker logic** as the developing-human-CNS reference (Silbereis 2016, this batch) and the organoid literature. This is the cell-biology dissection Garcez 2016 explicitly lacks, making the two papers a matched in-vitro/in-vivo pair.
- **Underwrites gene×environment modeling.** ZIKV downregulates a roster of **bona fide microcephaly genes (ASPM, MCPH1, STIL, CASC5, CENPF, RBBP8, TBR2)** — i.e., an environmental insult converging on the *same genes* whose mutation causes genetic microcephaly. That convergence is the conceptual core of programmable-organoid gene×environment screens: perturb a risk gene, add an environmental hit (here a virus), read the proliferative/differentiation phenotype.
- **AXL receptor lead.** Highlighting **AXL** as an induced candidate ZIKV entry receptor connects to the organoid/NSC entry-receptor work (Nowakowski 2016) and offers a manipulable node for organoid experiments.
- **Boundary condition for organoids.** The strong **immune/cytokine** transcriptional signature in vivo flags exactly what avascular, microglia-free organoids cannot capture — a useful caveat when the review argues for (and about the limits of) organoid environmental-insult models.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Mouse, not human; embryonic only.** Microcephaly was demonstrated in mouse embryos; pups did not survive (eaten by dams), so **postnatal/long-term outcomes and behavior are untested**. Human relevance still rests on parallel human-culture/organoid data.
- **Non-physiological route and dose.** Direct **intracerebroventricular injection** of a high-titer stock bypasses placental/blood-brain-barrier transmission and maternal immunity; dose-response and a lower-titer survival model are explicitly left for future work.
- **Small n for omics.** RNA-seq is **n = 2 per group** (whole-brain bulk), mixing many cell types and the host immune response; cell-type-specific transcriptomics is not resolved here.
- **Apoptosis vs. proliferation locus.** Death is concentrated in **neurons (CP/IZ)** while the proliferative defect is in **NPCs (VZ/SVZ)** — the relative contribution of each (and of indirect cytokine effects) to final cortical thinning is not fully disentangled.
- **Single strain (SZ01).** One Asian-lineage isolate; strain-, lineage-, and timing-dependence of the phenotype not mapped (the African MR766 contrast is cited, not tested here).
- **Correction note:** RNA-seq accession (PRJCA000267) was omitted from the original publication and added by erratum (Cell Stem Cell 19(5):672, Nov 3, 2016); no scientific results changed.
