# Oscillations in notch signaling regulate maintenance of neural progenitors.

## Source

- Authors: Shimojo H, Ohtsuka T, Kageyama R
- Journal: Neuron (2008)
- DOI: [10.1016/j.neuron.2008.02.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2008.02.014)
- PMID: [18400163](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18400163/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

Expression of the Notch effector gene Hes1 is required for maintenance of neural progenitors in the embryonic brain, but persistent and high levels of Hes1 expression inhibit proliferation and differentiation of these cells. Here, by using a real-time imaging method, we found that Hes1 expression dynamically oscillates in neural progenitors. Furthermore, sustained overexpression of Hes1 downregulates expression of proneural genes, Notch ligands, and cell cycle regulators, suggesting that their proper expression depends on Hes1 oscillation. Surprisingly, the proneural gene Neurogenin2 (Ngn2) and the Notch ligand Delta-like1 (Dll1) are also expressed in an oscillatory manner by neural progenitors, and inhibition of Notch signaling, a condition known to induce neuronal differentiation, leads to downregulation of Hes1 and sustained upregulation of Ngn2 and Dll1. These results suggest that Hes1 oscillation regulates Ngn2 and Dll1 oscillations, which in turn lead to maintenance of neural progenitors by mutual activation of Notch signaling.

## Key findings

Primary study (Neuron vol. 58, 52-64, 10 April 2008) using real-time bioluminescence imaging in mouse embryonic telencephalon. Central discovery: **the Notch effector Hes1 — and, unexpectedly, the proneural gene Ngn2 and Notch ligand Dll1 — oscillate within individual dividing neural progenitors**, and this oscillation (not sustained Hes1) is what maintains the progenitor pool.

**Hes1 is expressed at variable levels in cycling VZ progenitors**
- Hes1 protein is restricted to the VZ and to dividing cells: virtually all Hes1+ cells were **Ki67+** (S1).
- Cell-cycle mapping by BrdU pulse-chase at E14.5 (harvest 30 min / 90 min / 8 h / 14 h = S / S-G2 / early G1 / late G1; M-phase by anti-pH3): Hes1 protein present at high *and* low levels in S/G2; **absent in early G1** (cell bodies at ventricular surface); then re-expressed at variable levels in **~50% of BrdU+ cells** after early G1, absent in the other ~50% — consistent with asymmetric division producing one Hes1+ progenitor and one Hes1- (differentiating) daughter.

**Hes1 oscillates (~2-3 h period)**
- Real-time imaging used an **ubiquitinated firefly-luciferase reporter (pHes1-Ub1-Luc)** with reporter half-life ~10 min; necessary because **Hes1 mRNA and protein half-lives are ~20 min**.
- In dissociation cultures (E9.5-E14.5): **60-85% of progenitors lost Hes1 expression** soon after dissociation (Notch disrupted); the remaining **15-40% showed oscillatory Hes1** for several hours. Periods varied cycle-to-cycle and cell-to-cell.
- **Average Hes1 oscillation period = 2-3 h across E9.5-E14.5** (slight tendency to be longer at earlier stages; not significantly changed over development). Slice cultures (electroporation E13.5, imaged ~1 day later), which preserve Notch signaling, confirmed oscillation (~3 h period) in progenitors located in outer VZ / descending (late G1, S, G2); no Hes1 in cells ascending in early G1.

**Sustained high Hes1 represses proneural, Notch-ligand, and cell-cycle genes**
- Microarray of FACS-sorted GFP+ cells 18 h after pEF-Hes1 electroporation (E13.5): **40 genes repressed >2-fold** by sustained Hes1, including proneural **Mash1 (Ascl1), Math3 (Neurod4), Ngn2**; Notch ligands **Dll1, Jag1**; cell-cycle regulators **cyclin D1 (Ccnd1), cyclin E2 (Ccne2)**; plus Notch components Hes5, Manic fringe (Mfng) and G2-M arrest genes Gadd45g, Myt1, Tis21.
- In vivo confirmation (in situ + qPCR): sustained Hes1 downregulated cyclin D1, Ngn2, Dll1; inhibited BrdU uptake; caused **G1-phase retardation**. Implication: proper expression of these genes *requires* Hes1 to be oscillatory, not constant.

**Ngn2 and Dll1 also oscillate, driven by Hes1**
- Ngn2 protein and Dll1 mRNA are expressed at variable levels in dividing (BrdU+/Ki67+) progenitors, most prominently at **E10.5-E12.5**; fraction of Ngn2+ cells that were BrdU+ ≈ **39.0% ± 15.1% (E10.5)** and **38.9% ± 6.4% (E12.5)**; Dll1+ that were BrdU+ ≈ **46.0% ± 2.1% (E10.5)** and **42.0% ± 4.5% (E12.5)**; both reduced by E14.5 (more Ngn2+ cells are then postmitotic neurons).
- **Inverse correlation**: where Hes1 protein is high, Ngn2/Dll1 are low, and vice versa (E12.5 and E14.5). Graded Hes1 misexpression (varying pEF-Hes1 dose; also Tet-Off + doxycycline) dose-dependently lowered Ngn2/Dll1.
- Single-cell reporters **pNgn2-Ub1-Luc** and **pDll1-Ub1-Luc** (Ngn2/Dll1 mRNA half-lives <30 min): both oscillated in ~half of GFP+ cells (the **TuJ1-negative** progenitors) and were **persistent in the other half (TuJ1+ differentiating neurons)**. So oscillatory in progenitors, sustained in neurons.

**Oscillation depends on Notch and Jak2-Stat3; it enables mutual (lateral) Notch activation**
- **DAPT (γ-secretase inhibitor)** persistently repressed Hes1 and led to *sustained upregulation* of Ngn2 and Dll1 (the pro-differentiation state). **AG490 (Jak2-Stat3 inhibitor)** abolished Hes1 oscillation — Jak-Stat signaling also regulates the oscillator.
- Persistent intermediate/high Hes1 misexpression in some cells drove **premature neuronal differentiation (TuJ1+) of neighboring nontransfected cells** in the VZ, because Dll1 was kept repressed in the transfected cells and Notch stayed inactive in neighbors.
- **Model**: out-of-phase oscillation — high Hes1 ↔ low Ngn2/Dll1; ~1 h later low Hes1 ↔ high Ngn2/Dll1, and the transient Dll1 then activates Notch in neighboring progenitors, sustaining *their* Hes1 oscillation. Thus oscillatory (vs. sustained) Notch output keeps the progenitor pool proliferative without committing it. Sustained Hes1 instead characterizes slowly-dividing **compartment boundary cells** (e.g., isthmus, zona limitans); Id factors and Stat3-Socs3 dynamics are implicated in choosing sustained vs. oscillatory Hes1.

## Methods

- **System**: mouse embryonic dorsal telencephalon (E9.5-E14.5), analyzed in vivo and as dissociation + organotypic slice cultures.
- **Real-time single-cell imaging**: destabilized ubiquitinated-luciferase reporters under Hes1, Ngn2, or Dll1 promoters (pHes1/pNgn2/pDll1-Ub1-Luc; reporter t½ ~10 min) imaged on a cooled CCD (20-min exposures, 2×2 or 4×4 binning) on an inverted microscope at 37 °C/5% CO2. pEF-EGFP co-electroporated to mark/trace cells and visualize nuclear "elevator" (interkinetic) movement.
- **In utero electroporation** at E13.5 (gain-of-function: pEF-Hes1 at graded doses; Tet-Off Hes1; reporter introduction), harvest 18-24 h later.
- **Cell-cycle phase assignment** by timed BrdU pulse-chase (50 mg/g) plus anti-pH3 (M phase) and cell-body position relative to ventricular surface.
- **Readouts**: immunohistochemistry (Hes1, Ngn2, BrdU, Ki67, pH3, cyclin D1, TuJ1, GFP), in situ hybridization / FISH (Dll1, Ngn2, cyclin D1, Hes5, Mfng, etc.), high-density microarray and qRT-PCR on FACS-sorted GFP+ cells.
- **Pharmacology**: DAPT (Notch/γ-secretase blockade); AG490 (Jak2-Stat3 blockade).

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

Cited as the mechanistic basis for **how Notch signaling maintains the neural-progenitor pool** — directly supporting the manuscript's progenitor-maintenance section.
- Establishes that **progenitor maintenance is a dynamic, oscillatory property of Notch-Hes1**, not a static on-state. Lateral inhibition via oscillating Dll1↔Notch keeps neighboring radial-glia-like progenitors cycling while permitting individual cells to peel off and differentiate — the cellular logic that an organoid must reproduce to sustain a proliferative ventricular-zone-like compartment and generate neurons over time.
- Provides the **Hes1 ⊣ proneural (Ngn2/Ascl1) ⊣ cell-cycle (cyclin D1/E2)** wiring that downstream organoid/disease-modeling and CRISPR-screen work invokes when interpreting how progenitor genes tune the timing/amount of neurogenesis.
- Connects to the human-specific Notch theme central to the broader review: NOTCH2NL paralogs (Fiddes 2018, Suzuki 2018) act on exactly this Notch-Hes-Dll axis to expand human cortical progenitor pools/neurogenesis; Shimojo supplies the canonical (mouse) oscillatory mechanism those papers build on.
- Cited specifically for **progenitor maintenance** (per ingest brief).

## Open questions / limitations

- All data are **mouse embryonic telencephalon**; human/organoid generality (e.g., whether human outer radial glia or organoid progenitors show the same ~2-3 h Hes1 oscillation) is not tested here.
- Authors explicitly note they **could not measure whether Dll1 *protein* oscillates** (technically hard to quantify surface Dll1); if Dll1 protein is stable, mRNA oscillation may merely maintain a steady protein level rather than oscillating ligand presentation.
- Period varies considerably cycle-to-cycle and cell-to-cell; the functional meaning of stage-dependent period differences (longer at earlier, symmetric-division stages) is left open.
- The exact cell-cycle points at which Hes1 inhibits vs. permits cycling are not pinned down ("further analysis required").
- Mechanism selecting **oscillatory vs. sustained Hes1** (Jak-Stat / Stat3-Socs3 dynamics, Id factors at compartment boundaries) is proposed but not fully resolved.
