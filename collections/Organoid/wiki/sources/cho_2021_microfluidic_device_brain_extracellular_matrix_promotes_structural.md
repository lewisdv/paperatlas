# Microfluidic device with brain extracellular matrix promotes structural and functional maturation of human brain organoids.

## Source

- Authors: Cho AN, Jin Y, An Y, Kim J, Choi YS, Lee JS, Kim J, Choi WY et al.
- Journal: Nature communications (2021)
- DOI: [10.1038/s41467-021-24775-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-24775-5)
- PMID: [34354063](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34354063/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

Brain organoids derived from human pluripotent stem cells provide a highly valuable in vitro model to recapitulate human brain development and neurological diseases. However, the current systems for brain organoid culture require further improvement for the reliable production of high-quality organoids. Here, we demonstrate two engineering elements to improve human brain organoid culture, (1) a human brain extracellular matrix to provide brain-specific cues and (2) a microfluidic device with periodic flow to improve the survival and reduce the variability of organoids. A three-dimensional culture modified with brain extracellular matrix significantly enhanced neurogenesis in developing brain organoids from human induced pluripotent stem cells. Cortical layer development, volumetric augmentation, and electrophysiological function of human brain organoids were further improved in a reproducible manner by dynamic culture in microfluidic chamber devices. Our engineering concept of reconstituting brain-mimetic microenvironments facilitates the development of a reliable culture platform for brain organoids, enabling effective modeling and drug development for human brain diseases.

## Key findings

- **Two stacked engineering elements on top of a standard unguided protocol.** Baseline is the Lancaster cerebral-organoid protocol (iPSCs, no patterning small molecules). Added: (1) **brain extracellular matrix (BEM)** — decellularized human brain tissue — supplemented into the Matrigel embedding gel at an optimal **0.4 mg/ml** (higher doses, 1-2 mg/ml, were worse); and (2) a **pump-free microfluidic chamber device** driven by a bi-directional laboratory rocker (gravity/hydrostatic flow, low shear), mimicking cerebrospinal/interstitial fluid flow. BEM is added at the neuroepithelial stage (~day 11); organoids move to the device ~4 days later.
- **BEM is biochemically brain-like, not mechanically distinct from Matrigel.** Proteomics: BEM and human brain are ~2-5% matrisome (Matrigel ~90%) and contain collagens, proteoglycans (neurocan, versican, heparan sulfate) and glycoproteins (laminin, tenascin); 85/90 (94%) BEM ECM proteins are present in native brain, and 352 brain-enriched proteins are in BEM vs only 9 in Matrigel. Storage modulus at 1 Hz is similar (Mat 115.56 ± 5.55 Pa vs BEM 126.17 ± 13.52 Pa) — so effects are attributed to **biochemical**, not mechanical, cues. Batch-to-batch BEM composition was reproducible (top-10 iBAQ proteins overlapped across 3 batches).
- **BEM alone enhances neurogenesis, cortical organization and forebrain identity.** At d30 BEM organoids are larger (1.84 ± 0.35 vs 1.56 ± 0.44 mm diameter; p = 0.0349) with a maintained thick laminin basement membrane (sustained to d75, unlike Matrigel), more Tuj1+/MAP2+ neuronal area (p < 0.0001 / p = 0.0012), ~2x SOX2 upregulation, and more p-Vim+/PAX6+ radial glia. BEM yields thicker, better-organized cortical layers (TBR1+/CTIP2+ deep layers, TBR2+ IPCs, SATB2+ upper-layer, HOPX+ basal radial glia) and larger ventricle-like structures and volume at d75. Effect is **tissue-specific**: intestine/liver/heart ECM did not reproduce it (BEM largest, e.g. BEM 1723 ± 239 µm vs Mat 1385 ± 256 µm at d30).
- **The microfluidic device improves oxygen/nutrient delivery and sharply reduces death.** Glucose-diffusion simulations and intra-organoid O2-sensing nanoparticles confirm better core oxygenation in the device (BEM-plate phosphorescence >2x higher than BEM-device, i.e. plate organoids more hypoxic; HIF-1α+ area smaller in device at d120). Apoptosis (cleaved caspase-3) dropped from **12.7 ± 6.2% (BEM-plate) to 2.9 ± 2.2% (BEM-device)**; EthD-1 dead-cell area and necrotic core were reduced; proliferation (Ki67+, Nestin+) increased. Some device organoids reached **~8 mm by d60**.
- **The device further accelerates structural maturation and corticogenesis.** d60 volume: Mat-plate 0.14 ± 0.02 vs BEM-plate 0.31 ± 0.14 vs BEM-device **0.56 ± 0.17 mm3**; device organoids are less spherical (more folded/complex). Device organoids have more p-Vim+ mitotic RGCs, Reelin+ Cajal-Retzius cells, thicker TBR1+/CTIP2+/SATB2+ layers and clearer SOX2+/HOPX+ outer-SVZ separation from VZ, plus innate IBA1+/CD68+ microglia and later GFAP+ astrocytes.
- **Maturation markers appear much earlier than in conventional organoids.** GAD1/GABA/VGLUT1 by **d45** and SYNI/PSD95 by **d60** — versus the ~3-6 months typically reported — indicating accelerated excitatory/inhibitory neuron, synapse and glial development without exogenous neurotrophic factors.
- **Functional electrophysiology is improved in device organoids.** Larger glutamate-evoked Ca2+ influx and spontaneous Ca2+ spikes; larger Na+ currents; higher action-potential incidence (**33/39 device vs 19/27 plate** cells), more multi-spike firing, higher AP threshold (p = 0.0380); a postsynaptic current was detected only in device organoids (rare) — consistent with emerging synaptic networks.
- **The device reduces variability and is reproducible across lines.** Lower coefficient of variation in size and gene expression (NES, TUBB3, OLIG1, BCL2 up; BAX down), confirmed with a second iPSC line (KYOU-DXR0109B) and a 24-chamber scalable device. **Disease relevance:** differentially expressed genes in developing BEM and BEM-device organoids significantly overlapped with risk genes for **schizophrenia, autism, epilepsy/Parkinson's disease and stroke**.

## Methods

- **Organoids:** human iPSC-derived cerebral organoids (Lancaster protocol); EBs -> neuroectoderm; at ~d11 embedded in Matrigel ± human BEM (0.4 mg/ml optimal); ~d15 transferred to the microfluidic device or kept on orbital shaker/plate as control. Validated on a 2nd iPSC line and with porcine BEM (pBEM) as a standardization alternative.
- **BEM preparation:** human (and porcine) brain tissue decellularized with non-ionic detergent; characterized by GAG assay, H&E/Masson's trichrome, and LC-MS/MS proteomics (matrisome/Human Protein Atlas comparison).
- **Microfluidic device:** 3-layer, 5-chamber (2 organoid + 3 reservoir) pump-free chip; bi-directional gravity-driven flow via a rocker; design (y5-z3 channel) chosen by COMSOL-type glucose-transfer simulation; scalable 24-chamber version for parallel culture.
- **Readouts:** brightfield/H&E size; IHC for progenitor, radial-glia, layer, synaptic, glial and microglial markers; light-sheet + CUBIC tissue-clearing 3D morphometry (volume, sphericity, ventricle thickness via IMARIS); qPCR panels; bulk RNA-seq (Pearson correlation to fetal/adult human brain regions, GO/GSEA, disease-risk-gene overlap); intra-organoid O2-sensing PtTFPP-PUAN nanoparticles, HIF-1α IHC, glucose/lactate metabolite assays; Fluo-4 AM calcium imaging and whole-cell patch-clamp electrophysiology.
- **Statistics:** unpaired two-sided t-tests; data as mean ± SD; multiple independent replicates per panel (typically 3-5).

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Primary citation for the "organoid-on-chip / microfluidic perfusion (ECM)" advanced-platform theme.** This paper is the cleanest single source combining *both* halves of that theme — a brain-specific ECM (addressing the non-neural-matrix limitation of Matrigel) *and* pump-free microfluidic perfusion (addressing the diffusion/necrosis bottleneck) — with quantified survival, maturation and reproducibility gains.
- **Directly supports the review's reproducibility/quality-control framing.** A recurring criticism of organoids (echoed by [Eichmüller 2022](eichmller_2022_human_cerebral_organoids_new_tool_clinical_neurology.md), [Bhaduri 2020](bhaduri_2020_cell_stress_in_cortical.md)) is batch-to-batch variability and stress/necrosis; the device's lower CV and reduced apoptosis are concrete evidence that engineered microenvironments improve consistency for disease modeling and drug screening.
- **Explicit ASD hook:** the authors report that genes dysregulated in BEM/BEM-device organoids overlap significantly with **autism (and schizophrenia, epilepsy) risk genes**, positioning the platform as disease-modeling-ready — useful for the review's argument that better platforms make genotype-phenotype studies tractable.
- **Accelerated, factor-free maturation (synapses/E-I neurons by d45-60)** strengthens the gap-filling narrative: ASD-relevant synaptic and excitatory/inhibitory phenotypes can be reached faster and more reproducibly than in conventional culture.
- **Complementary to the corpus's other platform papers:** the authors benchmark against ALI-COs ([Giandomenico 2019](giandomenico_2019_cerebral_organoids_at_air_liquid_interface_generate.md)), vascularized/transcription-factor-induced organoids, and spinning bioreactors — letting the review present a menu of survival/maturation strategies (slice ALI vs perfusion-on-chip vs vascularization).

## Open questions / limitations

- **Unguided whole-brain organoids carry intrinsic regional variability;** while the device lowers CV, the platform relies on spontaneous self-organization (no patterning factors), so regional identity is mixed and not directly controllable.
- **BEM is sourced from human (or porcine) brain tissue,** raising supply, donor-variability and standardization concerns; the authors propose porcine BEM and stage-matched fetal/adult BEM as future refinements but do not resolve sourcing at scale.
- **BEM is transient** (retained ~60-70 days), so late benefits are attributed to early signaling rather than sustained matrix presence — the long-term sufficiency of an early ECM pulse is inferred, not mechanistically dissected.
- **Cortical lamination is improved but still not a true six-layer cortex;** layer markers are present and thicker but precise in-vivo spatial organization is not fully recapitulated (a general organoid limitation the paper does not overcome).
- **Disease relevance is shown only as risk-gene-expression overlap,** not as a modeled ASD/disease phenotype; no patient-derived or genotype-specific organoids are tested here.
- **Functional read-outs are modest** (rare postsynaptic currents; small patch-clamp n), and the system still lacks true perfused vasculature — the device improves but does not eliminate diffusion limits in the largest organoids.
