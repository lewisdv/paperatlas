# The genetics of early telencephalon patterning: some assembly required.

- DOI: [10.1038/nrn2463](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn2463)

## Abstract

The immense range of human behaviours is rooted in the complex neural networks of the cerebrum. The creation of these networks depends on the precise integration of specific neuronal subtypes that are born in different regions of the telencephalon. Here, using the mouse as a model system, we review how these proliferative zones are established. Moreover, we discuss how these regions can be traced back in development to the function of a few key genes, including those that encode fibroblast growth factors (FGFs), sonic hedgehog (SHH), bone morphogenetic proteins (BMPs), forkhead box G1 (FOXG1), paired box 6 (PAX6) and LIM homeobox protein 2 (LHX2), that pattern the early telencephalon.

> **Type note:** This is a **mouse-genetics review** (~E8.5–E11), not an organoid paper. It is ingested as the patterning-gradient *background* citation that grounds how guided brain organoids are regionalized.

## Key findings

Synthesizes how a small set of morphogens and transcription factors carve the early telencephalon (mouse, ~E8.5→E11) into four progenitor domains: **dorsal cortex (pallium)**, **dorsal midline (cortical hem / choroid plexus)**, and the **ventral ganglionic eminences (MGE, LGE, CGE)**.

- **Dorsoventral axis = SHH vs GLI3 antagonism.** GLI3 (zinc-finger TF; "extra-toes" mutant) is initially broad and dorsalizing; SHH (ventral-midline morphogen) restricts GLI3's repressor activity. In *Shh⁻/⁻* the telencephalon shrinks and ventral cell types are lost, but ventral patterning is **rescued in *Shh⁻/⁻;Gli3⁻/⁻* double mutants** → SHH acts mostly **indirectly**, by relieving GLI3 repression, rather than by directly instructing ventral fate. In the telencephalon SHH works largely through **GLI repressors** (vs GLI activators in spinal cord).
- **FOXG1 maintains the telencephalon and drives ventral identity via FGFs.** *Foxg1* marks the telencephalic primordium from ~E8.5; *Foxg1⁻/⁻* loses ventral cell types and (unlike *Shh⁻/⁻*) is **not** rescued by removing *Gli3* — the *Foxg1⁻/⁻;Gli3⁻/⁻* double mutant **loses the telencephalon entirely**. FOXG1 induces **FGF8**; FGF8 can reciprocally induce *Foxg1* (FGF↔FOXG1 positive-feedback loop).
- **FGFs are the obligate positive effectors (and the telencephalic organizer).** FGF signaling acts **dose-dependently**: *Fgfr1⁻/⁻* loses MGE-derived (LHX6/LHX7, NKX2.1) ventromedial types; *Fgfr1⁻/⁻;Fgfr2⁻/⁻* (or null *Fgf8*) loses all ventral precursors (NKX2.1, GSH2) and progressively the neocortex; loss of all three receptors (Fgfr1/2/3) is predicted to delete both ventral telencephalon and cortex. FGFs emanate from the **anterior neural ridge** and behave as a Spemann-type organizer (mirror-image whisker-barrel duplications). FGFs operate **downstream of SHH** (SHH maintains Fgf3/8/15/17/18), and are described as both **mitogens and morphogens**.
- **FGF gradients set cortical areal identity.** Partial FGF reduction shifts cortical TF gradients anteriorly, expanding posterior (visual/sensory) areas at the expense of anterior (e.g. prelimbic) areas.
- **PAX6 vs GSH2 define the pallial–subpallial boundary.** PAX6 ("small-eye" mutant) is expressed dorsal-high→ventral-low; GSH2 the reverse; their **mutual antagonism** positions the boundary (*Pax6⁻/⁻* → cortex becomes dorsal-LGE; *Gsh2⁻/⁻* → reverse; partially rescued in the *Pax6⁻/⁻;Gsh2⁻/⁻* double mutant). PAX6/GLI3 also cooperate to promote dorsal fate (loss of Pax6, like loss of Gli3, partially rescues *Shh⁻/⁻*).
- **EMX2 ↔ PAX6 countergradients pattern the cortical sheet.** EMX2 lies downstream of GLI3; EMX2 and PAX6 act antagonistically in opposing gradients to assign neocortical **areal** identity (codified later as the "cooperative-concentration" model).
- **Dorsal midline via BMP + WNT.** The dorsomedial midline (choroid plexus, cortical hem) is specified by **BMPs** and **WNTs**: BMP beads induce midline features and repress *Foxg1*/proliferation; *Bmpr1a⁻/⁻;Bmpr1b⁻/⁻* loses choroid plexus + cortical hem. The **cortical hem is the hippocampal organizer**, and **WNT3A** from the hem is required for hippocampal development (no WNT3A → no hippocampus). BMP/WNT effectors bind upstream of *Emx2* to set its graded expression; EMX1/EMX2 (GLI3-dependent) are then required for the hippocampus.
- **LHX2 is the cortical selector gene.** LHX2 (LIM-homeodomain) cell-autonomously specifies cortical (neocortical + hippocampal) fate and **suppresses hem/antihem** fates; *Lhx2⁻/⁻* loses the cortex to an expanded choroid plexus + cortical hem. Neocortex formation requires **both** EMX-gene and PAX6 expression.
- **Integrated model (Fig. 4):** SHH ⊣ GLI3 → (with FOXG1) → FGF expression → all telencephalic regions **except** the BMP/WNT-specified dorsomedial midline; downstream GSH2/NKX2.1 subdivide ventral domains, while GLI3→BMP/WNT→EMX, plus PAX6 and LHX2, subdivide the dorsal telencephalon.

## Methods

Narrative review of mouse (and some zebrafish) developmental genetics. Evidence base = loss-of-function single/compound mutants (e.g. *Shh*, *Gli3*, *Foxg1*, *Fgf8*, *Fgfr1/2/3*, *Pax6*, *Gsh2*, *Emx1/2*, *Bmpr1a/1b*, *Lhx2*, *Nkx2.1*), bead-implantation morphogen assays, explant cultures, and chimaera/mosaic selector-gene experiments. No new experimental data. Scope deliberately restricted to **after** telencephalic specification, ~E8.5–E11; anterior–posterior neuraxis specification is covered only by reference. Authors caution that alternative interpretations exist and that human-specific events are inferred from mouse.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Patterning-gradient background for guided organoid regionalization.** This is the review's reference framework for *why and how* guided/patterned brain organoids use exogenous morphogens to obtain regional identity. The same molecules that pattern the embryonic telencephalon are the knobs used in protocols: **WNT activation (WNT3A/CHIR/GSK3i) + SHH/dorsomedial cues** to bias dorsal cortex vs ventral (GE/interneuron) fate, with **FGF/BMP** shaping area/midline identity.
- **Source of the WNT/SHH (and FGF/BMP) citations** the review needs when stating that organoid dorsal-vs-ventral patterning recapitulates in-vivo SHH–GLI3 dorsoventral logic and BMP/WNT dorsomedial specification. Directly underpins the patterning step in dorsal-forebrain protocols such as [Kang 2021](kang_2021_human_forebrain_organoid_model_fragile_x_syndrome.md) (WNT-3A + CHIR99021) and the excitatory-cortex differentiation in [St George-Hyslop 2023](st_2023_loss_cntnap2_alters_human_cortical_excitatory_neuron.md).
- **Explains organoid E/I-balance geography.** Glutamatergic pyramidal neurons arise dorsally (cortical VZ) while GABAergic interneurons arise in the **ventral GEs (MGE→SST/PV, CGE→CR/VIP, LGE→olfactory/projection)** and migrate dorsally — the developmental basis for why **assembloids** (dorsal+ventral fusion) are needed to reconstruct interneuron migration and E/I balance in ASD models (cf. [Birey 2017](birey_2017_assembly_functionally_integrated_human_forebrain_spheroids.md), [Birey 2022](birey_2022_dissecting_molecular_basis_human_interneuron_migration_forebrain.md)).
- **Links patterning TFs to disease genes in the review.** FOXG1 (Rett-variant/ASD; cf. [Mariani 2015](mariani_2015_foxg1_dependent_dysregulation_gaba_glutamate_neuron_differentiation.md)), PAX6, LHX2, and the SHH/WNT/BMP axes are recurrent in neurodevelopmental disorders — this review supplies the canonical wild-type roles against which organoid disease phenotypes are interpreted. Complements the MeCP2/telencephalon-development organoid work ([Tenreiro 2025](tenreiro_2025_mecp2_regulates_telencephalic_development_human_cerebral_organoids.md)).

## Open questions / limitations

- **Mouse model; human-specific patterning inferred.** Quantitative gradient parameters and human-specific features (e.g. expanded oSVZ/oRG biology) are outside scope — the review explicitly extrapolates to human telencephalon.
- **Restricted developmental window** (~E8.5–E11, post-specification): earlier AP neuraxis specification and later neurogenesis/lamination/circuit refinement are not covered.
- **Morphogen logic is largely indirect/combinatorial** (e.g. SHH via GLI3 de-repression; FGF compensation across ligands), so single-factor manipulations in organoids may not map cleanly onto in-vivo roles.
- **Pre-2008 vintage.** Predates single-cell atlases and most human-organoid patterning work; gaps the authors flag (how factors regulate each other, signal integration over time, additional WNT/BMP roles) have since been partly addressed by atlas/organoid studies in this corpus.
- Not an organoid protocol — provides conceptual grounding only; no culture parameters.
