# Heart-forming organoids

## Current role in this corpus

Heart-forming organoids appear here as the developmental cardiac baseline for multicompartment heart models that later expand toward hematoendothelial richness, imaging-heavy preparation, or immune-bearing disease assays.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, heart-forming organoids are the main cardiac baseline when the goal is not isolated cardiomyocyte production but a broader developmental window that can later accept vascular, hematopoietic, imaging, or immune-assay extensions.
- Drakhlis 2021 is the developmental anchor, Dardano 2025 extends the same branch into blood-generating and imaging-preparation territory, and the heart macrophage assembloid shows how later disease assays still depend on a multicellular cardiac baseline.
- This entity therefore sits below more specific cardiac assay layers: first establish the heart-forming multicompartment substrate, then decide whether the next step is hematoendothelial extension, advanced imaging, or inflammatory rhythm testing.

## Main tension

- richer cardiac co-development versus greater protocol burden and variability
- developmental morphogenesis versus later assay specialization
