# Whole-mount 3D clearing and imaging

## Current role in this corpus

Whole-mount 3D clearing and imaging appears here as the main architecture-preserving imaging workflow for organoids when intact volumetric structure matters more than section-based snapshots.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, whole-mount imaging is the readout branch to choose when the relevant biology depends on preserved 3D organization, lumen geometry, cellular arrangement, or intact reporter architecture.
- Dekkers 2019 is the clearest imaging-first anchor, while Bakker 2022 shows how the same preparation-heavy mindset can branch into spatial molecular readouts rather than purely fluorescence architecture.
- This entity therefore marks a preparation-and-readout workflow rather than one modality: first preserve and expose intact 3D structure, then choose whether the downstream layer is fluorescence, spatial chemistry, or another volumetric assay.

## Main tension

- preserved 3D context versus heavier sample preparation and analysis burden
- rich volumetric architecture versus lower throughput than simpler section-based imaging
