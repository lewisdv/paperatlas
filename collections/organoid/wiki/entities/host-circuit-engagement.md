## Current role in this corpus

Host circuit engagement is the host-defined neural validation endpoint that appears when dish electrophysiology is no longer enough to answer the question.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, host circuit engagement is not a generic sign of "better maturation." It is a claim-specific escalation used when the endpoint only exists inside a host neural network.
- Kelley is the anchor because it cleanly separates dish-readout logic from host-connected function.
- Host circuit engagement therefore acts as a stop sign against premature animal work: use it only when the biological question truly depends on host participation.

## Main tension

- strongest host-facing validation versus loss of clean attribution to organoid-intrinsic biology
- neural realism versus logistical burden and recipient variability
