# Mesodermal progenitor cell mixing

## Current role in this corpus

Mesodermal progenitor cell mixing appears here as the most modular vascular-support assembly move: instead of engineering fate inside the host organoid, the vascular and support compartment is co-built by adding a mesodermal progenitor branch.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, mesodermal progenitor cell mixing is the reusable multicompartment route for adding vasculature plus mural or microglia-like support across host contexts.
- Worsdorfer 2019 is the key anchor because it shows that one added progenitor branch can populate more than endothelium alone.
- This entity therefore sits between stand-alone vessel baselines and full host transplantation: it is chosen when the goal is to raise tissue complexity inside the dish without committing to a single organ-specific engineering trick.

## Main tension

- reusable multicompartment gain versus weaker control over final composition
- broader support-cell recovery versus noisier specification and attribution
