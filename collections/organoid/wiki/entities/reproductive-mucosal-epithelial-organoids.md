# Reproductive mucosal epithelial organoids

## Current role in this corpus

Reproductive mucosal epithelial organoids appear here as the adult-tissue platform branch for cervical, endometrial, vaginal, and related reproductive-tract epithelial questions where niche state, infection exposure, or stromal context matters.

## Working synthesis

- In this corpus, reproductive mucosal organoids are the adult-epithelial branch for questions where tissue identity is shaped by adjacent mucosal niches, infection susceptibility, hormone-sensitive context, or added stromal or immune partners.
- Gurumurthy 2022 anchors niche divergence and infection-ready cervical baselines, JA 2024 adds stromal support as the first coculture layer, and the vaginal TRM protocol shows how the same branch can move into immune-education assays.
- This entity therefore sits between general adult epithelial platforms and more specific assay moves: first establish the reproductive mucosal epithelial baseline, then decide whether the next missing layer is stroma, immune cells, pathogen exposure, or endocrine context.

## Main tension

- donor-grounded epithelial specificity versus missing stromal, immune, or endocrine compartments
- niche-sensitive biology versus operational robustness
