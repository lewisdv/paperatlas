# Adult tissue-derived epithelial organoids

## Current role in this corpus

Adult tissue-derived epithelial organoids appear here as the donor-grounded baseline for organoid work that starts from mature tissue rather than from pluripotent developmental differentiation.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, adult tissue-derived epithelial organoids are the practical platform branch for preserving donor-grounded epithelial identity without going through an embryonic differentiation route.
- Broutier 2016 anchors the expandable adult endoderm baseline, Tysoe 2019 pushes that logic toward mature cholangiocyte and reconstructive use, Sheridan 2020 extends it into placental trophoblast biology, and R 2026 adds real-world surgical biobanking plus disease-stratified liver capture.
- This entity therefore sits beside, not underneath, patient-derived cancer PDOs: it is the branch to choose when mature tissue state and expandable epithelial access matter more than developmental emergence or tumor-specific translational escalation.

## Main tension

- donor-grounded epithelial specificity versus limited multicompartment tissue context
- practical expandability versus loss of richer native niche structure
