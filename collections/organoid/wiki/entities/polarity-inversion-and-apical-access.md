# Polarity inversion and apical access

## Current role in this corpus

Polarity inversion appears here as the lightest access-correction move for epithelial organoids when the main problem is that the apical surface is physically hidden.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, polarity inversion is not a generic engineering flourish. It is a geometry correction step used when the baseline epithelial organoid is already adequate but the readout surface is facing the wrong way.
- Its main payoff is speed and simplicity: it can unlock apical exposure questions without forcing a chip device, transplantation branch, or a more radical model switch.
- It therefore sits earlier than organ-on-chip in the access ladder: first ask whether the question is only "open the surface," and only escalate further if durable compartment separation or repeated sampling is required.

## Main tension

- fast geometry correction versus the limited control of longer-lived compartment architectures
- lighter assay burden versus weaker suitability for repeated permeability or sampling workflows
