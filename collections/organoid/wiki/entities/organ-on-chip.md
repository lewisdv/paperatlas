# Organ-on-chip

## Current role in this corpus

Organ-on-chip appears here as a device-layer move that converts a baseline organoid into a more accessible, polarized, and assay-compatible system.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, organ-on-chip is not just miniaturization. It is a way to fix access geometry, perfusion, or compartment separation when closed 3D organoids block the assay.
- The intestinal chip protocol is the clearest access-oriented example, while Homan-style perfused kidney culture shows the tissue-support version of the same move.
- Organ-on-chip therefore belongs between baseline culture and richer assay claims: first make the biology experimentally reachable, then ask more specific barrier, infection, secretion, or perfusion questions.

## Main tension

- better access and assay control versus extra device burden and chip-to-chip failure modes
- engineered geometry versus the self-organized richness of closed 3D organoids
