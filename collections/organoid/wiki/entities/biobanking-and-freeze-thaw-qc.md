# Biobanking and freeze-thaw QC

## Current role in this corpus

Biobanking and freeze-thaw QC appear here as operational workflow gates that determine whether a donor-derived organoid becomes a reusable translational platform or stays a one-off derivation.

## Working synthesis

- In this collection, banking is not a late convenience feature. It is one of the shortest tests of whether a PDO workflow is operationally stable enough for translational screening.
- Freeze-thaw recovery matters because it turns a successful derivation into a reusable asset: replicate alignment, retrospective re-testing, and programmatic screening all depend on it.
- This makes biobanking one of the clearest bridges between specimen logistics and assay readiness in the patient-derived branch.

## Main tension

- reusable translational infrastructure versus extra QC burden and failure points
- operational readiness versus the temptation to overinterpret fresh one-off outgrowths as stable platforms
