# Isolation and propagation of primary human cholangiocyte organoids for the generation of bioengineered biliary tissue

## Source

- PDF: [raw/sources/tysoe_2019_isolation_and_propagation_of_primary.pdf](../../raw/sources/tysoe_2019_isolation_and_propagation_of_primary.pdf)
- Article: [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-019-0168-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-019-0168-0)
- Status: deep ingested 2026-04-22
- Organ focus: adult human cholangiocyte organoids derived from the biliary tree and extended into transplantable scaffolded tissue
- Protocol focus: derive mature cholangiocyte organoids from primary tissue, expand them without losing adult biliary character, and seed them onto PGA or densified-collagen constructs

## Study design

- Starting material: primary human biliary tissue, initially optimized for common bile duct samples and later extended to gallbladder, ERCP brushings, and liver biopsies
- Protocol stages:
  - isolate primary cholangiocytes from fresh biliary tissue or minimally invasive clinical samples
  - embed the cells in Matrigel and culture them with EGF, R-spondin-1, and DKK-1 to favor long-term expansion of mature cholangiocytes rather than canonical-WNT-driven adult stem cell amplification
  - establish robust cholangiocyte organoid lines within about 2 weeks, then passage them roughly every 5 days at around 1:4 to 1:6 for expansion
  - prepare bioengineering scaffolds using either flat PGA matrices or densified collagen constructs, including tubular collagen geometries for bile-duct replacement
  - seed scaffold surfaces with small cholangiocyte organoid clumps rather than fully dissociated single cells, then culture the constructs for about 4 weeks before functional testing or transplantation
- Key validation: the derived organoids are reported as greater than 95% CK7-positive and CK19-positive cholangiocytes, show Sox9 and GGT expression, retain ALP and GGT activity plus bile-acid transport behavior, and form scaffolded tissue that can retain biliary markers and function after transplantation
- Distinct protocol emphasis: this is not just an adult biliary organoid paper, but a regenerative-medicine workflow that explicitly connects primary organoid derivation to tissue-engineered graft fabrication

## Key findings

- Uses the combination of EGF, R-spondin-1, and DKK-1 to preserve a mature cholangiocyte state instead of drifting toward a generic canonical-WNT stem-cell program.
- Shows that access to biliary organoids need not depend only on surgically excised common bile duct tissue, because gallbladder, ERCP, and liver-biopsy sources can also seed the platform.
- Identifies a concrete engineering rule that matters in practice: small cell clumps seed scaffolds better than single cells because aggressive dissociation damages attachment competence.
- Extends the organoids into PGA and densified-collagen grafts that can be shaped into flat or tubular constructs for biliary reconstruction.

## Distinctive contribution in this corpus

- One of the strongest adult-derived epithelial platform papers in the collection for explicitly bridging organoid biology and tissue engineering.
- Gives the corpus a mature cholangiocyte system that is faster and more directly translational than pluripotent cholangiocyte differentiation when the question is adult biliary physiology or repair.
- Provides a rare example where organoids are not just expanded for assay use but built into a reconstructive graft concept.

## Limitations and caveats

- Tissue access and tissue quality remain central bottlenecks, especially for fresh biliary material.
- Scaffold attachment can vary by line, so the paper's solution is often to extend culture time until confluent coverage is reached rather than expecting identical kinetics across donors.
- This is a highly epithelial platform and is not the right model for bile-duct development or multicompartment liver biology.
- The downstream scaffold and transplantation steps require significant bioengineering competence beyond standard organoid culture.

## Relevance to corpus

- Strengthens the adult-platform and gastrointestinal-endoderm branches with a mature biliary comparator.
- Useful when the project needs donor-derived adult cholangiocytes, regenerative-bile-duct engineering, or scaffold-compatible epithelial organoids.
- Helps distinguish organoids used for replacement and reconstruction from organoids used only for disease modeling or drug screens.
