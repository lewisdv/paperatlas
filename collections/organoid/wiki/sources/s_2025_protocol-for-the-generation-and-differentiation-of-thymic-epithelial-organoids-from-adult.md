# Protocol for the generation and differentiation of thymic epithelial organoids from adult mouse thymus tissue.

## Source

- PDF: [raw/sources/s_2025_protocol-for-the-generation-and-differentiation-of-thymic-epithelial-organoids-from-adult.pdf](../../raw/sources/s_2025_protocol-for-the-generation-and-differentiation-of-thymic-epithelial-organoids-from-adult.pdf)
- Article: [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12182356/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12182356/)
- Status: deep ingested 2026-04-22
- Organ focus: adult mouse thymic epithelial organoids expanded in 3D and differentiated toward cortical and medullary TEC states
- Protocol focus: immediate post-isolation adult thymus processing, long-term TEC organoid expansion, and staged cTEC or mTEC differentiation

## Study design

- Starting material: adult stem or progenitor cells from primary tissue
- Protocol stages:
  - isolate thymic tissue from 6- to 12-week-old adult mice and process it immediately by careful mechanical mincing rather than enzymatic digestion
  - embed the dissociated fragments in BME droplets with expansion medium that includes ROCK inhibition during the early plating period
  - keep plates inverted during initial polymerization so cells do not settle into an unwanted 2D layer
  - allow visible TEC organoids to emerge by about day 10 to day 14, then collect and re-plate the well-formed 3D structures to enrich epithelial organoids over contaminating thymocytes and debris
  - expand the established cultures by passaging approximately every 10 to 14 days, then switch to differentiation medium and finally DM+RR to drive cystic cTEC or mTEC-like states
- Key validation: the protocol expects cystic "sphere-with-lumen" conversion within roughly 3 to 5 days in differentiation medium, and by day 5 in DM+RR it recommends confirming markers such as Aire, Fezf2, H2-Aa, Psmb11, and Cxcl12 by qPCR or flow cytometry
- Distinct protocol emphasis: this is an adult epithelial-immune-organ support platform whose value lies in long-term TEC maintenance and subtype differentiation, not in complete thymus reconstruction

## Key findings

- Shows that adult thymic epithelium can be brought into a long-term expandable organoid format without first passing through pluripotent differentiation.
- Makes morphology an operational QC metric: expansion-phase organoids should become grape-like, whereas differentiation-phase organoids should become sphere-with-lumen structures before progression to the next stage.
- Documents practical failure points that matter in everyday culture, including 2D attachment, residual BME trapping organoids during recovery, and overcrowding inside dense droplets.
- Provides a relatively direct route to cTEC and mTEC-like states in a system that is easier to expand than primary TEC preparations.

## Distinctive contribution in this corpus

- One of the clearest adult-platform papers in the collection for a non-classic epithelial tissue that is tightly linked to immune development.
- Broadens the adult-platform concept page beyond liver, intestine, prostate, and cancer systems into thymic epithelium.
- Gives the corpus a practical starting point for TEC biology before adding thymocytes, stromal partners, or in vivo validation.

## Limitations and caveats

- The system lacks immune cells, vasculature, and stromal support, so it is not a full thymic microenvironment model.
- Even after differentiation, cTEC and mTEC states may remain functionally incomplete, with lower MHC class II than primary TECs explicitly noted as a concern.
- Residual expansion medium and residual BME can block proper differentiation, so washing quality matters.
- The protocol is not yet optimized to selectively enrich thymic epithelial stem cells.

## Relevance to corpus

- Strengthens the adult-platform branch with an epithelial organoid system that sits close to immune education and thymus biology.
- Useful when the question depends on TEC maintenance, thymic epithelial differentiation, or thymus-aging and dysfunction rather than on building a whole thymus analog from hPSCs.
- Serves as an adult-tissue counterpart to the collection's more composite epithelial-immune organoid systems.

## Open questions

- Which added partners, such as thymocytes or stroma, are most necessary before the organoids become functionally informative for selection biology?
- How can the protocol be tuned to increase functional maturation of cTEC and mTEC states rather than only their marker expression?
- What fraction of the expandable cells in this system are true long-term TEC progenitors versus transiently proliferative epithelial intermediates?
