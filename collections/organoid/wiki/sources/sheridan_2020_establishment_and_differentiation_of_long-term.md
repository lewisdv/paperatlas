# Establishment and differentiation of long-term trophoblast organoid cultures from the human placenta

## Source

- PDF: [raw/sources/sheridan_2020_establishment_and_differentiation_of_long-term.pdf](../../raw/sources/sheridan_2020_establishment_and_differentiation_of_long-term.pdf)
- Article: [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-020-0381-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41596-020-0381-x)
- Status: deep ingested 2026-04-22
- Organ focus: first-trimester human placenta trophoblast organoids that retain villous architecture and can be pushed into invasive EVT states
- Protocol focus: isolate trophoblast-enriched placental digests, maintain villous-like organoids long term, and switch established lines into HLA-G-positive EVT differentiation

## Study design

- Starting material: trophoblast-enriched cell digests from first-trimester human placentas, with best performance reported for 6-9 week gestation samples
- Protocol stages:
  - digest placental tissue, embed the resulting cells in Matrigel droplets, and derive trophoblast organoids over about 2-3 weeks
  - maintain and passage cultures every 7-10 days in trophoblast organoid medium tuned around WNT, TGF-beta, and MAPK-associated trophoblast signaling logic
  - differentiate established organoids for about 1-3 weeks by switching medium composition to drive EMT-like conversion into invasive HLA-G-positive EVT cells
  - use optional single-cell isolation and clonal growth workflows to test regenerative capacity and support downstream flow cytometry or microscopy assays
- Key validation: the protocol generated placental organoids from 86% of 6-9 week samples, maintained lines for more than a year, reproduced VCT plus SCT architecture by microscopy, and produced HLA-G-positive EVT differentiation in all tested lines
- Distinct protocol emphasis: a rare primary-tissue organoid system that keeps genuine placental architecture and lineage plasticity instead of collapsing trophoblast work into short-lived primary cells or flat stem-cell cultures

## Key findings

- Establishes a long-term 3D trophoblast system from primary placenta tissue, which is a major step up from short-lived primary cultures and simpler 2D trophoblast stem-cell approaches.
- Shows that the same organoid line can support both villous-like maintenance and invasive EVT differentiation by medium switching, rather than requiring separate models for each trophoblast state.
- Suggests clonogenic regenerative capacity within the trophoblast population, making the system useful for lineage, perturbation, and maternal-fetal interaction studies.

## Distinctive contribution in this corpus

- One of the strongest non-GI adult-platform papers in the collection because it is primary, lineage-flexible, and directly tied to human placental biology.
- Gives the corpus a placental interface model where tissue architecture and differentiated cell-state transitions are central to the protocol, not incidental.
- Extends the reproductive-tract side of the collection beyond cervix and endometrium into the maternal-fetal interface.

## Limitations and caveats

- Access to 6-9 week placental tissue is a major practical bottleneck, and the authors note lower success from later first-trimester samples and no term-placenta validation.
- The model is epithelial and trophoblast focused, lacking vascular, decidual, and Hofbauer-cell compartments that shape real placental physiology.
- Organoid polarity is partly inverted relative to in vivo exposure, with SCT facing the lumen, which complicates infection and transport experiments unless the geometry is redesigned.

## Relevance to corpus

- Strengthens the adult-platform branch with a primary tissue system that is not tumor-centered and not endoderm-centered.
- Useful when the project needs donor-grounded trophoblast biology, EVT invasion, placental hormone competency, or maternal-interface assay planning.
- Provides a useful contrast to reproductive-tract epithelial organoids and to trophoblast protocols focused more on perturbation than on baseline platform establishment.
