# Large-Scale Exome Sequencing Study Implicates Both Developmental and Functional Changes in the Neurobiology of Autism.

## Abstract

We present the largest exome sequencing study of autism spectrum disorder (ASD) to date (n = 35,584 total samples, 11,986 with ASD). Using an enhanced analytical framework to integrate de novo and case-control rare variation, we identify 102 risk genes at a false discovery rate of 0.1 or less. Of these genes, 49 show higher frequencies of disruptive de novo variants in individuals ascertained to have severe neurodevelopmental delay, whereas 53 show higher frequencies in individuals ascertained to have ASD; comparing ASD cases with mutations in these groups reveals phenotypic differences. Expressed early in brain development, most risk genes have roles in regulation of gene expression or neuronal communication (i.e., mutations effect neurodevelopmental and neurophysiological changes), and 13 fall within loci recurrently hit by copy number variants. In cells from the human cortex, expression of risk genes is enriched in excitatory and inhibitory neuronal lineages, consistent with multiple paths to an excitatory-inhibitory imbalance underlying ASD.

## Key findings

- **102 ASD risk genes at FDR <= 0.1** identified by an enhanced TADA model; **78 at FDR <= 0.05** and **26 at Bonferroni/family-wise error rate (FWER) <= 0.05** (Figure 2B; Table S2). **60 of 102 were not found by the prior ASC/Iossifov analyses**, and **30 are "truly novel"** (not previously linked to autosomal-dominant NDD: ASD, DD, epilepsy, intellectual disability). Cohort: **35,584 samples passing QC, 11,986 with ASD** (21,219 family-based: 6,430 ASD probands, 2,179 unaffected siblings + parents; 14,365 case-control: 5,556 cases, 8,809 controls). **9,345 rare de novo coding variants** called from family data.
- **De novo variants carry the largest per-variant effect.** In the 1,447 most-constrained genes (pLI >= 0.995): de novo PTVs show **3.5-fold** case enrichment (0.057 vs 0.016 variants/sample; 366 in 6,430 cases vs 35 in 2,179 sibs; p = 4x10^-17) vs only **1.2-fold** for inherited PTVs (p = 0.07) and **1.8-fold** in case-control (p = 4x10^-24). The most severe de novo missense (MPC >= 2) give **2.1-fold** case enrichment (p = 3x10^-8). Lowest PTV tier (pLI < 0.5) and milder missense tiers show no enrichment.
- **Variance explained is small and de novo-dominated.** All autosomal exome de novo variants explain **~1.92% of ASD variance** (de novo PTVs 1.3%, of which 1.2% from highest-pLI tier; de novo MPC>=2 missense 0.5%; all other missense 0.12%). Inherited rare variants contributed too little liability to include in the discovery model — consistent with the bulk of ASD heritability sitting in common variation (cf. Gaugler 2014), not in these rare alleles.
- **Sex differences support a female protective effect.** Affected females (n=1,097) carry ~2-fold more de novo PTVs in constrained genes than affected males (n=5,333) (p = 3x10^-6), i.e., females need a higher genetic load to cross the diagnostic threshold. Crucially, **no sex difference in per-variant trait liability (Z-score) was seen for any variant class**, so liabilities were estimated sex-combined. Chromosome X de novo variants were not used for discovery (low power; mostly paternal-origin, only females inherit paternal X); sex-stratified X case-control found no Bonferroni-significant gene.
- **A developmental/phenotypic axis splits the genes: ASDP vs ASD-NDD.** Comparing relative frequency R of disruptive de novo variants (PTVs or MPC>=1 missense) in ASD-ascertained vs **5,264 severe-NDD trios**: genes with R>1 = **ASD-predominant (ASDP)**, R<1 = **ASD-with-NDD (ASD_NDD, 49 genes)**. Carriers of disruptive de novo variants in ASD_NDD genes walk **2.6 +/- 1.2 months later** (p = 2.3x10^-5) and have IQ **11.9 +/- 6.0 points lower** (p = 1.1x10^-4) than ASDP-gene carriers; both groups differ from the rest of the cohort. Inherited PTVs are far more frequent in parents for ASDP genes (1.17/gene) than ASD_NDD genes (0.45/gene; p = 6.6x10^-6), evidence of stronger selection on ASD_NDD genes. [NOTE: the paper is internally inconsistent on the ASDP count — see Open questions.]
- **De novo burden is not limited to low-IQ cases.** ASD cases with IQ >= 70 (69.4% of cohort) still carry excess de novo variants vs expectation, true even when splitting at the cohort-mean FSIQ >= 82 (Figure 4D) — de novo variants do not act solely through cognitive impairment.
- **Two separable functional classes.** Gene Ontology + literature assign the 102 genes to **gene-expression regulation (GER, n=58)** (chromatin/transcription factors; e.g., 16 genes in "regulation of transcription from RNA pol II promoter," 5.7-fold, FDR 6.2x10^-6), **neuronal communication (NC, n=24)** (synaptic; 9 genes in "synaptic transmission," 5.0-fold, FDR 3.8x10^-3), **cytoskeleton (n=9)**, and **other**. PPI and regulatory-network analyses show GER genes do **not** regulate NC genes more than chance (even CHD8 does not), arguing the two classes act through distinct mechanisms that must converge phenotypically.
- **Genes are expressed early and enriched in neurons.** Cortically-expressed ASD genes are **prenatally biased** (p = 8x10^-8); GER genes strongly prenatal (p = 9x10^-15, peak early-to-late fetal), NC genes postnatally biased (p = 0.03, peak late-midfetal to infancy). In single-cell data (Nowakowski 2017; 4,261 prenatal forebrain cells, 6-37 pcw), 68 of 102 genes are expressed by 13 pcw rising to 81 by 23 pcw; enrichment is strongest in **maturing/mature excitatory and inhibitory neurons** (early excitatory neurons C3 express 72 genes, OR 5.0, p < 1x10^-10; striatal interneurons C1, 51 genes). Only non-neuronal enrichment is in OPC/astrocyte (C4). GTEx: strongest tissue enrichment in cortex (30 genes, OR 3.7, p = 3x10^-6); NC genes most cortex-enriched (OR 25), GER genes least (OR 1.5).
- **CNV driver genes and GWAS overlap.** **13 of the 102 genes fall in 12 of 51 recurrent genomic-disorder CNV loci** (2.3-fold over chance, p = 2.3x10^-3); 11q13.2-q13.4 contains two (SHANK2, KMT5B); BCL11A proposed as a new driver at 2p15-p16.1; HDLBP nominated over HDAC4 at 2q37.3. MAGMA shows common-variant enrichment in the 102 genes for **schizophrenia and educational attainment** (genetically correlated with ASD) but **not for the ASD GWAS itself** (underpowered) nor height (negative control). **KMT2E is implicated by both common (ASD GWAS) and rare variation** (Fisher p = 0.029).
- **Specific gain-of-function / missense-driven genes.** Four genes are driven primarily by recurrent de novo missense (>=4 MPC>=1, <=1 PTV): **DEAF1** (all 5 in SAND DNA-binding domain), **KCNQ3** (all 4 at voltage-sensor arginines, 3 at R230, confirmed GoF), **SCN1A**, **SLC6A1** (8 variants, A288V recurrent). PTV-dominant genes include ADNP, SYNGAP1, DYRK1A, ARID1B (significant PTV excess). Network analysis (DAWN) adds 138 genes (FDR <= 0.005), 83 beyond TADA.

## Methods

- **Cohort / design.** Whole-exome sequencing (WES) of 35,584 QC-passing samples (11,986 ASD). Two arms: 21,219 family-based (trios/quads: 6,430 ASD probands, 2,179 unaffected siblings, plus parents) and 14,365 case-control (5,556 ASD cases, 8,809 controls). 6,197 newly sequenced by the ASC; 11,265 newly incorporated, largely from the Danish iPSYCH study (4,811 cases + 5,214 controls). Comparison NDD set: 5,264 severe-NDD trios (Deciphering Developmental Disorders / DDD-style).
- **Variant definitions.** Rare = allele count <= 5 in the dataset/reference DBs (de novo additionally AF <= 0.1%). Variants binned into 7 functional-severity tiers: 3 PTV tiers by pLI (>=0.995, 0.5-0.995, 0-0.5), 3 missense tiers by MPC score (>=2, 1-2, 0-1), and synonymous. pLI (Lek 2016 / Kosmicki 2017) and MPC (Samocha 2017) index evolutionary constraint.
- **Core statistical model: enhanced TADA (TADA+).** Bayesian Transmitted-And-De-novo-Association framework (He 2013), updated here to use **pLI as a continuous weight for PTVs** and **MPC as a two-tier weight (>=2, 1-2) for missense**. Inputs: de novo PTVs + de novo missense from family data; PTVs only from case-control; inherited variants excluded (negligible liability). Genes ranked by FDR; simulations show calibrated FDR robust to assumed total ASD-gene count. Burden contrasts used two-sided Poisson exact / binomial exact tests; sex- and population-combined p-values via Fisher's method (corrected for 168 tests).
- **Downstream analyses.** ASDP vs ASD_NDD partition via relative de novo frequency R against the 5,264 NDD trios (+ case-control fallback for 3 genes); heterogeneity by Fisher's exact test; phenotype links (age of walking, FSIQ) by t-tests/chi-square. CNV-locus overlap by permutation against a curated 823-gene / 51-locus consensus. Common-variant overlap via MAGMA across 6 GWAS (ASD, SCZ, MDD, ADHD, educational attainment, height). Expression: GTEx (53 tissues), BrainSpan (10 developmental stages, WGCNA co-expression), and Nowakowski 2017 single-cell prenatal cortex (t-SNE cell-type clusters, Fisher's exact enrichment). Network prediction via DAWN, PPI connectivity, and ChIP/CLIP regulatory networks.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Canonical gene-count anchor.** This is the standard citation for "**102 ASD risk genes (FDR <= 0.1)**" (78 at FDR<=0.05, 26 at FWER) — the manuscript should cite Figure 2B and Table S2 when it states how many high-confidence ASD genes exist and motivates a programmable/multiplexed organoid platform that can perturb dozens-to-hundreds of genes rather than one at a time.
- **Grounds the "convergence on developmental stage + cell type" argument.** Figure 5 (prenatal bias, p=8x10^-8; enrichment in maturing excitatory/inhibitory neurons; 68->81 genes expressed 13->23 pcw) is direct human-tissue evidence that ASD genes converge on **mid-to-late fetal corticogenesis and neuronal lineages** — the exact window/cell types brain organoids model. Use it to justify organoid stage selection and excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) readouts.
- **Variant-architecture framing (de novo vs inherited vs common).** Supplies the numbers for the review's architecture section: de novo PTV 3.5-fold enrichment, de novo explaining ~1.92% of variance, inherited rare variation too weak to include, and common variation (SCZ/EA overlap, KMT2E dual evidence) carrying separate signal. Supports the case that proactive organoid screens should prioritize **high-effect de novo loss-of-function** alleles first.
- **Variant prioritization / clinical interpretation.** The pLI- and MPC-weighted TADA tiers and the GoF missense exemplars (KCNQ3 R230, DEAF1 SAND-domain, SCN1A, SLC6A1) are concrete cases where **variant class and protein location dictate mechanism** — a strong argument for isogenic, variant-resolved organoid assays (LoF vs GoF vs missense) over gene-level knockouts.
- **The ASDP vs ASD_NDD axis = a design dimension.** The split (ASD-predominant vs shared-with-NDD, with measurable IQ/motor differences) gives the review a principled way to choose which genes/phenotypes an organoid platform should aim to disentangle (core-ASD social/behavioral vs general neurodevelopmental severity).
- **Two separable functional modules (GER vs NC) that do not regulate each other.** Justifies multi-modal organoid phenotyping: chromatin/transcription-factor (GER, prenatal) perturbations likely alter neuron number/migration, whereas synaptic (NC, postnatal) perturbations alter E/I signaling — the review can frame organoids as the system to test how these distinct trajectories converge.
- **Sex-difference hook.** The female-protective-effect data (2-fold de novo enrichment in affected females; no liability difference) motivate using XX vs XY isogenic organoid lines to probe sex-modulated buffering.

## Open questions / limitations

- **How GER and NC mechanisms converge phenotypically is unresolved.** The paper explicitly shows the two functional classes do **not** regulate one another (even CHD8 does not regulate NC genes) and states the nature of convergence "is likely to hold the key" but is unknown — a gap the organoid review positions itself to address, not something this paper answers.
- **Internal inconsistency in the ASDP gene count.** The abstract and Figure 5 legend say **53 ASD-predominant**, while the main text and Figure 4A say **50 ASDP** (47 with de novo R>1 plus 3 assigned by case-control: UBR1, MAP1A, NUP155). The ASD_NDD count (49) is consistent throughout, and 53 + 49 = 102. Treat the ASDP figure as 53 (paper's headline number) with the "50" as an apparent typo; do not over-anchor on it.
- **Chromosome X is largely unaddressed.** De novo X variants were excluded from discovery (underpowered); no X gene reached Bonferroni significance. X-linked recessive-like inheritance (males inheriting from unaffected mothers) is acknowledged but not powered for.
- **Inherited and common variation are under-resolved.** Rare inherited variants contributed too little measurable liability to enter the model; the ASD GWAS signal was not enriched in the 102 genes (attributed to limited GWAS power, not necessarily biological non-overlap). The study cannot say whether common and rare risk converge on the same genes.
- **The ASDP/ASD_NDD partition is "a matter of degree," not categorical.** Disruptive de novo variants in both classes affect IQ and motor milestones; carriers of an ASDP-gene variant are not guaranteed IQ >= 70, nor ASD_NDD carriers IQ < 70. Larger cohorts are needed to reliably label individual genes as ASD-specific vs shared.
- **Associations are statistical, not mechanistic, and rest on bulk/single-cell expression and post-mortem/atlas data — not functional models.** No causal/developmental mechanism is established for individual genes; effect sizes for most genes are imprecise, and the gene list will grow with sample size (DAWN already predicts ~83 additional genes). Driver-gene calls within CNV loci (e.g., BCL11A, HDLBP) are nominations, not validated.
