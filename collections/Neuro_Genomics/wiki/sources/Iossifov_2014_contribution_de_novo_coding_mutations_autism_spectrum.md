# The contribution of de novo coding mutations to autism spectrum disorder.

## Source

- Authors: Iossifov I, O'Roak BJ, Sanders SJ, Ronemus M, Krumm N, Levy D, Stessman HA, Witherspoon KT et al.
- Journal: Nature (2014)
- DOI: [10.1038/nature13908](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature13908)
- PMID: [25363768](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25363768/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

Whole exome sequencing has proven to be a powerful tool for understanding the genetic architecture of human disease. Here we apply it to more than 2,500 simplex families, each having a child with an autistic spectrum disorder. By comparing affected to unaffected siblings, we show that 13% of de novo missense mutations and 43% of de novo likely gene-disrupting (LGD) mutations contribute to 12% and 9% of diagnoses, respectively. Including copy number variants, coding de novo mutations contribute to about 30% of all simplex and 45% of female diagnoses. Almost all LGD mutations occur opposite wild-type alleles. LGD targets in affected females significantly overlap the targets in males of lower intelligence quotient (IQ), but neither overlaps significantly with targets in males of higher IQ. We estimate that LGD mutation in about 400 genes can contribute to the joint class of affected females and males of lower IQ, with an overlapping and similar number of genes vulnerable to contributory missense mutation. LGD targets in the joint class overlap with published targets for intellectual disability and schizophrenia, and are enriched for chromatin modifiers, FMRP-associated genes and embryonically expressed genes. Most of the significance for the latter comes from affected females.

## Key findings

- **Cohort: 2,517 Simons Simplex Collection (SSC) families** (2,508 affected children, 1,911 unaffected siblings, plus parents) — whole-exome sequencing. Within-SSC sex bias 7 males : 1 female (affected females = 13.5% of SSC). Precision de novo rates measured over a 32-Gb "joint 40× region" (48% of targeted exome) in 1,867 quads.
- **De novo LGD rate: 0.21 per affected child vs 0.12 per unaffected sibling** (ascertainment differential 0.09, P = 2×10⁻⁵) → **~43% of proband LGDs are contributory**. De novo missense: 0.94 (affected) vs 0.82 (sibling), differential 0.12 (P = 0.01) → **~13% of missense contributory**. Synonymous rates equal (0.34 vs 0.33, n.s.), confirming no global rate inflation.
- **Diagnostic contribution:** de novo missense → ~12% of diagnoses; de novo LGD → ~9%. **Including CNVs, coding de novo mutation contributes to ~30% of all simplex ASD and ~45% of female diagnoses** (summed ascertainment differential 0.27 overall; 0.33 excluding higher-IQ males; 0.45 in females).
- **Target genes:** 391 validated de novo LGD mutations in **353 target genes**, of which **27 genes recurrent** (vs 7.6 expected, P < 0.0001). Among ~1,500 missense targets, **145 recurrent** (vs 115 expected, P = 0.001). **Most-hit gene: CHD8 (7 LGDs)**; DYRK1A hit 4×; CHD2 3×.
- **Estimated number of vulnerable genes (theme 1 headline):** in the "joint class" (affected females + lower-IQ males), **~387 genes vulnerable to contributory LGD mutation (95% CI 149–915)** and **~404 genes vulnerable to contributory missense** (CI 71–3,050) — overlapping, similar-sized sets. Class-vulnerability mode ~0.4 for LGD in low-IQ males (~40% of LGDs in vulnerable genes contribute), ~0.1 for missense.
- **Recessive model rejected:** almost all LGD mutations occur opposite a wild-type allele — 0/391 had a rare transmitted LGD opposite, only 4/391 a rare missense opposite → de novo LGDs act via **haploinsufficiency, not compound heterozygosity.**
- **IQ effect:** males with de novo LGD lose ~5 IQ points (P = 0.01); males with *recurrent* LGD lose ~20 points (P = 1×10⁻⁵); LGDs in FMRP targets → ~14-point drop (P = 0.001). Higher-IQ (>90) males show *no* significant enrichment in any functional gene class and few recurrent targets — a genetically distinguishable subgroup.
- **Sex / "joint class" overlap (theme 5):** the 67 LGD targets in affected females overlap significantly with the 166 LGD targets in lower-IQ males (10 observed vs 1.3 expected, P = 7×10⁻⁷); neither overlaps higher-IQ-male targets. Embryonically expressed-gene enrichment is driven almost entirely by females (LGD: 23 obs vs 8.5 exp, P = 5×10⁻⁶) — interpreted as damage acting before a "female protective effect."
- **Functional enrichment of proband LGD targets:** FMRP targets 55 obs vs 34.1 exp (P = 4×10⁻⁴); chromatin modifiers 26 obs vs 11.8 exp (P = 3×10⁻⁴); embryonically expressed 65 obs vs 45.0 exp (P = 2×10⁻³). The 27 recurrent-LGD genes are even more enriched: FMRP 14/2.6 (P = 4×10⁻⁸), chromatin modifiers 6/0.9 (P = 2×10⁻⁴). No enrichment in unaffected-sibling targets.
- **Cross-disorder overlap:** joint-class ASD LGD targets significantly overlap published intellectual-disability and schizophrenia de novo LGD target lists; higher-IQ-male targets do not — supporting shared mechanisms for the more severe ASD subgroup.
- **Parental origin / age:** de novo mutations arise ~3× more often on the paternal background; rates rise with parental age (partial explanation for older-parent ASD risk).

## Methods

- **Samples:** 2,517 SSC families (of ~2,800; 774 previously published) assembled at 13 clinical centers with standardized phenotyping; probands stratified by **non-verbal IQ** ("IQ"). Simplex design preferentially recruited single-proband families (two-proband families excluded); plus Simons Ancillary (n=123) and Twin (n=13) collections.
- **Sequencing:** exome capture/sequencing at three centers (CSHL, Yale, University of Washington); all data reanalyzed through a uniform CSHL pipeline, blind to affected status; de novo calls experimentally validated (MiSeq amplicon / Sanger / MIP capture).
- **Rate estimation:** measured over the "joint 40× region" (positions with ≥40× coverage in all quad members) for precision, then extrapolated exome-wide; **ascertainment differential** (affected − unaffected rate) used as the estimator of contribution.
- **Target/enrichment analysis:** improved null **"length model"** — probability of a de novo hit proportional to gene length — used for enrichment tests (FMRP targets, chromatin modifiers, embryonically expressed, postsynaptic density, essential, Mendelian genes) and to estimate vulnerable-gene counts from observed recurrence (two-sided binomial tests). Concept of **"gene vulnerability"** (P[a given mutation type in a given gene contributes]) and **"class vulnerability"** formalized: F·A = P·H·V.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- **Foundational "de novo architecture" citation (theme 1):** the canonical SSC numbers — ~43% of LGDs / ~13% of missense contributory, ~30% of simplex (45% female) diagnoses from coding de novo + CNV, and the **~400 vulnerable-gene estimate** — are the standard quantitative basis for the manuscript's genetic-architecture framing and gene-count claims.
- **Haploinsufficiency mechanism → organoid loss-of-function modeling (theme 7):** the finding that de novo LGDs act on a wild-type background (single-allele damage) directly justifies the review's use of heterozygous-knockout / dosage-sensitive organoid models and CRISPR LoF perturbations; the authors note the intact second allele as a therapeutic opening.
- **Convergence on developmental stage and pathways (theme 2):** enrichment of FMRP targets, chromatin modifiers, and *embryonically expressed* genes pinpoints early developmental, transcriptional/chromatin convergence — the mechanistic rationale for modeling early developmental windows in organoids. (Complements Willsey 2013 midfetal-cortex coexpression and Parikshak 2013 in this collection.)
- **Sex differences / female protective effect (theme 5):** higher de novo burden in affected females, the female-driven embryonic-gene enrichment, and the female↔low-IQ-male target overlap give the genetic basis for the review's sex-difference section (pairs with Shaw 2025 epidemiologic 3.4:1 ratio and Zhou 2022 penetrance-dependent male bias).
- **Severity stratification & cross-disorder pleiotropy:** the IQ-dependent recurrent-LGD effect (~20-point drop) and ASD↔ID↔schizophrenia target overlap support genotype-/severity-stratified organoid panels and the argument that mechanism-based interventions may generalize across NDDs.
- **Variant-effect interpretation motivation (themes 3/4/6):** with hundreds of vulnerable genes but only ~40% / ~90% / ~35% contribution probability for individual LGD / recurrent-LGD / missense events respectively, per-variant functional resolution (saturation mutagenesis, PS3/BS3 evidence) is exactly the gap the review's variant-prioritization and variant-effect-mapping sections address.

## Open questions / limitations

- Penetrance of individual mutations cannot be determined ("we do not see them often enough in an unselected population") — hence the population-level "gene/class vulnerability" framework rather than per-variant risk estimates.
- Vulnerable-gene counts carry very wide confidence intervals (LGD 149–915; missense 71–3,050); the ~400 figure is a point estimate for the joint class only.
- Estimate of de novo contribution is **conservative** — does not yet capture intermediate-size CNVs, copy-neutral rearrangements, regulatory mutations, or non-coding-gene mutations.
- Simplex (SSC) design; the authors estimate ~40% of "simplex" families are actually high-risk (where de novo mutation may play little role), complicating extrapolation to all ASD.
- Mechanism of haploinsufficiency vulnerability unresolved (dosage sensitivity, expression-variability buffering, monoallelic expression, possible dominant-negative truncations all raised as hypotheses).
- Coding-exome focus; predates the large inherited-LoF (Zhou 2022) and common-variant (Grove 2019) analyses needed for the full liability picture.
