# Recurrent reciprocal 1q21.1 deletions and duplications associated with microcephaly or macrocephaly and developmental and behavioral abnormalities.

## Source

- Authors: Brunetti-Pierri N, Berg JS, Scaglia F, Belmont J, Bacino CA, Sahoo T, Lalani SR, Graham B et al.
- Journal: Nature genetics (2008)
- DOI: [10.1038/ng.279](https://doi.org/10.1038/ng.279)
- PMID: [19029900](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19029900/)
- Added via: manuscript_brain_organoid_v6 reference ingest

## Abstract

Chromosome region 1q21.1 contains extensive and complex low-copy repeats, and copy number variants (CNVs) in this region have recently been reported in association with congenital heart defects, developmental delay, schizophrenia and related psychoses. We describe 21 probands with the 1q21.1 microdeletion and 15 probands with the 1q21.1 microduplication. These CNVs were inherited in most of the cases in which parental studies were available. Consistent and statistically significant features of microcephaly and macrocephaly were found in individuals with microdeletion and microduplication, respectively. Notably, a paralog of the HYDIN gene located on 16q22.2 and implicated in autosomal recessive hydrocephalus was inserted into the 1q21.1 region during the evolution of Homo sapiens; we found this locus to be deleted or duplicated in the individuals we studied, making it a probable candidate for the head size abnormalities observed. We propose that recurrent reciprocal microdeletions and microduplications within 1q21.1 represent previously unknown genomic disorders characterized by abnormal head size along with a spectrum of developmental delay, neuropsychiatric abnormalities, dysmorphic features and congenital anomalies. These phenotypes are subject to incomplete penetrance and variable expressivity.

## Key findings

- **Cohort scale.** Ascertained from 16,557 affected individuals referred for clinical array-CGH at one lab over 4 years (Texas Children's Hospital / Baylor). Identified **27 probands with distal 1q21.1 microdeletions** (clinical data for 21) and **17 probands with microduplications** (clinical data for 15). Largest such 1q21.1 series in the literature at the time.
- **Reciprocal dosage effect on head size (central result).**
  - Microdeletion carriers (probands + parents + sibs): mean head-circumference (FOC) Z = **−2.53** (95% CI −2.96 to −2.11); 21/29 had FOC Z < −2 (microcephaly).
  - Microduplication carriers: mean FOC Z = **+0.95** (95% CI 0.06 to 1.83); 10/24 had FOC Z > +2 (macrocephaly), more variable.
  - Probands-only: deletion mean Z = **−2.55** (95% CI −3.12 to −1.98); duplication mean Z = **+1.15** (95% CI 0.03 to 2.28).
  - Deletion vs duplication groups differ at **P < 0.0001** (unpaired t-test); each differs from population mean (deletion P < 0.0001; duplication P < 0.05).
  - 50 randomly selected array-CGH controls: mean Z = −0.62 (95% CI −1.33 to 0.08), NOT different from population mean (P = 0.083) — argues against ascertainment bias for head size.
- **Inheritance / penetrance.** CNVs inherited from a parent in most cases with parental data (both parents tested in 17/21 deletions, 12/15 duplications). Only **3 de novo deletions and 1 de novo duplication**. Some transmitting parents carried the CNV but were clinically unrecognized → **incomplete penetrance + variable expressivity**.
- **Phenotype spectrum (non-head-size).** Developmental delay / learning disability in most probands; behavioral abnormalities frequent — ADHD, autism / autism-spectrum behaviors, anxiety/depression, antisocial behavior, aggression, hallucinations. Recurrent but nonspecific dysmorphism: microdeletion = frontal bossing, deep-set eyes, bulbous nose; microduplication = frontal bossing, hypertelorism. Wide range of congenital anomalies without a single unifying pattern.
- **Breakpoint architecture.** Region flanked by large directly-oriented low-copy repeats (LCRs) that mediate non-allelic homologous recombination. Deletions fall into **Class I** (distal 1q21.1 only, ~1 Mb) and **Class II** (larger, including the TAR-syndrome region). 16/17 duplications were Class I. Distal 1q21.1 is **distinct from the proximal TAR-syndrome deletion region**. One complex case (subject 36) carried a duplication of distal 1q21.1 with a reciprocal TAR-region deletion, inherited from mother and shared by two affected sibs.
- **Candidate gene — HYDIN paralog.** A ~360-kb interchromosomal duplication of the 16q22.2 *HYDIN* locus was inserted into 1q21.1 during *Homo sapiens* evolution (not in the then-current genome build). The 1q21.1 *HYDIN2* paralog was **deleted or duplicated in all carriers** (FISH, BAC RP11-424M24) and is brain-expressed. Mechanistic plausibility: *HYDIN* mutation causes hydrocephalus in mice via ciliary dysfunction; the HYDIN ASH domain is shared with **ASPM**, a major determinant of cerebral cortical size; 16q22.2 deletions involving *HYDIN* cause microcephaly. Proposed as a **dosage-sensitive determinant of head size**. One atypical deletion sparing the HYDIN paralog had a normal head circumference (supporting evidence, single case).
- **Population frequency / pathogenicity.** Distal 1q21.1 CNVs seen at only ~0.02% in two large schizophrenia control sets; Mefford et al. found 0 deletions and 1 duplication in 4,737 controls; 0 CNVs in 550 in-house normal parents → enriched in clinically referred individuals and unlikely to be benign. The earlier reported 1q21.1–congenital-heart-defect link is attributed largely to ascertainment bias (only 1 bicuspid aortic valve in this cohort).

## Methods

- **Ascertainment:** retrospective review of 16,557 consecutive clinical samples on **targeted array comparative genomic hybridization (array-CGH)** over 4 years; referral reasons included intellectual disability, autism, congenital anomalies.
- **CNV confirmation / breakpoint mapping:** array-CGH against distal-1q21.1 BAC clones (RP11-337C18, RP11-533N14, RP11-102F23) plus **metaphase and interphase FISH** on PHA-stimulated peripheral-blood lymphoblasts. Additional FISH clones (RP11-769J20 for TAR extent; RP11-115G11 and RP11-15H9 for telomeric extent; RP11-424M24 for the HYDIN paralog) refined breakpoint classes.
- **LCR analysis:** segmental duplications from UCSC genome browser (Human Genome Build 36.1); analysis limited by 15 reference-sequence gaps within 1q21.1.
- **Phenotyping:** medical/family history, growth measurements, dysmorphology exam; parents of carriers evaluated where available (9/21 deletions, 6/15 duplications). Photographs with consent.
- **Head-circumference statistics:** measurements converted to age- and sex-matched Z scores (ABase calculator). Each group (deletion / duplication / 50 controls) tested vs population mean (zero) by one-sample t-test and against each other by unpaired two-tailed t-test with Welch's correction; GraphPad Prism 5.
- **Note:** descriptive/observational clinical-genetics study; no functional assay of individual genes — *HYDIN* candidacy rests on dosage correlation, expression and prior literature, not direct perturbation.

## Relevance to the brain-organoid ASD review

- Anchors the **CNV / dosage-architecture** strand of the review: 1q21.1 is a canonical recurrent reciprocal CNV where **gene dosage maps quantitatively onto a brain phenotype (head size)** — microdeletion→microcephaly, microduplication→macrocephaly. Macrocephaly/brain overgrowth is a recurring ASD-relevant endophenotype, and 1q21.1 CNVs carry autism, ADHD and schizophrenia risk with incomplete penetrance.
- Provides a concrete clinical motivation for **proactive functional dissection in organoids**: this 2008 study could only *correlate* the HYDIN paralog (and unnamed neighboring genes) with head size; it could not test causality or resolve which gene(s) drive overgrowth vs neuropsychiatric features. Patient-derived or engineered brain organoids are the modern route to convert such dosage correlations into mechanism (cortical-size phenotypes, proliferation of neural progenitors).
- Establishes the **incomplete-penetrance / variable-expressivity** problem that organoid models and the saturation/variant-effect mapping methods in this collection aim to address: a CNV (or variant) can be "the same lesion" yet yield divergent outcomes, demanding controlled cellular readouts.
- Bridges to the other two ingested papers (Sahu BRCA2 SGE, Beltran 500 domains): together they trace the arc from **structural/dosage variation interpreted by clinical cytogenetics** (this paper) to **base-resolution variant-effect maps** (their methodology), the methodological lineage the review extends into organoids.

## Open questions / limitations

- **Causal gene unresolved.** The HYDIN paralog is a *candidate* by dosage, brain expression and homology only — never functionally perturbed here; the paralog is absent from the contemporaneous genome build, and 15 reference gaps in 1q21.1 limit breakpoint/structure resolution. Other genes in the interval (e.g. *NOTCH2NL*, *GJA5/8*, *PRKAB2*, *PDE4DIP* listed in Fig. 1) could drive head size or neuropsychiatric phenotypes; later work strongly implicated **NOTCH2NL** in cortical size, not examined here.
- **Penetrance/expressivity unquantified.** No penetrance estimates or genotype–phenotype rules beyond head size; many carriers were young children whose behavioral phenotypes may not have manifested. Counseling and especially prenatal interpretation remain difficult.
- **Ascertainment.** Cohort drawn from a clinically referred, phenotype-enriched population; despite the matched-control comparison, milder/asymptomatic carriers are under-sampled.
- **No cellular/developmental model.** Pre-organoid era — the brain-size mechanism is inferred, not demonstrated; exactly the gap the review proposes to close.
