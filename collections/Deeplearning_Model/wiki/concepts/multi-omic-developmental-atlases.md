# Multi-Omic Developmental Atlases

## Definition

- Multi-omic developmental atlases are integrated reference resources that combine multiple molecular layers, often plus spatial context, to reconstruct developing cell states, trajectories, and regulatory programs.
- In this collection, the concept matters because such atlases can act as benchmarking substrates and inference scaffolds for later predictive or generative models.

## In This Collection

- [A multi-omic atlas of human embryonic skeletal development](../sources/to_2024_a_multi-omic_atlas_of_human.md) is the clearest example, combining paired transcriptional and epigenetic profiling with spatial localization to map osteogenic and chondrogenic development.
- [HNOCA](../entities/HNOCA.md) is not fully multi-omic, but it shows a related atlas-as-infrastructure pattern for organoid benchmarking and projection.
- [AURORA](../entities/AURORA.md) sits at the broader human multi-omics end of the design space, showing how shared latent or integrative systems can span modalities for downstream reasoning.

## Claimed Benefits

- Supports lineage reconstruction with richer regulatory context than transcriptomics alone.
- Enables disease and trait linkage at cell-state resolution.
- Provides a reusable substrate for projection, perturbation interpretation, and developmental benchmarking.

## Caveats

- Integration quality strongly depends on assay compatibility, stage coverage, and reference choice.
- Atlas richness does not automatically imply causal validity of inferred trajectories or perturbation effects.
- In this collection, these resources are closer to reference infrastructures than directly reusable foundation models.
