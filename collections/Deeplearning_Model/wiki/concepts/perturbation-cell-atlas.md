# Perturbation Cell Atlas

## Definition

- Perturbation Cell Atlas is a proposed atlas of high-content perturbation states that would complement observational atlases by systematically learning how genetic, chemical, or natural-variation perturbations move cells through phenotypic space.
- In this collection, the concept is explicitly tied to causal and generative foundation models rather than only to data collection.

## In This Collection

- [Toward a foundation model of causal cell and tissue biology with a Perturbation Cell and Tissue Atlas](../sources/rood_2024_toward_a_foundation_model_of.md) defines the concept most directly and frames it as a complement to the Human Cell Atlas.
- [GEARS](../entities/GEARS.md), [CellOT](../entities/CellOT.md), [Squidiff](../entities/Squidiff.md), and [Tahoe-x1](../entities/Tahoe-x1.md) can be read as narrower perturbation-prediction components that might eventually plug into a broader perturbation atlas.
- [AIVC](../entities/AIVC.md) is adjacent in spirit because it also argues for community-scale, multi-system model building, although it is broader than perturbation data alone.

## Proposed Ingredients

- High-content pooled perturbation screens, especially Perturb-seq, optical pooled screens, and compressed perturbation designs.
- Coverage across multiple biological settings, including in vitro cell systems, organoids, animal models, and human tissues or natural genetic variation.
- Iterative experiment-model loops in which active learning or related design strategies choose the most informative next perturbations.
- Queryable models that can predict unmeasured perturbation outcomes, interpret mechanisms, and compare effects across scales and contexts.

## Claimed Benefits

- Reduces the need for experimentally exhaustive combinatorial perturbation measurement.
- Links molecular, imaging, spatial, and tissue-context readouts into one causal modeling program.
- Creates a bridge from perturbation prediction toward therapeutic design, mechanistic interpretation, and human genetics translation.

## Caveats

- The concept requires large-scale coordination, data standards, and multi-system experimental infrastructure that do not yet fully exist in this collection.
- Predictive or representational success does not automatically guarantee genetically causal validity.
- Translation across cell culture, organoids, animal models, and native human tissue remains an open generalization problem.
