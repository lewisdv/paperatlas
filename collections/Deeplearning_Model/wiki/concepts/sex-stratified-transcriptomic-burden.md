# Sex-Stratified Transcriptomic Burden

## Definition

- Sex-stratified transcriptomic burden is the idea that disease-associated transcriptomic dysregulation can differ between females and males in both magnitude and network organization, not only in a small set of sex-biased genes.
- In this collection, the concept matters as a heterogeneity-aware reference signal that future disease models may need to preserve or explain.

## In This Collection

- [Transcriptomic sex differences in postmortem brain samples from patients with psychiatric disorders](../sources/xia_2024_transcriptomic_sex_differences_in_postmortem.md) is the direct example, showing stronger female burden across psychiatric case-control contrasts and coexpression-network disruption.
- The collection does not yet contain many other explicit sex-stratified disease references, so this paper currently functions as an anchor for the theme rather than as part of a dense cluster.

## Claimed Benefits

- Reveals disease heterogeneity that pooled case-control analyses may hide.
- Provides a potential benchmark axis for future psychiatric or multimodal models.
- Helps connect disease-associated expression changes to broader immune, synaptic, and network-level dysfunction.

## Caveats

- Apparent burden differences can depend on sample balance, brain region, and tissue composition.
- Bulk transcriptomic analyses may conflate cell-type-composition shifts with within-cell-type dysregulation.
- Stronger association or burden does not by itself establish causal mechanisms or intervention targets.
